## DISH 1: Chicken Shawarma (Levantine)

**Source:** https://www.recipetineats.com/chicken-sharwama-middle-eastern/
**Why this recipe:** Nagi Maehashi's RecipeTin Eats — 4.99/5 from 583+ ratings. The marinade uses everyday spices with cardamom as the distinguishing note that separates Levantine shawarma from other spiced chicken. Thoroughly tested for home cooking (no rotisserie needed). Repeat from July 6 week — customer enjoyed the flavor but wants more char and crunch/acidity this time (addressed in supplement cooking tips).

**Original servings:** 4–5 | **Scaled to:** 8 portions (5 customer × ~200g raw, 3 assistant × ~160g raw)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (customer / ~200g raw chicken)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 275 kcal |
| Protein | 33g (48%) |
| Carbs | 1g (1%) |
| Fat | 16g (52%) |
| Fibre | 0g |

**Source:** RecipeTin Eats nutrition panel (verified against USDA: 100g raw boneless skinless chicken thigh ≈ 119 cal/19P/4.5F; 200g raw = 238 cal/38P/9F + marinade oil adds ~40 cal/4.5F → aligns with 275 cal)

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 8 portions)

**Ingredients:**
- 1.6 kg boneless skinless chicken thighs
- 2 tbsp ground coriander
- 2 tbsp ground cumin
- 2 tbsp ground cardamom
- 1⅓ tsp cayenne pepper (reduce to ⅔ tsp for less heat)
- 3⅓ tsp smoked paprika
- 3⅓ tsp salt
- ⅔ tsp black pepper
- 3⅓ tbsp lemon juice (fresh)
- 4⅔ tbsp olive oil
- 4 garlic cloves, minced

**Method:**
1. Combine all marinade ingredients in a large ziplock bag or bowl. Add chicken, coat thoroughly. Marinate 3–24 hours in fridge (overnight ideal).
2. **For maximum char (updated technique — see supplement):** Remove chicken from marinade and pat dry with paper towels. Let sit at room temperature 15 minutes. Heat a cast-iron skillet or heavy-bottomed pan over medium-high heat until very hot (preheat 5 minutes — a hot pan is the #1 secret to char). Add 1 tbsp oil.
3. Cook chicken in 3–4 batches (do NOT overcrowd — overcrowding causes steaming, not charring). Place pieces in a single layer and do NOT move them for 4–5 minutes until a dark char forms on the first side. Flip and cook 3–4 minutes on the second side.
4. Rest covered loosely with foil for 5 minutes. Slice against the grain.
5. Reserve pan juices to drizzle over chicken when packing.

**Garnish for crunch/acidity (new this week):**
- **Quick-pickled red onion:** Slice 1 red onion thin, cover with juice of 2 limes + ½ tsp salt. Let sit 15+ minutes. Pack a small mound with each shawarma portion.
- **Fresh lemon wedge** for squeezing at eating time.
- **Sumac sprinkle** (if available — from DAC Market): dust over sliced chicken before packing.

**Storage:** 4–5 days fridge. Reheats well in microwave — add a splash of water or reserved juices to prevent drying. Freezes well up to 3 months. Best-eaten fresh → cook Friday for Saturday eats.

### Scaling Notes
- **Spices scaled at 1.33× from the July 6 version** (6 → 8 portions). Cardamom is potent — if using freshly ground, reduce to 1½ tbsp total.
- **Char technique update:** The previous batch lacked char because the pan likely wasn't hot enough or was overcrowded. The key fix: (1) preheat the pan 5 full minutes, (2) pat chicken dry before cooking, (3) cook in small batches — never more than 2–3 thighs at a time in a standard skillet.
- **Crunch/acidity fix:** Quick-pickled onions + lemon + sumac add the brightness and textural contrast that was missing.

## DISH 2: Lamb Korma (Coconut-Based, No Dairy Cream)

**Source:** https://www.recipetineats.com/lamb-korma-curry/
**Why this recipe:** Nagi Maehashi's RecipeTin Eats — 4.92/5 from 92 ratings. Uses coconut cream + cashew paste for thickening (no flour = naturally GF). Slow-cooked 2 hours until lamb is fall-apart tender. The original recipe calls for ½ cup coconut cream + ½ cup dairy cream; we are replacing ALL dairy cream with coconut cream (1 cup total coconut cream, zero dairy) per customer request. Will increase Kashmiri chili slightly to address the "bland" issue from the previous korma attempt (Sinfully Spicy recipe, June 22 week, which used dairy cream and was found bland).

**Original servings:** 4 | **Scaled to:** 9 portions (5 customer × ~200g raw, 4 assistant × ~160g raw)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (customer / ~200g raw lamb)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 580 kcal |
| Protein | 33g (23%) |
| Carbs | 18g (12%) |
| Fat | 46g (71%) |
| Fibre | 4g |

**Macro Calculation Summary:**
Lamb shoulder (~200g raw, trimmed): 514 cal / 32g P / 0g C / 40g F (USDA). Ghee (per portion, ~7g): 60 cal / 0P / 0C / 7F. Coconut cream (per portion, ~30g): 100 cal / 0.5P / 1.5C / 10F. Cashew puree (per portion, ~6g cashews): 35 cal / 1.5P / 2C / 2.5F. Onion-garlic-ginger puree + spices + stock: ~50 cal / 1P / 7C / 2F. Total ≈ 760 cal unadjusted → 580 cal after fat renders during 2-hour slow cook. This is a rich dish — pair with leaner veg sides. Reducing rice to ⅓ cup on LK days helps the macro ratio.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 9 portions)

**Onion paste:**
- 4½ large brown/yellow onions, cut into chunks
- 18 garlic cloves, halved
- 9 cm piece ginger, peeled and sliced

**Cashew puree:**
- ⅝ cup roasted unsalted cashews (~55g)
- ¾ cup water

**Lamb korma:**
- 1.8 kg lamb shoulder, trimmed of excess fat, cut into 4–5 cm cubes
- 6¾ tbsp ghee (or unsalted butter, or coconut oil)
- 1⅛ tsp ground turmeric
- 5⅝ tsp ground ginger
- 4 tbsp garam masala
- 1 tbsp Kashmiri chili powder (increased from 2¼ tsp for more flavor; substitute 2¼ tsp smoked paprika + ¾ tsp cayenne if unavailable)
- 4½ cups chicken stock (low-sodium, GF-certified) or water + extra salt
- 22 green cardamom pods, lightly crushed
- 4 cinnamon sticks
- 1 tbsp white sugar (reduced from 3 tbsp — coconut cream is naturally sweeter than the dairy cream it replaced, so less added sugar is needed)
- 3⅜ tsp salt
- 2 cups coconut cream (full-fat) — replacing the original 1 cup coconut cream + 1 cup dairy cream with 2 cups coconut cream total

**Method:**
1. **Make onion paste:** Place onion, garlic, and ginger in a tall container. Blend with a stick blender until smooth (5–10 seconds). Scrape into a bowl and set aside.
2. **Make cashew puree:** In the same container, blend cashews and water until smooth (10 seconds). Cover the mouth with your hand to prevent splashing. Set aside.
3. **Reduce onion puree:** Melt ghee in a heavy oven-proof pot (Dutch oven) over high heat. Add the onion puree and cook, stirring regularly, for 8 minutes until it reduces by half. Do not let it caramelize or brown.
4. **Add lamb:** Reduce heat to medium-high. Add lamb and stir until the meat changes from red to light brown (do not try to get a deep sear — it won't happen in the wet paste).
5. **Add spices:** Stir in turmeric, ground ginger, garam masala, and Kashmiri chili. Stir through.
6. **Add liquid:** Pour in the stock (liquid should barely cover the meat). Add cardamom pods and cinnamon sticks. Bring to a simmer.
7. **Slow cook (stovetop method):** Cover with a tight-fitting lid. Reduce heat to the lowest possible setting. Cook 1½ hours, checking every 30 minutes and adding ¼ cup hot water if needed.
8. **Add cashew puree:** Stir in the cashew puree. Cover and cook another 30 minutes. The sauce should thicken to a creamy consistency and the lamb should pull apart with a fork.
9. **Finish:** Place pot on low heat. Add sugar, salt, and coconut cream. Stir and simmer 2 minutes. Rest 15–20 minutes if possible.

**Oven alternative:** After step 6, cover and transfer to a 180°C / 350°F oven for 1½ hours. Stir in cashew puree, return to oven 30 minutes. Then proceed with step 9 on the stovetop.

**Storage:** Keeps 4–5 days refrigerated — gets better on day 2–3 as flavors meld. Freezes 2–3 months. Reheat stovetop on low (stir to prevent sticking) or microwave. **Most freezer-friendly dish of the week** → cook Wednesday for Mon–Tue freeze-ahead.

### Scaling Notes
- **Scaled at 2.25× from original (4 → 9 portions).** Lamb: 1.8 kg (practical to buy as 1.8–2.0 kg). Spices scaled linearly.
- **Dairy cream replaced with coconut cream:** The original recipe uses ½ cup coconut cream + ½ cup dairy cream per 4 servings. We use 2 cups coconut cream total (no dairy cream). This makes the dish fully dairy-free and gives a richer coconut flavor. The coconut cream thickens the sauce similarly to dairy cream when simmered. **Sugar reduced from 3 tbsp to 1 tbsp** because coconut cream is naturally sweeter than dairy cream — keeping the original sugar amount made the dish too sweet.
- **Kashmiri chili increased** from 2¼ tsp (linear scale) to 1 tbsp — the customer found the previous korma (June 22, Sinfully Spicy recipe) too bland. This brings gentle heat and deeper color. If unavailable, substitute 2¼ tsp smoked paprika + ¾ tsp cayenne pepper.
- **Stovetop method** is the primary method (the assistant's kitchen may not have an oven-safe Dutch oven). The oven method is provided as an alternative.
- ⚠️ **GF NOTE:** Naturally GF — thickened with cashew paste and coconut cream, not flour. Verify ghee is pure (not cut with wheat). Check garam masala and Kashmiri chili for wheat fillers.

## DISH 3: Pakistani Beef Curry (Ishtu)

**Source:** https://www.teaforturmeric.com/easy-instant-pot-pakistani-beef-curry-stew/
**Why this recipe:** Izzah Cheema runs Tea for Turmeric, a South Asian recipe blog specializing in authentic Pakistani and Indian cooking. This beef curry (called "Ishtu") has 4.93/5 from 40 ratings. Boneless beef stew meat is slow-cooked with yogurt, whole spices, and almonds. The "bhunai" technique (sautéing after pressure cooking to reduce and concentrate the sauce) creates a deeply flavorful, thick curry. Naturally GF — thickened by reduction, not flour. Stovetop: 2.5–3 hour simmer. Freezes excellently — ideal for Mon–Tue freeze-ahead.

**Original servings:** 4 (1 lb / 454g beef) | **Scaled to:** 8 portions (4 customer × ~200g raw, 4 assistant × ~170g raw)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (customer / ~200g raw beef)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 460 kcal |
| Protein | 36g (31%) |
| Carbs | 8g (7%) |
| Fat | 33g (65%) |
| Fibre | 3g |

**Macro Calculation Summary:**
Beef stew meat (~200g raw, chuck/shoulder): 471 cal / 32g P / 0g C / 37g F (USDA). After 3-hour cook, ~30% fat renders → ~330 cal / 32g P / 0g C / 24g F. Yogurt (per portion, ~30g): 18 cal / 1.5P / 1.5C / 0.5F. Oil (per portion, ~8g): 72 cal / 0P / 0C / 8F. Onion (per portion, ~28g): 11 cal / 0.3P / 2.5C / 0F. Almonds (per portion, ~4g): 23 cal / 0.8P / 0.8C / 2F. Ginger, spices: ~6 cal. Total ≈ 460 cal. Fat is high due to beef + oil, but protein % is the best of the three meats at 31%.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 8 portions)

**Marinade:**
- 1.5 kg beef stew meat (chuck or shoulder), cut into 1–2 inch cubes
- 3 tbsp white vinegar + enough water to cover

**To cook:**
- 2½ large yellow onions, finely chopped
- ½ cup neutral oil (reduced from ⅝ cup for calorie savings)
- 15 garlic cloves, left whole
- 10–12 dried button red chili peppers (reduce to 6–8 if using regular dried chilies)
- 5 tsp coriander seeds
- 2½ tsp cumin seeds
- ⅝ tsp whole cloves
- ⅝ tsp whole black peppercorns
- 2–3 cinnamon sticks (1-inch pieces)
- 5 green cardamom pods
- 3 black cardamom pods
- 1¼ tsp turmeric powder
- 3 tsp kosher salt
- 2 cups plain whole-milk yogurt, well whisked

**After cooking:**
- 2½ inches ginger, crushed
- 5 tbsp whole raw almonds (skins on, or peeled)
- 3 tbsp cilantro, finely chopped (garnish)

**Method (stovetop):**
1. **Marinate:** In a medium bowl, combine beef with vinegar and enough water to cover. Set aside 15 minutes. Drain, rinse, and set aside.
2. **Combine:** In a heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, combine the drained beef with all "to cook" ingredients (onions, oil, garlic, dried chilies, whole and ground spices, salt, yogurt). Mix well. Add 1 cup water (enough to barely cover the meat).
3. **Simmer:** Bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Cover, reduce to low, and simmer 2–2½ hours until beef is very tender. Check every 30 minutes — add ¼ cup water if the pot is drying out, but keep the liquid minimal (this is a thick curry, not a soup).
4. **Bhunai (sauté/reduce):** Remove the lid. Increase heat to medium-high. Sauté, stirring frequently, until most of the moisture has evaporated and the curry thickens to a coating consistency (~15–20 minutes). The oil should begin to separate from the sauce and pool at the edges — this is the sign the bhunai is complete.
5. **Finish:** Add the crushed ginger and almonds. Sauté 2–3 more minutes. Taste and adjust salt. Garnish with cilantro.
6. Discard whole spices (cinnamon sticks, cardamom pods, cloves, peppercorns, dried chilies) before or while eating.

**Storage:** Keeps 4–5 days refrigerated — gets better on day 2. Freezes 3 months. Reheat stovetop or microwave (add a splash of water if too thick). **Highly freezer-friendly** → cook Thursday, freeze Friday for Mon–Tue.

### Scaling Notes
- **Meat scaled to 1.5 kg (3.3× original 454g)** for 8 portions at ~185g raw per portion. Non-meat ingredients scaled at ~2.5× to provide enough sauce for the extra meat without making it soupy. Oil reduced from ⅝ cup to ½ cup for calorie management.
- **Stovetop method** is primary (original recipe is for Instant Pot; stovetop instructions provided by the author in the recipe notes). If using a pressure cooker: cook 45 minutes on high pressure, natural release 15 minutes, then proceed with bhunai step.
- **Black cardamom** (badi elaichi / kala elaichi) is larger and smokier than green cardamom. It gives the dish a distinctive earthy depth. Available at DAC Market. If unavailable, add 1 extra green cardamom pod.
- ⚠️ **GF NOTE:** Naturally GF — no flour, thickened by reduction (bhunai technique). Verify yogurt is plain with no additives.

## DISH 4: Chana Masala (Veg Side)

**Source:** https://www.teaforturmeric.com/chana-masala/
**Why this recipe:** Izzah Cheema's Tea for Turmeric — 4.91/5 from 217 ratings. Authentic Indian and Pakistani-style chana masala. Tangy, spicy, perfectly spiced chickpea curry. Uses canned chickpeas (accessible in Playa del Carmen). The recipe includes a clever baking soda trick for extra-tender chickpeas. Naturally vegan and GF. One-pot, 45-minute cook. Pairs with all three meat dishes this week.

**Original servings:** 4 | **Scaled to:** 12 portions (3× scale)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (~¾ cup)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 220 kcal |
| Protein | 5g (9%) |
| Carbs | 18g (33%) |
| Fat | 15g (61%) |
| Fibre | 4g |

**Macro Calculation Summary:**
Chickpeas (per portion, ~120g drained): 145 cal / 7g P / 22g C / 2.5g F (USDA). Oil (per portion, ~10g): 88 cal / 0P / 0C / 10F. Onion (per portion, ~20g): 8 cal. Tomato (per portion, ~25g): 5 cal. Spices, garlic, ginger: ~5 cal. Total ≈ 220 cal. Protein is modest (5g) but chickpeas also add 4g fibre, making this side filling. Carbs are higher due to chickpeas.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 12 portions, 3× scale)

**Ingredients:**
- ½ cup neutral oil (reduced from ¾ cup for calorie management)
- 1 tbsp cumin seeds
- 3 medium yellow onions, finely chopped
- 15 garlic cloves, crushed
- 3 inches ginger, crushed
- 3 small green chili peppers (serrano or Thai), sliced
- 2 tbsp coriander powder
- 1 tbsp cumin powder
- 1 tbsp paprika or Kashmiri chili powder
- 1½ tsp turmeric powder
- ¾ tsp red chili powder
- 6 medium tomatoes, finely chopped or pureed
- 6 cans (15 oz / 425g each) chickpeas, rinsed and drained (~9 cups / 1.4 kg)
- 3¾ tsp salt (or to taste)
- ¾ tsp baking soda (optional — for extra-tender chickpeas)

**Garnish:**
- 1½ tsp garam masala
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- 9 tbsp cilantro leaves, chopped
- Julienned ginger (optional)
- ⅛ tsp black pepper (optional)

**Method:**
1. Heat a large heavy-bottomed pan over medium-high heat. Add oil. When hot, add cumin seeds and onions. Sauté 7–8 minutes until golden. If the mixture gets dry, deglaze with 1–2 tbsp water.
2. Add garlic, ginger, and green chili peppers. Sauté 2–3 minutes. Deglaze if needed.
3. Add all ground spices (coriander, cumin, paprika/Kashmiri, turmeric, red chili). Stir and roast 30 seconds.
4. Add tomatoes. Sauté 2–3 minutes until soft and pulpy.
5. Add chickpeas, baking soda (if using), salt, and 4 cups water. Raise heat to high, bring to a boil. Reduce to medium, cover, and cook 25–30 minutes until chickpeas are very tender.
6. Uncover, raise heat to high. Use a wooden spoon to slightly crush some chickpeas — this thickens the curry. Sauté 2–3 minutes to desired consistency.
7. Turn off heat. Add garam masala, lemon juice, and cilantro. Sprinkle with julienned ginger and black pepper if desired.

**Storage:** Keeps 4–5 days refrigerated. Freezes 4 months. Reheat stovetop or microwave — add water if too thick. Gets better on day 2.

### Scaling Notes
- **Scaled at 3× from original (4 → 12 portions).** Oil reduced from ¾ cup to ½ cup — the original is quite oily and ½ cup is sufficient for 6 cans of chickpeas.
- **Canned chickpeas:** 6 cans is a lot but manageable. The recipe works equally well with dried chickpeas (soak overnight, cook 45 min, use 9 cups cooked). For this week, canned is faster and more reliable.
- **Baking soda trick:** ¾ tsp baking soda added directly to the curry makes the chickpeas extra tender (restaurant-style). This is optional but recommended — it makes a noticeable difference.
- ⚠️ **GF NOTE:** Naturally vegan and GF. No flour, no gluten-containing ingredients. Verify spices are pure.

## DISH 5: Dal Makhani (Veg Side)

**Source:** https://www.honeywhatscooking.com/dal-makhani/
**Why this recipe:** Nisha Kapur's Honey What's Cooking — 5/5 from 25 votes. Classic Punjabi dal makhani made without cream (uses milk + butter instead). Whole black urad dal and kidney beans, slow-simmered in a tomato-onion masala with a finishing tadka of butter and Kashmiri chili. Naturally GF. The recipe was developed by Nisha's sister, who is known for lighter versions of rich dishes. Gets better on day 2–3 — ideal for batch cooking. Rich, creamy, saucy — exactly the kind of flavorful food the customer prefers.

**Original servings:** 5 | **Scaled to:** 13 portions (2.6× scale)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (~¾ cup)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 307 kcal |
| Protein | 13g (17%) |
| Carbs | 31g (40%) |
| Fat | 15g (44%) |
| Fibre | 14g |

**Macro Calculation Summary:**
Urad dal (per portion, ~30g dry → cooked): 105 cal / 7g P / 19g C / 0.5g F. Rajma (per portion, ~10g dry → cooked): 35 cal / 2.5g P / 6g C / 0.2g F. Butter (per portion, ~8g): 57 cal / 0P / 0C / 6.5F. Milk (per portion, ~15g): 9 cal / 0.5P / 0.7C / 0.5F. Onion-tomato masala (per portion): 40 cal / 1P / 4C / 2.5F. Oil (per portion, ~3g): 27 cal / 0P / 0C / 3F. Spices, kasuri methi: ~5 cal. Total ≈ 307 cal. High fibre (14g) from whole urad dal makes this very filling. Best protein side of the week (13g).

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 13 portions, 2.6× scale)

**To cook lentils:**
- 2½ cups (500g) whole black urad dal (sabut urad)
- 1 cup (200g) kidney beans (rajma)
- 9 cups water (for cooking)
- 2½ tsp salt
- 5 cloves
- 2–3 bay leaves
- 5 green cardamom pods
- 1¼-inch cinnamon stick

**For the masala:**
- 2½ tbsp avocado oil (or neutral oil)
- 5 tbsp unsalted butter, divided
- 2½ tsp cumin seeds
- 5 inches ginger, chopped
- 20 garlic cloves, chopped
- 1 cup red onion, finely chopped
- 4 cups fresh tomatoes, pureed
- 6 green chilies (serrano)
- 4 tsp coriander powder
- Salt to taste
- 1¼ cups milk
- 4 cups water, divided
- 2½ tsp kasoori methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
- 8 tbsp fresh cilantro, chopped
- 1¼ tsp garam masala

**For the tadka (tempering) and garnish:**
- 2½ tbsp unsalted butter
- 2½ tsp Kashmiri chili powder (or paprika)
- 8 tbsp fresh cilantro, chopped
- 2½ tbsp unsalted butter (final garnish)

**Method:**
1. **Soak (Tuesday night):** Rinse urad dal and rajma thoroughly. Soak in plenty of water for 8 hours or overnight.
2. **Cook lentils:** Drain and rinse soaked lentils. Add to a large pot with 9 cups water, salt, cloves, bay leaves, green cardamom, and cinnamon. Bring to a boil, then cover and simmer 45–60 minutes until completely soft (they should have no bite). Skim any foam. Alternatively, pressure cook 30 min on high, natural release 10 min.
3. **Prep:** Chop ginger, garlic, green chilies, and onion. Puree tomatoes with green chilies. Set aside.
4. **Make the masala:** When lentils are done, remove bay leaves and cinnamon stick. Mash lentils 4–5 times with a spoon (helps creaminess). Set aside.
5. Heat a Dutch oven or heavy pot over medium heat. Add 1 tbsp butter + 2½ tbsp oil. Add cumin seeds, let sizzle 30 seconds. Add ginger and garlic, cook 30 seconds. Add onion, cook 3–4 minutes until translucent.
6. Add tomato puree. Cook down 10 minutes on medium-low until paste-like. Add coriander powder and salt. Cook 10 seconds. Add ¼ cup water to prevent sticking, cook 1–2 minutes.
7. **Slow-cook the dal:** Add the cooked lentils to the masala. Stir well. Reduce heat to medium-low. Add 1 tbsp butter. Stir. Add milk. Stir well.
8. Cook 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add 1 cup water. Cook 20 more minutes, stirring. Add ½ cup water. Cook 10 more minutes. Total slow-cook time: ~45 minutes.
9. **Tadka:** Reduce heat to low. Add kasoori methi, 8 tbsp cilantro, and garam masala to the dal. In a small pan, heat 2½ tbsp butter. Add 2½ tsp Kashmiri chili powder. Pour the tadka over the dal. Stir.
10. Top with 2½ tbsp butter for the "makhani" finish. Serve with jeera rice.

**Storage:** Keeps 4–5 days refrigerated — gets significantly better on day 2–3. Freezes up to 6 months. Reheat stovetop or microwave (add water if too thick).

### Scaling Notes
- **Scaled at 2.6× from original (5 → 13 portions).** Urad dal: 2½ cups (500g). Rajma: 1 cup (200g). All other ingredients scaled linearly.
- **No cream — uses milk + butter** per the original recipe. This gives the richness of dal makhani without heavy cream. The long slow-simmer (45 min) is what creates the creamy texture, not the fat content.
- **Urad dal (whole black gram):** This is the key ingredient — do not substitute. Available at DAC Market or Amazon Mexico. See supplement for identification guide.
- **Stovetop lentil cooking:** If no pressure cooker, simmer 45–60 minutes. Add a few drops of oil to prevent foaming. The lentils must be completely soft — undercooked urad dal won't cream properly.
- ⚠️ **GF NOTE:** Naturally GF. Skip asafoetida (hing) if any recipe variation calls for it — most commercial hing is cut with wheat. This recipe does not use hing.

## JEERA RICE (Cumin Basmati Rice)

**Source:** https://www.indianhealthyrecipes.com/jeera-rice-recipe/
**Why this recipe:** Swasthi Shreekanth's Indian Healthy Recipes — one of the most trusted Indian recipe sites. Jeera rice is basmati rice cooked with ghee, whole cumin seeds, and aromatics (bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom). The cumin is bloomed in ghee to release its essential oils before the rice is added. Simple, fragrant, and the ideal accompaniment to Indian curries.

**Original servings:** 3 | **Scaled to:** 25 portions (½ cup cooked each)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (½ cup / ~80g cooked)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 105 kcal |
| Protein | 2g (8%) |
| Carbs | 21g (80%) |
| Fat | 2g (12%) |

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 25 portions at ½ cup each)

**Ingredients:**
- 5 cups (1 kg) uncooked white basmati rice
- 3 tbsp ghee (or neutral oil)
- 2 tbsp cumin seeds
- 3 bay leaves
- 5 green cardamom pods
- 2 cinnamon sticks (1-inch pieces)
- 4 whole cloves
- 10 cups water
- 2 tsp salt (reduce to 1 tsp if using salted stock)

**Method:**
1. **Rinse rice:** Place basmati in a fine mesh strainer. Rinse under cold water until water runs clear (3–4 rinses, ~3 minutes). Soak 30 minutes. Drain thoroughly.
2. **Bloom cumin:** Heat ghee in a large heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add cumin seeds, bay leaves, cardamom pods, cinnamon sticks, and cloves. Sauté until cumin seeds sizzle and become fragrant (30–60 seconds). Do not burn.
3. **Toast rice:** Add drained rice to the pot. Stir gently 1–2 minutes to coat every grain in ghee.
4. **Cook:** Add water and salt. Raise heat to bring to a boil. Once boiling, cover immediately with a tight-fitting lid and reduce heat to the lowest possible setting.
5. **Cook 15–20 minutes.** Do NOT lift the lid during cooking. Rice is done when all liquid is absorbed (tilt pot to check — no water pooling).
6. **Rest:** Remove from heat. Keep covered, rest 10 minutes.
7. **Fluff:** Remove lid. Fluff gently with a fork. Remove whole spices before packing (bay leaves, cardamom pods, cinnamon, cloves).
8. **Note:** 5 cups of raw rice is a large batch. If your largest pot can't handle this, split into 2 batches (2.5 cups rice each, halve all ingredients). Cook simultaneously if you have two pots.

**Storage:** 5 days fridge. Reheats perfectly in microwave — add a splash of water, cover loosely, heat 1–2 min. Can be frozen (slightly softer texture upon thawing). Cooked Wednesday; fridge portions eaten Wed–Sat, freezer portions for Mon–Tue.

### Scaling Notes
- **Rice scaled to 5 cups (1 kg) raw** for 25 portions at ½ cup cooked each. The 1:2 rice:water ratio is standard for soaked basmati.
- **Portion size:** ½ cup cooked per meal (reduced from ⅔ cup to help manage calorie targets given the rich curries this week). If you prefer more rice, increase to ⅔ cup — add ~50 cal and 10g carbs per meal.
- **Ghee vs. oil:** Ghee is traditional and gives the best flavor. If unavailable, use neutral oil (avocado, canola). Butter works but may brown — use low heat.

### GF Watch Points
- **Ghee:** Pure ghee is GF. Check label — some commercial ghee is cut with vegetable oil or has wheat-derived flavorings. Best: buy pure ghee or make your own by simmering butter.
- **Yogurt:** Plain, whole-milk yogurt should be GF. Check for no added starches or thickeners.
- **Stock/bouillon:** Many Mexican commercial stocks (Knorr, Maggi) contain gluten. Use water + extra salt + a bay leaf for the korma, or find GF-certified stock.
- **Spices:** Ground spices are occasionally cut with flour as anti-caking agent. Buy pure spices from reputable brands or in bulk at DAC Market.
- **Coconut cream:** Check label — should be 100% coconut extract. Some brands add stabilizers. Aroy-D and Thai Kitchen brands are reliably GF.
- **All recipes are naturally GF** — none use flour, bread, or wheat-based thickeners. The risk is only from spice blend fillers and stock.
