## 🐷 DISH 1: Cochinita Pibil (Slow-Cooker Yucatán Pork)

**Source:** https://www.rickbayless.com/recipe/slow-cooked-achiote-pork/
**Why this recipe:** Rick Bayless is the foremost authority on Mexican cooking in the English-speaking world — chef-owner of Frontera Grill and Topolobampo, James Beard Award winner, and host of 12 seasons of *Mexico: One Plate at a Time* on PBS. This is his everyday slow-cooker adaptation of cochinita pibil, designed specifically for home cooks. It uses prepared achiote seasoning and a slow cooker (which we have). The banana leaves are optional. Naturally GF. The 6-hour slow cook produces fall-apart pork that gets better with time — ideal for the 6-portion dish that stretches to Saturday.

**Original servings:** 6 | **Scaled to:** 6 portions at 70% size (use original recipe as-is)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (meat only, no rice/veg/sides)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 290 kcal |
| Protein | 22g (30%) |
| Carbs | 3g (4%) |
| Fat | 21g (66%) |
| Fibre | 1g |

*Includes ~140g cooked pork shoulder + achiote marinade. Pork shoulder is inherently fatty — this is the highest-fat meat dish. Protein is solid at 22g per 70% portion.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
Pork shoulder (~140g cooked, trimmed of excess fat): 280 cal / 22g P / 0g C / 21g F. Achiote marinade (70% of full portion): 10 cal / 0g P / 3g C / 0g F. Total ≈ 290 cal.

### Batch Recipe (Makes 6 portions at 70% size — use original recipe as-is)

**Ingredients:**
- 1.4 kg (3 lbs) bone-in pork shoulder roast
- 1/2 of a 3.5-oz (100g) package prepared achiote seasoning *(check GF — see notes)*
- 1/2 cup lime juice (about 4–5 limes)
- 2 tsp salt
- 1 large white onion, sliced 1/4-inch thick
- 1 1/2 large red onions, thinly sliced (for pickled onion)
- Banana leaves, defrosted if frozen (optional)
- About 1/3 cup roasted-chile salsa or habanero hot sauce (Yucateco or Frontera brand)

**Method:**
1. Place the achiote seasoning in a small bowl. Pour in 1/2 cup of the lime juice and 2 tsp salt. Work into a smooth, thick marinade with the back of a spoon.
2. If using banana leaves, cut 2 two-foot sections and use them to line the slow cooker — lay one down the length, the other across the width. Lay in the pork and pour the marinade over and around the roast. Scatter the white onion over the meat.
3. Pour 1/2 cup water around the meat. Fold up the banana leaves to roughly cover everything (or just put the lid on if not using leaves). Slow-cook on HIGH for 6 hours until the meat is fall-off-the-bone tender.
4. While the meat cooks, combine the 1 1/2 red onions with the remaining lime juice in a small bowl. Sprinkle with 1/2 tsp salt, toss, and set aside to marinate (stir occasionally).
5. Transfer the meat and onions to a cutting board. Spoon off any rendered fat from the juices. If there's a lot of broth (2+ cups), boil it down to about 1 cup in a saucepan. Season with salt to taste.
6. Shred the pork with two forks. Spoon the reduced sauce over the meat. Top with pickled red onions. Serve with arroz rojo, slaw, warm corn tortillas (optional), and salsa/hot sauce.

**Storage:** Keeps 5 days refrigerated. Freezes well up to 3 months. Reheat gently on stovetop or microwave. The Saturday portion for the assistant holds fine to day 6 refrigerated — this is why cochinita gets the 6-portion allocation.

### Scaling Notes
- Original recipe makes 6 full servings. We use it as-is for 6 portions at 70% size — each portion is simply a smaller serving. The cook divides the shredded pork into 6 equal portions.
- **Slow cooker note:** Rick Bayless specifies HIGH for 6 hours. The dish can hold on "keep warm" for 4 more hours. No slow cooker? Braise in a Dutch oven at 300°F (150°C) for 2.5–3 hours.

## 🍗 DISH 2: Chicken Mole Poblano

**Source (chicken):** https://www.holajalapeno.com/chicken-mole/
**Source (mole sauce):** https://www.holajalapeno.com/mole-poblano/
**Why this recipe:** Kate Ramos is a classically-trained chef and cookbook author with 30+ years of experience, featured in *Better Homes & Gardens, Parade, Elle,* and *Cosmopolitan*. Her site ¡Hola! Jalapeño is one of the most respected Mexican cooking blogs. This mole poblano is made from scratch with dried chiles, nuts, seeds, and Mexican chocolate — thickened with a charred corn tortilla instead of bread, making it naturally gluten-free. The chicken is braised (not just poached) for deeper flavor. Mole improves significantly over 2–3 days, making it perfect for batch cooking.

**Original servings:** 6–8 (chicken) + 8–10 (sauce) | **Scaled to:** 6 portions at 70% size

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (meat only, no rice/veg)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 350 kcal |
| Protein | 32g (36%) |
| Carbs | 14g (16%) |
| Fat | 18g (46%) |
| Fibre | 2g |

*Includes ~140g cooked skinless chicken thigh + 2.5 tbsp mole sauce. Removing the skin before eating significantly reduces fat. This is the highest-protein meat dish per 70% portion.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
Chicken thigh meat, skinless (~140g cooked, from bone-in skin-on thighs): 230 cal / 29g P / 0g C / 12g F. Mole sauce (2.5 tbsp / ~40ml): 120 cal / 2.5g P / 14g C / 5.5g F. Total ≈ 350 cal.

### Batch Recipe (Makes 6 portions at 70% size)

**For the mole sauce (makes ~2 cups — use 1 cup for this recipe, freeze the rest):**
- 5 dried pasilla chiles
- 4 dried ancho chiles
- 2 dried chipotle chiles
- 1/4 cup + 1.5 tbsp avocado oil (or vegetable oil), divided
- 1/4 cup raisins (about 1.5 oz)
- 1/4 cup prunes (about 2 oz)
- 1/2 large white onion, quartered
- 4 cloves garlic, unpeeled
- 1-inch piece canela (Ceylon cinnamon) or regular cinnamon
- 5 black peppercorns
- 1/4 tsp anise seed
- 5 oz tomatoes (about 1.5 large), cored
- 2 tbsp sliced almonds
- 2 tbsp shelled pistachios (or peanuts)
- 1/4 cup white sesame seeds
- 1/2 stale corn tortilla
- 1/2 tsp dried Mexican oregano
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp ground coriander
- 3/4 oz (about 20g) Mexican dark chocolate (Ibarra or Hernán brand)

**For the chicken:**
- 7 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs (about 1.4 kg)
- 1/4 large white onion, chopped
- 1 small carrot, chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, chopped
- 1/4 cup avocado oil
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 4 cups water

**Method — Mole Sauce (make the day before if possible):**
1. Remove stems and seeds from all dried chiles. Rinse under running water to remove dust.
2. Bring a large saucepan of water to a boil. Heat 2 tbsp of the oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Fry the chiles in batches, turning frequently, until they deepen in colour and become fragrant (30–60 seconds per side). Transfer to the boiling water. Add raisins and prunes. Remove from heat and soak 30 minutes.
3. Heat a comal or dry skillet over high heat. Char the onion wedges and unpeeled garlic cloves until blackened on all sides, about 15 minutes. Transfer to a bowl. Peel garlic once cool.
4. Reduce heat to low. Toast canela, peppercorns, and anise seed until fragrant, 30–60 seconds. Grind to a fine powder in a spice grinder or molcajete.
5. Return heat to high. Char the tomatoes until skins are blackened on all sides, about 10 minutes. Transfer to a bowl.
6. Heat 1 tsp oil in a small pan. Toast almonds and pistachios until golden, 2 minutes. Remove. Add another tsp oil, toast sesame seeds 1 minute, stirring constantly. Remove. Wipe pan clean.
7. Heat 1 more tsp oil. Fry the tortilla until crisp. Remove and break into pieces.
8. Remove chiles and dried fruit from soaking liquid (SAVE the liquid). Blend chiles and fruit with 1/4 cup soaking liquid until smooth. Strain through a fine mesh sieve into a bowl, pushing through with a spoon.
9. Blend the onion, peeled garlic, ground spices, nuts, 2 tbsp sesame seeds, tortilla pieces, coriander, salt, and oregano with 1/4 cup soaking liquid until smooth. Strain into a separate bowl.
10. Blend the tomatoes until smooth. Strain into a third bowl.
11. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chile puree — it will sputter. Stir constantly and fry 4–5 minutes until it darkens. Add the nut puree, stir in. Add the tomato puree and the chocolate. Stir constantly until chocolate melts and sauce is smooth.
12. If too thick, add soaking liquid 1/4 cup at a time until it coats the back of a spoon. Taste and adjust salt. Makes about 2 cups. Use 1 cup for this recipe; freeze the rest.

**Method — Chicken:**
1. Season chicken thighs generously with salt and pepper on all sides.
2. Heat oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear chicken until golden brown on both sides, 8–10 minutes total. Remove to a plate.
3. Add chopped onion, carrot, and garlic. Season with salt and pepper. Cook, stirring, until browned, about 5 minutes.
4. Add 4 cups water and scrape up browned bits. Return chicken to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cover and cook 1 hour until tender.
5. Remove chicken to a plate. Strain the broth through a fine mesh sieve (discard vegetables). Remove and discard chicken skin. Shred or leave thighs whole.
6. In the same pot over medium heat, add 1 cup mole sauce. Thin with 1/2 cup reserved chicken broth until silky. Bring to a simmer.
7. Add chicken, turning to coat. Simmer on low 20 minutes, stirring frequently so the mole doesn't burn.
8. Serve over arroz rojo with mole sauce spooned over. Garnish with sliced white onion and sesame seeds.

**Storage:** Keeps 4–5 days refrigerated — mole gets better each day. Freezes 3 months. Reheat stovetop on low (stir frequently — mole burns easily) or microwave.

### Scaling Notes
- Mole sauce: the 50% scale recipe makes ~2 cups. Use 1 cup for 6 portions (generous 2.5 tbsp each). Freeze the remaining 1 cup for future meals.
- Chicken: 7 bone-in, skin-on thighs (1.4 kg) for 6 portions. After cooking and removing skin/bones, yields ~875g meat = ~145g per portion. Close to the 140g target.
- ⚠️ **GF NOTE:** The mole is thickened with a corn tortilla instead of bread — this is what makes it naturally GF. Do NOT substitute bolillo or bread. Check that your achiote/seasoning blends are GF.

## 🐟 DISH 3: Pescado a la Veracruzana (Veracruz-Style Fish)

**Source:** https://mexicanfoodjournal.com/veracruz-style-white-fish/
**Why this recipe:** Mexican Food Journal is run by Douglas Cullen, who spent 25 years living across Mexico. The site has published nearly 200 authentic Mexican recipes since 2013. This recipe has a 3.55-star rating from 48 votes with consistent rave reviews. It's one of the few Mexican sauces with no chile — a mild, Mediterranean-influenced tomato sauce with olives, capers, and herbs. Naturally GF. Quick to cook (30 minutes), making it the freshest dish in the rotation. The traditional fish is huachinango (red snapper), which is readily available in Playa del Carmen.

**Original servings:** 4 | **Scaled to:** 4 portions at 70% size (0.7× original)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (meat only, no rice/veg/sides)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 245 kcal |
| Protein | 35g (57%) |
| Carbs | 6g (10%) |
| Fat | 8g (33%) |
| Fibre | 1g |

*Includes ~140g cooked white fish + 3.5 tbsp Veracruz sauce. This is the highest-protein, lowest-fat meat dish by far. Lean fish is an excellent protein source.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
White fish (~140g cooked, huachinango/red snapper): 160 cal / 33g P / 0g C / 1.5g F. Veracruz sauce (3.5 tbsp — tomato, olives, capers, onion, garlic, oil, thyme, bay leaf): 85 cal / 1.5g P / 6g C / 6g F. Total ≈ 245 cal.

### Batch Recipe (Makes 4 portions at 70% size, 0.7× original)

**Ingredients:**
- 600g firm white fish fillets (huachinango/red snapper, mero/grouper, or tilapia)
- 4 medium tomatoes
- 3 cloves garlic
- 2 tsp dried thyme
- 3/4 tsp black pepper
- 3/4 tsp epazote (or 3/8 tsp dried oregano)
- 3/4 tsp salt + more to taste
- 4 bay leaves
- 20 green olives, chopped
- 8 capers
- 3/4 cup low-sodium chicken broth
- 3/4 medium white onion, roughly chopped
- 4 tbsp cooking oil, divided

**Method:**
1. **Make the sauce:** Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Fry the onion and garlic for 4 minutes. Add the tomato, black pepper, and salt. Reduce heat to low and cook 15 minutes until tomatoes release their liquid.
2. Crush the tomatoes, onion, and garlic with a bean masher or potato masher (or pulse briefly in blender for a smoother sauce).
3. Add thyme, epazote (or oregano), and bay leaves. Stir. Add chicken broth. Add chopped olives and capers. Simmer 15 minutes.
4. **Cook the fish:** In a separate pan over medium heat, lightly brown each side of the fish fillets in 2 tbsp oil — don't let them get too brown.
5. Once browned on both sides, place the fish fillets in the simmering sauce. Cook 10 minutes until the sauce has thickened slightly and the fish is fully cooked and has absorbed the flavours.
6. Check and adjust salt. Serve over arroz rojo with sauce spooned over and a slice of avocado.

**Storage:** ⚠️ Fish doesn't hold as long. Eat within 2–3 days. Portions are spread across Mon (asst), Tue (cust lunch), Wed (cust dinner), Thu (asst). The Mon–Wed portions are fine refrigerated. The Thu portion MUST be frozen right after cooking and thawed Wednesday night. Alternatively, freeze the sauce base (steps 1–3) separately and cook the fish fresh on Thursday.

### Scaling Notes
- Scaled at 0.7× from original (4 full servings → 4 portions at 70% size). Fish: 600g total (140g per portion). Sauce ingredients scaled at 0.7×.
- ⚠️ **Fish freshness:** The Thursday portion (day 4) must be frozen. Alternatively, freeze the sauce base (steps 1–3) separately and cook the fish fresh on Thursday.
- **Fish selection:** Huachinango (red snapper) is the traditional choice and is readily available in Playa del Carmen. Mero (grouper) is an excellent alternative. Both are firm white fish that hold up well to simmering.

## 🫘 DISH 4: Frijoles Charros (Mexican Cowboy Beans)

**Source:** https://www.mexicoinmykitchen.com/charro-beans-soup-recipe/
**Why this recipe:** Mexico In My Kitchen is run by Mely Martínez, author of *The Mexican Home Kitchen* cookbook. This recipe has 4.72 stars from 32 votes. Mely is from northern Mexico where charro beans originated. The recipe uses pinto beans cooked with chorizo, bacon, tomatoes, onions, garlic, and serrano peppers — a hearty, brothy bean dish that's naturally GF. Beans hold excellently for batch cooking and the flavours deepen over time. The slow cooker can be used for the bean-cooking step.

**Original servings:** 8 | **Scaled to:** 8 portions at 70% size (0.7× original)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (veg only, no rice/meat)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 275 kcal |
| Protein | 13g (19%) |
| Carbs | 26g (38%) |
| Fat | 13g (43%) |
| Fibre | 10g |

*Includes ~1/2 cup beans with broth, 20g chorizo, 10g bacon, vegetables. This is a carb-heavy bean dish — the chorizo and bacon add flavour and fat. High fibre (10g) makes it very filling.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
Pinto beans (1/2 cup cooked with broth): 120 cal / 8g P / 22g C / 0.5g F. Chorizo (20g): 80 cal / 2.5g P / 0g C / 7g F. Bacon (10g): 55 cal / 2g P / 0g C / 5g F. Tomato/onion/garlic/cilantro: 20 cal / 0.5g P / 4g C / 0g F. Total ≈ 275 cal.

### Batch Recipe (Makes 8 portions at 70% size, 0.7× original)

**Ingredients:**
- 1 1/2 cups dry pinto beans (or 3 cans / 425g each, drained and rinsed)
- 1/2 medium white onion (for cooking beans)
- 3 cloves garlic, peeled (for cooking beans)
- 5 cups water (if cooking from dry)
- 60g (2 oz) Mexican chorizo
- 30g (1 oz) bacon, chopped
- 1/2 medium white onion, chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, chopped
- 2 large tomatoes, chopped
- 2 serrano peppers, chopped (use 1 jalapeño for milder heat)
- 1/2 cup cilantro, chopped
- Salt to taste

**Method:**
1. **Cook the beans:** Place dry pinto beans, 1/2 onion, 3 garlic cloves, and 5 cups water in a large pot. Cook on stovetop over medium-high heat for ~2 hours until tender. *Or use slow cooker: cook on HIGH for 5–6 hours.* *Or use canned beans: skip to step 2, use 3 cans with their liquid + 1 cup water.*
2. Once beans are cooked, drain the cooking onion and garlic (discard them). Keep the beans and their broth. Set aside.
3. **Cook the meats:** In a skillet over medium heat, fry the bacon and chorizo together for 10 minutes, breaking up the chorizo.
4. Add the chopped onion, chopped garlic, and serrano pepper. Fry 2 more minutes.
5. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook, stirring occasionally, 8 minutes until the tomato releases its juices and forms a sauce.
6. Stir the meat-tomato mixture into the pot with the beans and broth. Simmer 15 minutes to blend flavours. Add water if needed — consistency should be brothy.
7. Add salt to taste. Stir in chopped cilantro just before serving.
8. Serve with arroz rojo and meat dish.

**Storage:** Keeps 5 days refrigerated — gets better each day. Freezes excellently up to 3 months. Reheat stovetop or microwave.

### Scaling Notes
- Scaled at 0.7× of original (8 full servings → 8 portions at 70% size). 1.5 cups dry beans yields ~4.5 cups cooked with broth = ~1/2 cup per portion. Chorizo and bacon scaled to 60g and 30g respectively — enough for flavour without overwhelming the beans.
- **Slow cooker option:** The beans can cook in the slow cooker on HIGH for 5–6 hours (or LOW for 7–8 hours). This frees up the stovetop for other dishes.
- ⚠️ **PROTEIN NOTE:** This dish is carb-heavy (26g carbs, 19% protein). When paired with a meat dish, the combined meal protein is adequate. The fish + frijoles pairing is the strongest at 30% protein.

## 🫑 DISH 5: Rajas con Crema (Roasted Poblano Strips with Corn & Cream)

**Source:** https://www.muydelish.com/rajas-con-crema/
**Why this recipe:** Muy Delish is run by Ana Frias, who was born and raised in Sonora, Mexico. This recipe has 4.94 stars from 48 votes — an exceptional rating. It's a classic Mexican home dish that rarely appears on restaurant menus but is a staple in central and northern Mexican kitchens. Roasted poblano peppers are sliced into strips (rajas), sautéed with onion, garlic, and corn, then simmered in Mexican crema and melted Oaxaca cheese. Naturally GF. The air fryer can be used for roasting the poblanos.

**Original servings:** 4 | **Scaled to:** 8 portions at 70% size (1.4× original)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (veg only, no rice/meat)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 283 kcal |
| Protein | 9g (13%) |
| Carbs | 14g (20%) |
| Fat | 22g (70%) |
| Fibre | 3g |

*Includes 1/2 poblano pepper, 1/4 cup corn, cream cheese, crema, Oaxaca cheese. This is the highest-fat, lowest-protein dish. The cream and cheese dominate the calorie profile.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
Poblano pepper (1/2 pepper, roasted): 17 cal / 0.5g P / 3.5g C / 0g F. Corn (1/4 cup): 28 cal / 1g P / 6g C / 0.3g F. Cream cheese (0.7 oz / 20g): 70 cal / 1.5g P / 0.5g C / 7g F. Mexican crema (2 tbsp + 1 tsp): 70 cal / 0.5g P / 1g C / 7g F. Oaxaca cheese (1/4 cup shredded, ~28g): 77 cal / 5g P / 1g C / 6g F. Butter/onion/garlic: 21 cal / 0g P / 1.5g C / 2g F. Total ≈ 283 cal.

### Batch Recipe (Makes 8 portions at 70% size, 1.4× original)

**Ingredients:**
- 6 large poblano peppers
- 3 tbsp butter
- 3/4 large white onion, sliced in strips
- 3 cloves garlic, finely chopped
- 1 1/2 cups corn kernels (fresh, canned, or frozen/thawed)
- 1 1/2 tsp chicken bouillon powder (or vegetable bouillon for vegetarian)
- 3/4 tsp black pepper
- 6 oz (170g) cream cheese
- 1 1/2 cups Mexican crema (or heavy cream or sour cream)
- 1 1/2 cups shredded Oaxaca cheese (or Chihuahua, Mozzarella, Monterey Jack)

**Method:**
1. **Roast the poblanos:** Place poblano peppers on a baking sheet and broil in the oven, turning occasionally, until charred on all sides (15–20 minutes). *Or air fryer: 400°F (200°C) for 7–10 minutes, turning halfway.* *Or directly over a gas flame on the stove, turning with tongs.*
2. Transfer roasted peppers to a bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let steam 10–15 minutes.
3. Peel off the charred skins, remove stems and seeds, and slice into strips (rajas).
4. In a large skillet over medium-high heat, melt butter. Add onion strips and sauté 3–4 minutes until softened.
5. Add garlic and poblano strips. Cook 2 minutes, stirring often.
6. Add corn, bouillon, and black pepper. Mix well.
7. Reduce heat to medium. Add cream cheese and crema. Stir until cream cheese melts and everything is combined.
8. Cook 5 minutes until the sauce bubbles gently and thickens slightly.
9. Stir in shredded cheese, cover the pan, and turn off the heat. Let sit 2–3 minutes until cheese melts.
10. Serve with arroz rojo and meat dish.

**Storage:** Keeps 4 days refrigerated. Freezes up to 3 months (sauce may separate slightly — stir while reheating). Reheat stovetop on low or microwave. ⚠️ For the Friday portions (day 5), freeze 2 portions on Sunday and thaw Thursday night.

### Scaling Notes
- Scaled at 1.4× of original (4 full servings → 8 portions at 70% size). 6 poblanos for 8 portions (3/4 poblano per portion). Cream cheese at 6 oz (170g). Crema and cheese scaled to 1.5 cups each.
- **Air fryer tip:** The air fryer is excellent for roasting poblanos — 400°F for 7–10 minutes, turning halfway. Much faster than the oven.
- ⚠️ **FRESHNESS NOTE:** Rajas holds 4 days refrigerated. Cooked Sunday, good through Thursday. Friday's 2 portions must be frozen Sunday and thawed Thursday night.
- ⚠️ **PROTEIN NOTE:** This is the lowest-protein dish (9g per portion). When paired with fish, the combined meal is 27% protein. When paired with cochinita, it's only 18%. Consider adding a boiled egg (+6g protein, +70 cal) on cochinita + rajas days.

## 🥗 SIDE: Cilantro-Lime Cabbage Slaw

**Source:** https://www.holajalapeno.com/cilantro-lime-cole-slaw-recipe/
**Why this recipe:** Also from Kate Ramos at ¡Hola! Jalapeño. This is a mayonnaise-free, vegan, GF slaw with cabbage, carrots, red onion, cilantro, and a lime-olive oil dressing. It's specifically designed as a topping for cochinita pibil and fish tacos — perfect for this week. Keeps its crunch for 3–4 days refrigerated.

**Original servings:** 6 | **Scaled to:** 6 servings (for the 6 cochinita portions)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 50 kcal |
| Protein | 1g |
| Carbs | 6g |
| Fat | 3g |

### Batch Recipe (Makes 6 servings, 1.5× original)

**Ingredients:**
- 1/2 head red cabbage, thinly sliced
- 1/2 head green cabbage, thinly sliced
- 1 large carrot, julienned or grated
- 1/3 red onion, thinly sliced
- 4 tbsp chopped cilantro

**Dressing:**
- 3 tbsp freshly squeezed lime juice
- 1 tbsp rice wine vinegar (or apple cider vinegar)
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- 1/4 tsp ground cumin
- 4 tbsp olive oil

**Method:**
1. Combine all vegetables in a large bowl and toss.
2. In a small bowl, whisk lime juice, vinegar, salt, pepper, and cumin until salt dissolves. Slowly drizzle in oil while whisking continuously.
3. Drizzle dressing over vegetables and toss to coat. Taste and adjust salt/lime.
4. **Make ahead:** Prep vegetables and dressing separately. Toss together up to 1 hour before serving (slaw loses crunch after a few hours in dressing).

**Storage:** Undressed slaw keeps 3–4 days refrigerated in an airtight container. Dressed slaw is best within 1–2 hours.

## 🍚 ARROZ ROJO (Mexican Red Rice) — Cook Once, Eat All Week

**Source:** https://www.rickbayless.com/recipe/red-tomato-rice/
**Cross-check:** https://www.mexicoinmykitchen.com/mexican-rice-recipe-authentic/ (Mely Martínez, 4.75★/39 votes)
**Why this recipe:** Rick Bayless's classic arroz rojo — rice toasted in oil, then cooked in a blended tomato/onion/garlic puree with chicken broth. The toasting technique ensures fluffy, separate grains. Mely Martínez's version uses the same technique with slightly more oil — both are consistent. Naturally GF. More flavorful and authentic than the plain white rice it replaces.

**Original yield:** 6 cups cooked (6–8 servings) | **Used for:** 16 portions at 1/3 cup each (5.3 cups needed)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (1/3 cup / 80g cooked)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 115 kcal |
| Protein | 2g |
| Carbs | 22g |

### Macro Calculation Summary

### Batch Recipe (Makes ~6 cups cooked — use as-is)

**Ingredients:**
- 1 15-oz can diced tomatoes (fire-roasted preferred), lightly drained
- 1/2 small white onion, roughly chopped
- 2 garlic cloves, peeled and halved
- 1 1/2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 1/2 cups white rice (medium-grain preferred, long-grain fine)
- 1 3/4 cups chicken broth
- 3 serrano chiles (or 2 jalapeños), slit lengthwise
- 1/4 cup coarsely chopped fresh parsley
- 3/4 tsp salt (if using salted broth) or 1 1/2 tsp (if using unsalted broth/water)

**Method:**
1. **Make the tomato puree:** In a blender, combine the drained tomatoes, onion, and garlic. Blend to a smooth puree.
2. **Fry the rice:** In a medium (3-quart) saucepan with a tight-fitting lid, heat the oil over medium. Add the raw rice and stir frequently until the kernels turn from translucent to milky white, 5–6 minutes. Some light browning is fine.
3. Add the tomato puree and stir. Cook 2–3 minutes until it reduces and looks somewhat dry.
4. **Simmer:** Add the chicken broth, bring to a full boil, then add the slit chiles, parsley, and salt. Stir thoroughly, scraping down any rice kernels clinging to the sides.
5. Cover and cook over the lowest heat for 20 minutes. Do NOT lift the lid or stir during cooking.
6. Remove from heat. Use a fork to gently fluff the rice and release the steam. Re-cover and let stand 5 minutes for the starch to firm.
7. Remove the chiles (or leave as decoration). Yields ~6 cups cooked rice.

**Storage:** Keeps 4–5 days refrigerated. Reheat in microwave with a sprinkle of water. Do not freeze — texture degrades.

### Scaling Notes
- Recipe makes 6 cups. We need 5.3 cups (16 × 1/3 cup). The extra 2/3 cup is buffer — the cook can eat it or serve a slightly larger portion.
- **Key technique:** Do NOT stir the rice while it simmers. Stirring releases starch and makes it gummy. Fluff only with a fork after cooking.
- **Pot size:** Use a wide pot (about 8 inches diameter) for even cooking. A deep, narrow pot will cook unevenly.

**To boost protein on lower-protein days:**
- Add a boiled egg (+6g protein, +70 cal) — brings cochinita + rajas to ~21% protein

### GF Watch Points
- **Achiote seasoning (recado rojo):** Some commercial brands cut with wheat flour. Check the label — common GF brands include El Yucateco and Mexicali. If unsure, make your own: blend 3 tbsp annatto seeds, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp coriander, 1 tsp oregano, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp allspice, 1/2 tsp black pepper.
- **Chicken bouillon powder:** Some brands contain wheat. Knorr is NOT reliably GF. Look for Herdez, Better Than Bouillon (GF-labeled), or use salt + a pinch of cumin and garlic powder instead.
- **Mexican chocolate (Ibarra):** Contains cacao, sugar, cinnamon, and soy lecithin — naturally GF. Hernán brand is also GF.
- **Chorizo:** Some commercial chorizos use bread crumbs as filler. Check the label or buy from a carnicería (butcher) that makes it fresh. Cacique brand chorizo is generally GF but always verify.
- **Corn tortillas:** Naturally GF, but check for cross-contamination if highly sensitive.
- **Canned diced tomatoes:** Check label for added wheat — most are GF but verify.

### Freshness Schedule
- **Eat early (Mon–Wed):** Pescado a la Veracruzana. Fish doesn't hold as long. Mon (asst), Tue (cust lunch), Wed (cust dinner) are fine refrigerated. The Thu (asst) portion MUST be frozen and thawed Wednesday night.
- **Freeze for Friday:** Rajas con Crema — 2 portions must be frozen Sunday and thawed Thursday night for Friday meals.
- **Peak on day 2–3:** Chicken Mole and Frijoles Charros — both improve as flavours meld.
- **Steady all week:** Cochinita Pibil — holds well for 6 days (the reason it gets the Saturday portion).
- **Saturday (assistant only):** Cochinita + Frijoles — both hold fine to day 6.
- **Slaw:** Prep vegetables and dressing separately. Dress up to 1 hour before eating. Undressed vegetables keep 3–4 days.

### Cooking Order (Sunday Batch Cook)
1. **Start beans first** (if using dry): Slow cooker on HIGH, 5–6 hours. Or soak overnight and cook on stovetop.
2. **Make mole sauce** (can be done Saturday or Sunday morning): It's the most time-consuming step.
3. **Start cochinita in slow cooker** (if beans are done) or in a second pot: 6 hours on HIGH.
4. **Make arroz rojo**: 25 minutes. Can be done while other dishes simmer.
5. **Braise chicken for mole**: 1 hour simmer.
6. **Make frijoles charros**: 30 minutes once beans are cooked.
7. **Make rajas con crema**: 25 minutes. Roast poblanos in air fryer.
8. **Cook fish Veracruzana**: 30 minutes — do this last so it's the freshest.
9. **Make slaw**: 15 minutes — prep vegetables and dressing separately, store in fridge.
10. **Pickle red onions**: 15 minutes — done while cochinita cooks.
