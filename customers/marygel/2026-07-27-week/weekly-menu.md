## 🥩 DISH 1: Chile Colorado (Mexican Red Chile Beef Stew)

**Source:** https://www.lemonblossoms.com/blog/chile-colorado-beef-stew/  
**Why:** 5★ / 70 reviews. Explicit **stovetop** method (also oven/IP — we use stovetop only). Dried chile sauce, no nuts. Freezes excellently. Perfect Sunday braise.

**Original servings:** 8 | **Scaled to:** 4 dinner portions (generous)

### Per-portion macros (meat only, ~200g cooked beef + sauce)
| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | ~420 kcal |
| Protein | 38g |
| Carbs | 8g |
| Fat | 26g |
| Fibre | 2g |

**Macro note:** Recipe card 474 kcal/serving for full 8-serv batch with 4 lb beef — our 4 portions use ~1.1 kg beef raw → similar density. USDA chuck braised supports ~38g P per portion.

### Scaled batch (4 portions)

**Chile paste:**
- 2 dried guajillo chiles, stems/seeds removed  
- 3 dried New Mexico or ancho chiles, stems/seeds removed  
- **Omit chile de árbol** for medium heat (or use 1 max)  
- 2 cups chicken or beef broth (warm), divided use  
- 1 tomato, charred on dry skillet (or ¾ cup canned fire-roasted tomatoes)

**Stew:**
- 1.1 kg beef chuck (diezmillo), 2.5 cm cubes  
- Salt & black pepper  
- 1½ Tbsp oil  
- ½ medium onion, diced  
- 3 garlic cloves, minced  
- 1½ tsp ground cumin  
- 1 tsp dried oregano (Mexican if available)  
- Pinch ground allspice  
- 1 Tbsp apple cider vinegar  
- 1 Tbsp masa harina + splash cooking liquid (slurry)  
- Optional: ½ tsp chicken bouillon  

**Method (stovetop only):**
1. Prep chiles: remove stems/seeds. Toast in dry skillet 30–60 sec per side until fragrant (not black). Or microwave 2×15 sec. Soak in 1 cup warm broth 20–30 min (or microwave 2 min covered).
2. Char tomato in dry hot skillet, turning, until blistered.
3. Blend chiles + soaking liquid + tomato until very smooth (2 min). Hold lid with towel if warm.
4. Season beef. Sear in oil in heavy pot, batches, until browned. Set aside.
5. Sauté onion 3–4 min; garlic 1 min; cumin/oregano/allspice 1 min.
6. Return beef + juices. Add chile puree + remaining ~1 cup broth. Boil, then **low simmer, covered, 1½ hours** until fork-tender. Stir occasionally.
7. Mix masa with 2–3 Tbsp hot liquid; stir in with vinegar; simmer 5 min. Salt to taste.

**Storage:** Fridge 3–4 days. Freezes 2–3 months. Reheat stovetop + splash broth.

### Scaling notes
- Original 4 lb / 8 serv → 1.1 kg / 4 serv (~0.55×). Spices ~0.5×. Arbol omitted for medium heat.

## 🐷 DISH 2: Pork Chile Verde

**Source:** https://www.muydelish.com/pork-chile-verde/  
**Why:** Ana Frias (Sonora) — 5★ / 12 ratings. Fully **stovetop**; tomatillos roasted on **skillet** (not oven). Bright tomatillo sauce, no nuts. Freezer-friendly.

**Original servings:** 6 | **Scaled to:** 4 portions

### Per-portion macros (meat + sauce)
| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | ~320 kcal |
| Protein | 36g |
| Carbs | 10g |
| Fat | 14g |

### Scaled batch (4 portions)

**Pork:**
- 1.2 kg pork shoulder or loin, 2–3 cm cubes (shoulder more forgiving for batch)  
- 2 garlic cloves  
- ¼ onion  
- 1 bay leaf  
- 1 tsp salt + ½ tsp black pepper  
- Water to cover  

**Salsa verde:**
- 7–8 tomatillos, husked  
- 1 jalapeño, stem + **seeds removed** (medium heat)  
- ½–1 serrano optional, seeds out — or skip for milder  
- 2 garlic cloves  
- ¼ onion  
- ½ cup packed cilantro  
- 1 tsp chicken bouillon  
- ½ tsp sugar  
- ~1¼ cups reserved pork broth  
- Salt to taste  

**Method:**
1. Pork + water + onion/garlic/bay/salt/pepper. Boil, skim foam. Medium simmer covered ~1 hour until tender. **Reserve 1½ cups broth.** When liquid is low, raise heat and brown pork lightly.
2. Meanwhile: dry skillet or foil-lined skillet — roast tomatillos, chiles, garlic, onion until blistered/soft, turning often.
3. Blend roasted veg + cilantro + bouillon + sugar + 1 cup pork broth until smooth.
4. Pour salsa over pork; rinse blender with remaining ½ cup broth into pot. Simmer medium-low covered **20 min**. Salt to taste.

**Storage:** Fridge 4 days. Freezes 3 months. Better next day.

### Scaling notes
- ~0.7× of original 2 lb/6 serv. Heat controlled by seeding chiles.

## 🍗 DISH 3: Chicken Tinga (Tinga de Pollo)

**Source:** https://muybuenoblog.com/chicken-tinga/  
**Why:** Yvette Marquez — 4.74★ / 26 ratings; cookbook recipe. Stovetop. Smoky chipotle-tomato. Meal-prep/freezer star. **Medium heat:** use **half** the chipotle can.

**Original servings:** 12 | **Scaled to:** 4 portions

### Per-portion macros (chicken + sauce only; no tostada toppings)
| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | ~280 kcal |
| Protein | 40g |
| Carbs | 8g |
| Fat | 10g |

### Scaled batch (4 portions)

- 700g boneless skinless chicken breast (or thighs)  
- Water to cover (~3–4 cups)  
- 1½ tsp salt (divided)  
- 1 garlic clove + ½ onion (for poach)  
- ½ large tomato (for blender)  
- **Half of a 200g can chipotles in adobo** (~3–4 peppers + some sauce) — **not whole can**  
- 2 Tbsp oil  
- ½ large onion, chopped  
- 2 tomatoes, chopped  
- 1 garlic clove, chopped  

**Method:**
1. Poach chicken with water, ¾ tsp salt, garlic, onion quarter ~25–30 min. Reserve broth. Shred chicken.
2. Blend: poached onion + garlic + tomato quarter + chipotles (half can) + ¾ tsp salt + broth to half-blender. Puree smooth.
3. Oil in pan: chopped onion 2 min; chopped tomato + garlic 2 min.
4. Add chicken + sauce (+ splash broth if dry). Simmer 10 min. Taste — add more chipotle only if you want more heat.

**Storage:** Fridge 5 days. Freezes 3 months. Excellent day 2.

### Scaling notes
- Original 2 lb / 12 serv → 700g / 4 (~generous plates). Chipotle halved for medium heat per author tip.

## 🥒 DISH 4: Calabacitas (Zucchini, Corn & Peppers)

**Source:** https://www.averiecooks.com/skillet-zucchini-corn-and-peppers-calabacitas/  
**Why:** 4.77★ / 59 ratings. 15-min skillet. Vegan, nut-free. Perfect quick veg side.

**Original servings:** 6 | **Scaled to:** 6 side portions

### Per-portion macros
| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | ~120 kcal |
| Protein | 3g |
| Carbs | 14g |
| Fat | 7g |

### Scaled batch (6 sides) — essentially 1× original

- 3–4 Tbsp oil  
- 1 large onion, diced  
- 1 red + 1 orange (or green) bell pepper, diced  
- 2 large zucchini, diced  
- 1½ cups corn (frozen thawed or fresh)  
- 1 can (110g) diced green chiles  
- 2 tsp cumin, 1 tsp oregano, 1 tsp salt, ½ tsp pepper  
- Pinch cayenne optional

**Method:** Oil + onion + peppers 5 min → zucchini, corn, chiles, spices 5 min → tomatoes + lime → taste. Done.

**Storage:** Fridge 4–5 days. Freezes OK but softer.

## 🫘 DISH 5: Frijoles de la Olla (Pot Beans)

**Source:** https://www.muydelish.com/frijoles-de-la-olla/  
**Why:** Ana Frias — 5★ / 18 ratings. Classic stovetop pot beans. Base for the week; nut-free.

**Original:** 8 cups | **We make:** full batch (~8 cups → 6 generous side scoops + broth)

### Per-portion macros (~¾ cup beans + broth)
| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | ~110 kcal |
| Protein | 7g |
| Carbs | 18g |
| Fat | 1g |
| Fibre | 6g |

### Batch

- 2 cups dried pinto beans (or black)  
- 6 cups water (plus boiling water as needed)  
- ¼ white onion  
- 1 garlic clove  
- ½–1 tsp salt (add late)  

**Method:**
1. Rinse; soak 3 hr–overnight covered by 5 cm water. Drain, rinse.
2. Pot: beans + 6 cups water + onion + garlic. Boil → low simmer covered 1½ hr.
3. Add salt; cook ~30 min more until creamy-tender (~2 hr total). Top up with **boiling** water only if needed.
4. Taste; salt again.

**Storage:** Fridge 1 week. Freezes 3 months with broth.

## 🍚 CARB: Arroz Rojo (Mexican Red Rice)

**Source:** https://mexicanmademeatless.com/easy-arroz-rojo-arroz-mexicano/  
**Why:** Fail-proof stovetop method (15+ years). Long-grain + tomato blend. We use **chicken broth** (household is omnivore).

**Makes:** 12 side portions (triple the 1-cup dry rice base)

### Per portion (~½ cup cooked)
| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | ~160 kcal |
| Protein | 3g |
| Carbs | 28g |
| Fat | 4g |

### Scaled batch (3×)

- 6 small Roma tomatoes (or 3¾ cups plain tomato sauce shortcut)  
- 1½ small white onions  
- 6 garlic cloves  
- ~¾ cup water for blending  
- 1½ tsp salt  
- 6 Tbsp oil  
- **3 cups long-grain white rice** (not jasmine/basmati)  
- **4½ cups chicken broth**  

**Method:**
1. Blend tomato + onion + garlic + water + salt smooth. Measure **3¾ cups** sauce.
2. Large pot medium heat: oil + rice, toast to light gold, stirring.
3. Add tomato sauce carefully (splatters); simmer 5 min thickening.
4. Add broth; gentle boil → **lowest heat, tight lid, 15–20 min**. Do not peek often.
5. Check liquid absorbed; off heat, fluff with fork; rest covered 10 min.

**Storage:** Fridge 4–5 days. Freeze 3 months. Reheat with splash of water.
