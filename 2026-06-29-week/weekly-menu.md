## 🐷 DISH 1: Cochinita Pibil (Slow-Cooker Yucatán Pork)

**Source:** https://www.rickbayless.com/recipe/slow-cooked-achiote-pork/
**Why this recipe:** Rick Bayless is the foremost authority on Mexican cooking in the English-speaking world — chef-owner of Frontera Grill and Topolobampo, James Beard Award winner, and host of 12 seasons of *Mexico: One Plate at a Time* on PBS. This is his everyday slow-cooker adaptation of cochinita pibil, designed specifically for home cooks. It uses prepared achiote seasoning and a slow cooker (which we have). The banana leaves are optional. Naturally GF. The 6-hour slow cook produces fall-apart pork that gets better with time — ideal for the 6-portion dish that stretches to Saturday.

**Original servings:** 6 | **Scaled to:** 6 portions at 70% size (use original recipe as-is)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (meat only, no rice/veg/sides)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 290 kcal |
| Protein | 22g (30%) |
| Carbs | 3g (4%) |
| Fat | 21g (66%) |
| Fibre | 1g |

*Includes ~140g cooked pork shoulder + achiote marinade. Pork shoulder is inherently fatty — this is the highest-fat meat dish. Protein is solid at 22g per 70% portion.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
Pork shoulder (~140g cooked, trimmed of excess fat): 280 cal / 22g P / 0g C / 21g F. Achiote marinade (70% of full portion): 10 cal / 0g P / 3g C / 0g F. Total ≈ 290 cal.

### Batch Recipe (Makes 6 portions at 70% size — use original recipe as-is)

**Ingredients:**
- 1.4 kg (3 lbs) bone-in pork shoulder roast
- 1/2 of a 3.5-oz (100g) package prepared achiote seasoning *(check GF — see notes)*
- 1/2 cup lime juice (about 4–5 limes)
- 2 tsp salt
- 1 large white onion, sliced 1/4-inch thick
- 1 1/2 large red onions, thinly sliced (for pickled onion)
- Banana leaves, defrosted if frozen (optional)
- About 1/3 cup roasted-chile salsa or habanero hot sauce (Yucateco or Frontera brand)

**Method:**
1. Place the achiote seasoning in a small bowl. Pour in 1/2 cup of the lime juice and 2 tsp salt. Work into a smooth, thick marinade with the back of a spoon.
2. If using banana leaves, cut 2 two-foot sections and use them to line the slow cooker — lay one down the length, the other across the width. Lay in the pork and pour the marinade over and around the roast. Scatter the white onion over the meat.
3. Pour 1/2 cup water around the meat. Fold up the banana leaves to roughly cover everything (or just put the lid on if not using leaves). Slow-cook on HIGH for 6 hours until the meat is fall-off-the-bone tender.
4. While the meat cooks, combine the 1 1/2 red onions with the remaining lime juice in a small bowl. Sprinkle with 1/2 tsp salt, toss, and set aside to marinate (stir occasionally).
5. Transfer the meat and onions to a cutting board. Spoon off any rendered fat from the juices. If there's a lot of broth (2+ cups), boil it down to about 1 cup in a saucepan. Season with salt to taste.
6. Shred the pork with two forks. Spoon the reduced sauce over the meat. Top with pickled red onions. Serve with arroz rojo, slaw, and salsa/hot sauce.

**Storage:** Keeps 5 days refrigerated. Freezes well up to 3 months. Reheat gently on stovetop or microwave. The Saturday portion for the assistant holds fine to day 6 refrigerated — this is why cochinita gets the 6-portion allocation.

### Scaling Notes
- Original recipe makes 6 full servings. We use it as-is for 6 portions at 70% size — each portion is simply a smaller serving. The cook divides the shredded pork into 6 equal portions.
- **Slow cooker note:** Rick Bayless specifies HIGH for 6 hours. The dish can hold on "keep warm" for 4 more hours. No slow cooker? Braise in a Dutch oven at 300°F (150°C) for 2.5–3 hours.

Chicken thigh, skinless (~130g cooked per portion, from ~500g raw): 215 cal / 26g P / 0g C / 12g F. Chipotle-tomato sauce (onion, garlic, tomato, chipotle, parsley, oil — per portion): 115 cal / 1g P / 7g C / 8g F. Total ≈ 330 cal.

### Batch Recipe (Makes 4 portions at 70% size, 0.67× original)

**Ingredients:**
- 500g boneless, skinless chicken thighs (about 3 large thighs)
- 1 1/2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1/2 medium white onion, sliced
- 1 large garlic clove, finely chopped
- 2 cups tomato, cubed (about 3 medium tomatoes)
- 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped (plus extra for garnish)
- 2 chipotle peppers in adobo, chopped *(check GF — see notes)*
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Water for poaching chicken

**Method:**
1. **Cook the chicken:** Place chicken thighs in a pot with water to cover, 1/2 tsp salt, and a piece of onion. Bring to a boil, reduce to simmer, and cook 20 minutes until tender. Remove chicken, let cool, and shred with two forks. Reserve 1/2 cup cooking liquid. *(Or use slow cooker: cook on HIGH 3 hours with water to cover.)*
2. **Make the sauce:** Heat oil in a large frying pan over medium heat. Add the sliced onion and stir-fry 3 minutes. Add the garlic and cook 2 more minutes until fragrant.
3. Add the cubed tomato and parsley. Lower heat and cook 6–7 minutes until tomatoes release their juices. If tomatoes aren't juicy enough, add 2 tbsp of the reserved chicken cooking liquid.
4. Add the shredded chicken and chopped chipotle peppers. Simmer 8 minutes until all flavours blend and the sauce thickens slightly. Season with salt and pepper.
5. Serve over arroz rojo with sauce spooned over. Garnish with chopped parsley.

**Storage:** Keeps 4 days refrigerated. Freezes well for 1 month. Reheat stovetop or microwave.

### Scaling Notes
- Scaled at 0.67× from original (6 full servings → 4 portions at 70% size). 500g raw boneless skinless chicken thighs yield ~2 cups shredded. 2 chipotles for medium spice — use 1 for mild, 3 for hot.
- ⚠️ **GF NOTE:** Most chipotles in adobo are naturally GF, but some brands add wheat. La Costeña (common in Mexico) is generally GF — always check the label.
- **Chicken alternative:** Boneless skinless chicken breast works too (slightly less fat, same protein). Adjust cooking time to 15 minutes poaching.

## 🐮 DISH 3: Chile Colorado de Res (Beef in Red Chile Sauce)

**Source:** https://www.isabeleats.com/chile-colorado-recipe/
**Why this recipe:** Isabel Orozco-Moore is a first-generation Mexican American who runs Isabel Eats, one of the most popular Mexican recipe sites. This chile colorado has 4.79 stars from 344 votes — one of the highest-rated Mexican beef recipes online. It's a family recipe: beef chuck simmered in a red chile sauce made from dried guajillo, ancho, and árbol chiles, blended with onion, garlic, cumin, and oregano. The recipe includes a GF substitution note (use tapioca starch or cornstarch instead of flour). Slow-cooker friendly. Holds 5+ days, freezes beautifully — ideal for the 6-portion dish.

**Original servings:** 6 | **Scaled to:** 6 portions at 70% size (use original recipe as-is, with GF substitution)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (meat only, no rice/veg)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 328 kcal |
| Protein | 37g (45%) |
| Carbs | 15g (18%) |
| Fat | 13g (36%) |
| Fibre | 5g |

*Includes ~130g cooked beef chuck + red chile sauce. This is the highest-protein meat dish per portion — beef chuck is protein-dense. The chile sauce adds complex carbs from dried chiles with minimal fat.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
Beef chuck, braised (~130g cooked, trimmed): 280 cal / 35g P / 0g C / 15g F. Red chile sauce (guajillo, ancho, árbol, onion, garlic, cumin, oregano, cornstarch, beef broth — per portion): 48 cal / 2g P / 15g C / 0.5g F. Total ≈ 328 cal. *(Recipe's own nutrition data: 328 cal / 37g P / 15g C / 13g F per serving — consistent.)*

### Batch Recipe (Makes 6 portions at 70% size — use original recipe as-is, with GF substitution)

**Ingredients:**
- 8 dried guajillo chiles, stemmed and seeded
- 2 dried ancho chiles, stemmed and seeded
- 1 dried árbol chile, stemmed *(keep seeds for heat, or remove for mild)*
- 1/2 yellow onion
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt, divided, plus more to taste
- 1 clove garlic
- 1/2 tsp ground cumin
- 2 lbs (900g) beef stew meat (chuck roast), cut into 1/2-inch chunks
- 2 tbsp cornstarch *(GF substitution for all-purpose flour)*
- 1/4 tsp freshly ground black pepper
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 1/2 cups beef broth *(check GF — see notes)*
- 1 bay leaf

**Method:**
1. **Make the red chile sauce:** Add the guajillo chiles, ancho chiles, árbol chile, and onion to a medium pot. Cover with water until completely submerged. Bring to a boil over high heat. Remove from heat, cover, and let sit 20 minutes to soften the chiles. *(Don't let it boil continuously — it makes the chles bitter.)*
2. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the softened chiles and onion to a blender. Add 1 cup of the chile-soaking water, the oregano, 1 tsp salt, garlic, and cumin. Blend until smooth. Add some beef broth if the mixture is too thick. Set aside.
3. **Coat the beef:** Toss the beef chunks with cornstarch, the remaining 1/2 tsp salt, and black pepper in a large bowl.
4. **Brown the meat:** Heat oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add half the meat and brown on all sides, about 5 minutes. Transfer to a bowl. Repeat with the remaining meat.
5. **Simmer:** Return all the meat to the pot. Add the blended red chile sauce, beef broth, and bay leaf. Stir to combine. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cover and cook 45 minutes until the meat is tender.
6. **Reduce:** Uncover, taste, and add more salt if needed. Raise heat to medium and cook 15 more minutes until the sauce reduces and thickens slightly. If you prefer a thicker sauce, cook longer.
7. Discard the bay leaf. Serve over arroz rojo with sauce spooned over.

**Storage:** Keeps 5 days refrigerated — gets better each day. Freezes 3 months. Reheat stovetop on medium (cover the pot — chile splatters and stains!) or microwave.

### Scaling Notes
- Original recipe makes 6 servings. We use it as-is for 6 portions at 70% size. 2 lbs beef chuck yields ~750g cooked meat = ~125g per portion.
- ⚠️ **GF SUBSTITUTION:** The original recipe uses all-purpose flour to coat the beef. We substitute cornstarch — this is the only change. The recipe author explicitly notes this GF substitution.
- **Slow cooker option:** Make the red chile sauce and sear the beef as directed, then add everything to the slow cooker. Cook on HIGH for 4–5 hours or LOW for 6–8 hours. The beef will be more fall-apart tender.
- **Spice level:** With 1 árbol chile (seeds in), this is medium-hot. For milder, remove the seeds from the árbol or omit it entirely. For hotter, add 2–3 árbol chiles.
- **Beef broth:** Most commercial beef broths are GF, but some bouillon cubes contain wheat. Check the label. Better Than Bouillon (GF-labeled) is a good option.

## 🫘 DISH 4: Frijoles Charros (Mexican Cowboy Beans)

**Source:** https://www.mexicoinmykitchen.com/charro-beans-soup-recipe/
**Why this recipe:** Mexico In My Kitchen is run by Mely Martínez, author of *The Mexican Home Kitchen* cookbook. This recipe has 4.72 stars from 32 votes. Mely is from northern Mexico where charro beans originated. The recipe uses pinto beans cooked with chorizo, bacon, tomatoes, onions, garlic, and serrano peppers — a hearty, brothy bean dish that's naturally GF. Beans hold excellently for batch cooking and the flavours deepen over time. The slow cooker can be used for the bean-cooking step.

**Original servings:** 8 | **Scaled to:** 8 portions at 70% size (0.7× original)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (veg only, no rice/meat)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 275 kcal |
| Protein | 13g (19%) |
| Carbs | 26g (38%) |
| Fat | 13g (43%) |
| Fibre | 10g |

*Includes ~1/2 cup beans with broth, 20g chorizo, 10g bacon, vegetables. This is a carb-heavy bean dish — the chorizo and bacon add flavour and fat. High fibre (10g) makes it very filling.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
Pinto beans (1/2 cup cooked with broth): 120 cal / 8g P / 22g C / 0.5g F. Chorizo (20g): 80 cal / 2.5g P / 0g C / 7g F. Bacon (10g): 55 cal / 2g P / 0g C / 5g F. Tomato/onion/garlic/cilantro: 20 cal / 0.5g P / 4g C / 0g F. Total ≈ 275 cal.

### Batch Recipe (Makes 8 portions at 70% size, 0.7× original)

**Ingredients:**
- 1 1/2 cups dry pinto beans (or 3 cans / 425g each, drained and rinsed)
- 1/2 medium white onion (for cooking beans)
- 3 cloves garlic, peeled (for cooking beans)
- 5 cups water (if cooking from dry)
- 60g (2 oz) Mexican chorizo
- 30g (1 oz) bacon, chopped
- 1/2 medium white onion, chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, chopped
- 2 large tomatoes, chopped
- 2 serrano peppers, chopped (use 1 jalapeño for milder heat)
- 1/2 cup cilantro, chopped
- Salt to taste

**Method:**
1. **Cook the beans:** Place dry pinto beans, 1/2 onion, 3 garlic cloves, and 5 cups water in a large pot. Cook on stovetop over medium-high heat for ~2 hours until tender. *Or use slow cooker: cook on HIGH for 5–6 hours.* *Or use canned beans: skip to step 2, use 3 cans with their liquid + 1 cup water.*
2. Once beans are cooked, drain the cooking onion and garlic (discard them). Keep the beans and their broth. Set aside.
3. **Cook the meats:** In a skillet over medium heat, fry the bacon and chorizo together for 10 minutes, breaking up the chorizo.
4. Add the chopped onion, chopped garlic, and serrano peppers. Fry 2 more minutes.
5. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook, stirring occasionally, 8 minutes until the tomato releases its juices and forms a sauce.
6. Stir the meat-tomato mixture into the pot with the beans and broth. Simmer 15 minutes to blend flavours. Add water if needed — consistency should be brothy.
7. Add salt to taste. Stir in chopped cilantro just before serving.
8. Serve with arroz rojo and meat dish.

**Storage:** Keeps 5 days refrigerated — gets better each day. Freezes excellently up to 3 months. Reheat stovetop or microwave.

### Scaling Notes
- Scaled at 0.7× of original (8 full servings → 8 portions at 70% size). 1.5 cups dry beans yields ~4.5 cups cooked with broth = ~1/2 cup per portion. Chorizo and bacon scaled to 60g and 30g respectively — enough for flavour without overwhelming the beans.
- **Slow cooker option:** The beans can cook in the slow cooker on HIGH for 5–6 hours (or LOW for 7–8 hours). This frees up the stovetop for other dishes.
- ⚠️ **PROTEIN NOTE:** This dish is carb-heavy (26g carbs, 19% protein). When paired with a meat dish, the combined meal protein is adequate. The chile colorado + frijoles pairing is the strongest at 30% protein.

## 🥗 SIDE: Cilantro-Lime Cabbage Slaw

**Source:** https://www.holajalapeno.com/cilantro-lime-cole-slaw-recipe/
**Why this recipe:** Also from Kate Ramos at ¡Hola! Jalapeño. This is a mayonnaise-free, vegan, GF slaw with cabbage, carrots, red onion, cilantro, and a lime-olive oil dressing. It's specifically designed as a topping for cochinita pibil — perfect for this week. Keeps its crunch for 3–4 days refrigerated.

**Original servings:** 6 | **Scaled to:** 6 servings (for the 6 cochinita portions)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 50 kcal |
| Protein | 1g |
| Carbs | 6g |
| Fat | 3g |

### Batch Recipe (Makes 6 servings, 1.5× original)

**Ingredients:**
- 1/2 head red cabbage, thinly sliced
- 1/2 head green cabbage, thinly sliced
- 1 large carrot, julienned or grated
- 1/3 red onion, thinly sliced
- 4 tbsp chopped cilantro

**Dressing:**
- 3 tbsp freshly squeezed lime juice
- 1 tbsp rice wine vinegar (or apple cider vinegar)
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- 1/4 tsp ground cumin
- 4 tbsp olive oil

**Method:**
1. Combine all vegetables in a large bowl and toss.
2. In a small bowl, whisk lime juice, vinegar, salt, pepper, and cumin until salt dissolves. Slowly drizzle in oil while whisking continuously.
3. Drizzle dressing over vegetables and toss to coat. Taste and adjust salt/lime.
4. **Make ahead:** Prep vegetables and dressing separately. Toss together up to 1 hour before serving (slaw loses crunch after a few hours in dressing).

**Storage:** Undressed slaw keeps 3–4 days refrigerated in an airtight container. Dressed slaw is best within 1–2 hours.

## 🍚 ARROZ ROJO (Mexican Red Rice) — Cook Once, Eat All Week

**Source:** https://www.rickbayless.com/recipe/red-tomato-rice/

### Batch Recipe (Makes ~6 cups cooked — use as-is)

**Ingredients:**
- 1 15-oz can diced tomatoes (fire-roasted preferred), lightly drained
- 1/2 small white onion, roughly chopped
- 2 garlic cloves, peeled and halved
- 1 1/2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 1/2 cups white rice (medium-grain preferred, long-grain fine)
- 1 3/4 cups chicken broth
- 3 serrano chiles (or 2 jalapeños), slit lengthwise
- 1/4 cup coarsely chopped fresh parsley
- 3/4 tsp salt (if using salted broth) or 1 1/2 tsp (if using unsalted broth/water)

**Method:**
1. **Make the tomato puree:** In a blender, combine the drained tomatoes, onion, and garlic. Blend to a smooth puree.
2. **Fry the rice:** In a medium (3-quart) saucepan with a tight-fitting lid, heat the oil over medium. Add the raw rice and stir frequently until the kernels turn from translucent to milky white, 5–6 minutes. Some light browning is fine.
3. Add the tomato puree and stir. Cook 2–3 minutes until it reduces and looks somewhat dry.
4. **Simmer:** Add the chicken broth, bring to a full boil, then add the slit chiles, parsley, and salt. Stir thoroughly, scraping down any rice kernels clinging to the sides.
5. Cover and cook over the lowest heat for 20 minutes. Do NOT lift the lid or stir during cooking.
6. Remove from heat. Use a fork to gently fluff the rice and release the steam. Re-cover and let stand 5 minutes for the starch to firm.
7. Remove the chiles (or leave as decoration). Yields ~6 cups cooked rice.

**Storage:** Keeps 4–5 days refrigerated. Reheat in microwave with a sprinkle of water. Do not freeze — texture degrades.

### Scaling Notes
- Recipe makes 6 cups. We need 5.3 cups (16 × 1/3 cup). The extra 2/3 cup is buffer.
- **Key technique:** Do NOT stir the rice while it simmers. Stirring releases starch and makes it gummy. Fluff only with a fork after cooking.
- **Pot size:** Use a wide pot (about 8 inches diameter) for even cooking.

### GF Watch Points
- **Achiote seasoning (recado rojo):** Some commercial brands cut with wheat flour. Check the label — common GF brands include El Yucateco and Mexicali. If unsure, make your own: blend 3 tbsp annatto seeds, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp coriander, 1 tsp oregano, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp allspice, 1/2 tsp black pepper.
- **Chipotles in adobo:** Most are naturally GF, but some brands add wheat. La Costeña (common in Mexico) is generally GF — always check the label.
- **Beef broth:** Most commercial beef broths are GF, but some bouillon cubes contain wheat. Check the label. Better Than Bouillon (GF-labeled) is a good option.
- **Chorizo:** Some commercial chorizos use bread crumbs as filler. Check the label or buy from a carnicería (butcher) that makes it fresh.
- **Cornstarch:** Naturally GF — this replaces the all-purpose flour in the chile colorado recipe.
- **Canned diced tomatoes:** Check label for added wheat — most are GF but verify.

### Freshness Schedule
- **Eat early (Mon–Wed):** Calabacitas con Elote. Zucchini gets soft after 3 days. Cooked Sunday, good through Wednesday. For Thursday/Friday, either cook fresh those days (20 minutes) or prep the chopped vegetables on Sunday and cook fresh each day.
- **Peak on day 2–3:** Chile Colorado and Frijoles Charros — both improve as flavours meld.
- **Steady all week:** Cochinita Pibil — holds well for 6 days (the reason it gets the Saturday portion).
- **Saturday (assistant only):** Cochinita + Calabacitas — cochinita holds fine to day 6. Cook the calabacitas fresh on Saturday (20 minutes).
- **Slaw:** Prep vegetables and dressing separately. Dress up to 1 hour before eating. Undressed vegetables keep 3–4 days.

### Cooking Order (Sunday Batch Cook)
1. **Start beans first** (if using dry): Slow cooker on HIGH, 5–6 hours. Or soak overnight and cook on stovetop.
2. **Start cochinita in slow cooker** (if not using slow cooker for beans): 6 hours on HIGH. If beans are in the slow cooker, cook cochinita in a Dutch oven at 300°F (150°C) for 2.5–3 hours.
3. **Make chile colorado red chile sauce:** Soak chiles 20 minutes, blend. Can be done while other dishes cook.
4. **Brown and simmer chile colorado beef:** 1 hour simmer. Can use slow cooker (4–5 hours HIGH) if stovetop is busy.
5. **Poach chicken for tinga:** 20 minutes. Shred and set aside.
6. **Make arroz rojo**: 25 minutes. Can be done while other dishes simmer.
7. **Make frijoles charros**: 30 minutes once beans are cooked.
8. **Make tinga**: 20 minutes once chicken is poached.
9. **Make calabacitas**: 20 minutes — do this last so it's the freshest. Or prep vegetables and cook fresh on Monday.
10. **Make slaw**: 15 minutes — prep vegetables and dressing separately, store in fridge.
11. **Pickle red onions**: 15 minutes — done while cochinita cooks.
