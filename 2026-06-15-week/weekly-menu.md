## 🍗 DISH 1: Butter Chicken (Murgh Makhani)

**Source:** https://www.recipetineats.com/butter-chicken/
**Why this recipe:** Nagi (RecipeTin Eats) is one of the most trusted recipe developers online. This specific recipe has 4.96 stars from 1,093 reviews — an extraordinary level of validation. It's a chef recipe (adapted from Australian chef Luke Mangan), uses supermarket-available ingredients, and is naturally gluten-free. The yogurt marinade + cream sauce is exactly the saucy, bold profile the customer wants.

**Original servings:** 3–4 | **Scaled to:** 2 generous portions

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 635 kcal |
| Protein | 42g (26%) |
| Carbs | 55g (35%) |
| Fat | 27g (38%) |
| Fibre | 2g |

*Includes 1 cup (150g cooked) basmati rice. Calories from curry: ~435; from rice: ~200.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
Chicken thighs (250g cooked per portion): 520 cal / 60g P / 0g C / 28g F. Sauce (cream, passata, butter, yogurt): ~175 cal / 3g P / 10g C / 15g F. Rice (150g cooked): 200 cal / 4g P / 45g C / 0.5g F. Total ≈ 635 cal with rice. Sauce calories reduced vs. original (originally ~400 cal for a 310g serving at 3-4 servings).

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 2 portions)

**Ingredients:**
- 500g boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-size pieces
- 1/3 cup plain full-fat yogurt
- 2 tsp lemon juice
- 2/3 tsp turmeric powder
- 1 1/3 tsp garam masala
- 1/4 tsp chilli powder (pure, or cayenne)
- 2/3 tsp ground cumin
- 2 tsp fresh ginger, grated
- 1 1/2 cloves garlic, crushed
- 1 1/2 tbsp ghee or butter
- 2/3 cup tomato passata (pureed tomatoes)
- 2/3 cup heavy cream
- 2 tsp sugar
- 3/4 tsp salt
- Fresh coriander/cilantro for garnish

**Method:**
1. Combine yogurt, lemon juice, turmeric, garam masala, chilli powder, cumin, ginger, and garlic in a bowl. Add chicken, coat well. Cover and marinate overnight (minimum 3 hours).
2. Heat ghee in a large skillet over high heat. Remove chicken from marinade (don't shake off excess) and cook ~3 minutes until white all over.
3. Add passata, cream, sugar, salt, and any remaining marinade from the bowl. Stir well.
4. Reduce heat to low and simmer 20 minutes until sauce thickens and deepens in colour.
5. Garnish with coriander. Serve over basmati rice (see rice instructions below).

**Storage:** Keeps 4–5 days refrigerated. Freezes well up to 3 months (sauce may separate slightly on thaw — stir while reheating). Reheat gently on stovetop or microwave.

### Scaling Notes
- Scaled at 0.67× from original (750g → 500g chicken for 2 portions).
- Spices scaled linearly at this modest ratio.
- Salt scaled at 0.6× (original 1.25 tsp for 3-4 servings → 0.75 tsp) — safer to under-salt and adjust.

## 🍗 DISH 2: Lamb Rogan Josh

**Source:** https://www.recipetineats.com/rogan-josh/
**Why this recipe:** Another RecipeTin Eats powerhouse — 4.97 stars from 253 ratings. Based on Rick Stein's India recipe with Nagi's refinements for more curry-house depth. All spices are supermarket-available (no Indian grocer required). Naturally GF. The 2-hour slow simmer produces fall-apart lamb in a rich, creamy tomato sauce — ideal batch cooking.

**Original servings:** 4 | **Scaled to:** 2 generous portions

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 670 kcal |
| Protein | 35g (21%) |
| Carbs | 63g (38%) |
| Fat | 28g (38%) |
| Fibre | 5g |

*Includes 1 cup basmati rice. Protein is moderate — this dish leans into the rich sauce and lamb fat for satiety. Paired on the same day as higher-protein dishes to balance.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
Lamb shoulder (200g cooked per portion, from ~375g raw with trimming): 360 cal / 31g P / 0g C / 26g F. Sauce (ghee, passata, yogurt, spices): ~110 cal / 2g P / 18g C / 5g F. Rice (150g cooked): 200 cal / 4g P / 45g C / 0.5g F. Note: original recipe nutrition lists 381 cal for 4 servings at 750g lamb. At 2 portions (half batch, 375g lamb), lamb per portion is similar but with more sauce concentration.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 2 portions)

**Ingredients:**
- 1 3/4 tbsp ghee (or butter)
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 3 green cardamom pods, lightly bruised
- 2 cloves
- 1/2 large onion, finely chopped
- 1 1/2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 1/2 tsp fresh ginger, grated
- 2 1/2 tbsp tomato passata
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 375g boneless lamb shoulder, cut into 3cm cubes
- 3/4 cup chicken stock (reduced salt)

**Spices (scaled):**
- 1 tbsp paprika
- 1/4 tsp chilli powder (or to taste)
- 2 tsp ground coriander
- 2 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 1/8 tsp nutmeg
- 1/2 tsp garam masala
- 1/4 tsp fennel powder

**Finishes:**
- 1/4 cup plain yogurt
- 1/4 tsp extra garam masala
- 1/4 tsp extra fennel powder

**Method:**
1. Melt ghee in a heavy-based pot over medium heat. Add cinnamon, cardamom, cloves — cook 1 minute.
2. Add onion, cook 7 minutes until golden. Add garlic and ginger, cook 1 minute.
3. Stir in all ground spices, toast 30 seconds.
4. Add tomato passata and salt, then stock. Stir, add lamb. Bring to a simmer.
5. Cover, reduce heat to low-medium, simmer gently 1 hour 45 minutes. Stir occasionally.
6. Remove lid, simmer 15 minutes more to reduce sauce. Lamb should be fork-tender.
7. Stir in yogurt, extra garam masala, and extra fennel. Cook 2 more minutes.
8. Serve over basmati rice, garnish with coriander.

**Storage:** Keeps 5 days refrigerated, even better on day 2. Freezes 3 months. Reheat stovetop or microwave.

### Scaling Notes
- Scaled at 0.5× from original (750g → 375g lamb for 2 portions).
- Whole spices (cinnamon, cardamom, cloves): 0.5× is fine — whole spices don't over-concentrate.
- Ground spices: 0.5× linear scaling is appropriate at this ratio.
- Simmer time remains the same (smaller volume doesn't cook faster for a braise).

## 🍗 DISH 3: Goan Fish Curry

**Source:** https://www.carolinescooking.com/goan-fish-curry/
**Why this recipe:** 4.85 stars from 32 ratings with consistent rave reviews. Caroline's Cooking is a well-established food blog. This recipe makes its own masala from whole toasted spices — no pre-made curry powder — giving authentic Goan depth. Coconut-tamarind base is naturally GF, tangy, and creamy. Quick: 20 minutes total, good for the fresher dish in the rotation.

**Original servings:** 2 | **Scaled to:** 2 portions (as-is)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 640 kcal |
| Protein | 42g (26%) |
| Carbs | 56g (35%) |
| Fat | 33g (46%) |
| Fibre | 4g |

*Includes 3/4 cup (115g cooked) basmati rice. Fish provides lean protein; coconut milk drives the fat content. Adjust rice up for more carbs if desired.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
White fish (225g haddock/cod per portion): 190 cal / 42g P / 0g C / 2g F. Coconut milk (1/2 cup per portion): 220 cal / 2g P / 4g C / 24g F. Oil, onion, tomato, tamarind, spices: ~80 cal / 1g P / 10g C / 3g F. Rice (115g cooked): 150 cal / 3g P / 34g C / 0.4g F. Total ≈ 640 cal.

### Batch Recipe (Makes 2 portions)

**Ingredients:**
**Spice paste (masala):**
- 2 tsp coriander seeds
- 1 tsp cumin seeds
- 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
- 2–3 cloves garlic
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, chopped
- 1/4 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
- 1/2 tsp paprika
- 1/2 tsp turmeric

**Curry:**
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil
- 2 green chilis (serrano or similar), finely diced
- 1 onion, finely diced
- 1 tomato, diced
- 450g (1 lb) firm white fish (haddock, cod, or local替代 like huachinango/red snapper), cut into large chunks
- 2 tsp tamarind concentrate
- 1 cup coconut milk (full-fat, unsweetened)

**Method:**
1. Toast coriander, cumin, and mustard seeds in a dry skillet over medium heat ~2 minutes until fragrant (mustard seeds may pop).
2. Crush garlic and ginger with salt to a paste (mortar & pestle or mini chopper). Add toasted seeds, crush. Mix in cayenne, paprika, turmeric to form a rough paste.
3. Heat oil in a medium skillet over medium heat. Sauté onion 3–4 minutes until softened.
4. Add spice paste, cook 1–2 minutes. Add tomato and chilis, cook until liquid evaporates (~1 minute).
5. Add tamarind and coconut milk, stir to combine.
6. Add fish chunks, cook 2–3 minutes until just cooked through.
7. Serve over basmati rice.

**Storage:** Best eaten within 2–3 days (fish doesn't hold as long as land meats). Place this dish on Mon/Tue/Wed. Can freeze the sauce base (without fish) and add fresh fish when reheating. Reheat gently — fish breaks up easily.

### Scaling Notes
- Recipe is already 2 servings — no scaling needed.
- ⚠️ **Fish freshness:** For a 5-day week, the fish curry should be eaten Mon/Tue. It's placed on Tue (dinner) and Fri (lunch) — the Fri portion should be frozen or the fish cooked fresh into pre-made sauce on Thu night.

## 🥬 DISH 4: Chana Masala (Chickpea Curry)

**Source:** https://ministryofcurry.com/chana-masala/
**Why this recipe:** Ministry of Curry is run by Archana Mundhe, an Indian home cook teaching authentic techniques. This recipe has 4.58 stars from 26 ratings with strong user feedback ("tastes like my favourite Indian restaurant"). Naturally GF and vegan-friendly. The homemade spice blend (or garam masala shortcut) creates deep, earthy flavour. Chickpeas hold up excellently for batch cooking.

**Original servings:** 4 | **Scaled to:** 2 generous portions (with a boiled egg or yogurt side for protein boost)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 650 kcal |
| Protein | 28g (17%) |
| Carbs | 100g (62%) |
| Fat | 16g (22%) |
| Fibre | 12g |

*Includes 1.5 cups basmati rice and 1 side serving (170g Greek yogurt or 2 boiled eggs). Without the side, macros are: 530 cal / 15g P / 92g C / 12g F. The side is essential for protein balance.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
Chickpeas (1 cup cooked per portion): 269 cal / 15g P / 45g C / 4g F. Onion, tomato, ghee, spices: ~80 cal / 2g P / 10g C / 5g F. Rice (225g cooked, 1.5 cups): 300 cal / 6g P / 68g C / 0.7g F. Greek yogurt (170g): 100 cal / 17g P / 6g C / 0.7g F. *Alternative: 2 boiled eggs add 140 cal / 12g P / 1g C / 10g F.*

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 2 generous portions)

**Ingredients:**
- 1 (15 oz / 425g) can chickpeas, drained and rinsed *(or 1/2 cup dry, soaked and cooked)*
- 1 tbsp ghee (or oil for vegan)
- 1 bay leaf
- 1/2 large yellow onion, finely diced
- 1 tsp ginger paste
- 1 tsp garlic paste
- 1/2 green chili, slit (optional)
- 1 plum tomato, finely diced
- 1 1/2 tsp chana masala spice blend *(or simplify: 1/2 tsp garam masala + 1/2 tsp coriander powder + 1/4 tsp red chili powder + pinch turmeric)*
- 3/4 tsp salt
- 1/8 tsp turmeric
- 1/4 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder (or paprika)
- 1/2 cup water
- 1/2 tbsp kasoori methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
- 2 tbsp chopped cilantro

**Method:**
1. Heat ghee in a skillet over medium heat. Add bay leaf and onion, sauté 8–10 minutes until golden.
2. Add ginger paste, garlic paste, green chili. Cook 1 minute.
3. Add tomato, cook 5 minutes until soft and mushy.
4. Stir in spice blend, salt, turmeric, chili powder. Cook 30 seconds.
5. Add drained chickpeas and water. Simmer 5–10 minutes.
6. Crush kasoori methi between palms, stir in. Garnish with cilantro.
7. Serve with rice and a side of Greek yogurt or boiled eggs.

**Storage:** Keeps 5 days refrigerated excellently — chickpeas get better as flavours meld. Freezes 2–3 months. Reheat stovetop or microwave.

### Scaling Notes
- Scaled at 0.5× from original (1 cup dry chickpeas → 1 can / 1/2 cup dry).
- Spices scaled linearly at 0.5×. Kasoori methi kept at 0.5 tbsp (generous) — it's a finishing herb, hard to overdo.
- ⚠️ **PROTEIN NOTE:** This dish is lower in protein (~15g without sides). The yogurt or egg side is not optional for an athlete — it brings protein to ~28g per meal.

## 🥬 DISH 5: Dal Makhani (Creamy Black Lentils & Kidney Beans)

**Source:** https://www.veganricha.com/dal-makhani-beans-in-creamy-buttery/
**Why this recipe:** Vegan Richa is a widely respected Indian food blogger with multiple published cookbooks. This recipe has 5 stars from 21 ratings and comes from her mother's home kitchen. It's naturally GF, uses almond + cashew milk for creaminess (dairy-free but can use real cream). The combination of urad dal and kidney beans makes it protein-rich for a vegetarian dish. Slow-simmered lentils are ideal batch cooking.

**Original servings:** 4 | **Scaled to:** 2 generous portions

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 630 kcal |
| Protein | 29g (18%) |
| Carbs | 82g (52%) |
| Fat | 24g (34%) |
| Fibre | 16g |

*Includes 1 cup basmati rice and 1 tbsp butter/ghee on top. Protein boosted by the bean+lentil combo (14g from legumes + 5g from rice + nuts/cream). Adding a boiled egg brings protein to ~35g.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
Black gram + kidney beans (1/4 cup dry each per portion, ~125g cooked total): 210 cal / 14g P / 37g C / 1g F. Almond/cashew milk, oil, onion, tomato: ~130 cal / 3g P / 7g C / 10g F. Butter finish (1 tbsp per portion): 100 cal / 0g P / 0g C / 12g F. Rice (150g cooked): 200 cal / 4g P / 45g C / 0.5g F. Total ≈ 640 cal. *If adding 1 boiled egg: +70 cal, +6g P.*

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 2 generous portions)

**Ingredients:**
- 1/2 cup whole urad dal (black gram) *— or substitute black lentils (beluga) if urad unavailable in Playa del Carmen; see note*
- 1/4 cup dried kidney beans (rajma)
- 2 cups water (for pressure cooking)
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/4 tsp red chili powder or cayenne

**Tempering:**
- 1 tsp oil
- 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
- 1 green chili (serrano), finely chopped
- 1/2 inch ginger, finely chopped
- 2–3 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 1/2 medium onion, chopped
- Pinch asafoetida (hing) — *⚠️ check: some hing is cut with wheat flour. Use GF-certified or skip.*
- 1/4 tsp turmeric
- 1 medium tomato, chopped
- 1/2 tsp garam masala
- 1/2 tbsp dried fenugreek leaves (kasoori methi)
- 1 tbsp almond milk (or dairy cream)
- 2–3 tbsp cashew cream (blend 2 tbsp cashews + 3 tbsp water)
- Vegan butter or ghee for serving

**Method:**
1. Soak urad dal and kidney beans overnight (8+ hours). Drain.
2. Pressure cook with 2 cups water, salt, and chili powder: 25 minutes at high pressure with natural release. *Or stovetop: simmer covered 60–70 minutes until very soft.*
3. In a pan, heat oil. Add cumin seeds, cook until fragrant.
4. Add ginger, garlic, green chili — sauté until golden.
5. Add onion, asafoetida — cook until translucent.
6. Add tomato, turmeric — cook until tomatoes are mushy (~5 min).
7. Add cooked dal/beans with their cooking water. Add garam masala, fenugreek, almond milk, cashew cream.
8. Simmer 30 minutes on low, mashing some beans against the pot. Add water if too thick.
9. Serve with a dollop of butter/ghee and rice.

**Storage:** Keeps 5 days refrigerated — gets creamier over time. Freezes excellently up to 3 months. Reheat stovetop or microwave, add a splash of water if thickened.

### Scaling Notes
- Scaled at 0.5× from original. Legumes and aromatics scale linearly.
- Asafoetida: the original calls for 1/8 tsp. At 0.5× that's a pinch. If using GF-certified hing, it's safe. If uncertain, skip — the garlic/ginger provide enough aromatic base.
- ⚠️ **URAD DAL AVAILABILITY:** Whole urad dal (black gram) may be hard to find in Playa del Carmen. Alternative: use black beluga lentils (more likely in health food sections) OR substitute 3/4 cup brown lentils + 1/4 cup kidney beans. Flavour will differ but still excellent.

## ⚠️ NOTES & CONSIDERATIONS

### GF Watch Points
- **Asafoetida (hing):** Many commercial hing powders are cut with wheat flour. Skip it entirely, or confirm a GF brand. The dal makhani works fine without it.
- **Garam masala:** Check your brand — some cheap blends add flour as filler. Most quality brands are GF.
- **Kasoori methi:** Pure dried fenugreek leaf, naturally GF.
- **Yogurt:** Plain yogurt is GF, but flavoured ones may have additives. Stick to plain full-fat Greek.

### Ingredient Availability in Playa del Carmen
- **Urad dal:** May require a trip to an Indian grocer or the international aisle at Walmart/Chedraui. Substitute black lentils (lentejas negras beluga) if unavailable — they're smaller and cook faster but give a similar earthy flavour.
- **Kasoori methi:** Hard to find locally. Can be omitted (the dal and chana masala will still be excellent) or ordered online. Epazote (a local Mexican herb) is NOT a substitute — different flavour entirely.
- **Tamarind concentrate:** Should be available at Chedraui or Walmart in the Asian/International section. If not, substitute 1 tbsp lime juice + 1/2 tsp brown sugar for the Goan fish curry — different but still good.
- **Fish:** Huachinango (red snapper) or mero (grouper) are excellent local substitutes for haddock/cod — both are firm white fish common in the Yucatán.
- **Passata:** Look for "puré de tomate" or just puree canned tomatoes yourself in a blender.

### Freshness Schedule
- **Eat early (Mon–Wed):** Goan Fish Curry. Fish doesn't hold as long. The Friday portion should be frozen right after cooking and thawed Thursday night.
- **Peak on day 2–3:** Lamb Rogan Josh and Dal Makhani — both improve as flavours meld.
- **Steady all week:** Butter Chicken, Chana Masala — both hold well for 5 days.

### Protein Boosts
- The vegetarian dishes (Chana Masala, Dal Makhani) are lower in protein. The yogurt/egg sides are built into the plan — don't skip them.
- If still hungry or needing more protein, add a second egg, extra Greek yogurt, or a protein shake on the side.

### Calories
- This menu averages ~650 kcal/meal. For a highly active 37M, this may be on the lower end. If you find yourself hungry, increase rice portions by 50% per meal (+100 cal) or add an extra tbsp of ghee/butter to any dish (+100 cal).
