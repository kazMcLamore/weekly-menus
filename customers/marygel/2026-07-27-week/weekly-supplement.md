### B) Ingredients that may be new

#### Dried guajillo / ancho (or New Mexico) chiles
**What:** Dried red peppers for chile colorado sauce — fruity, mild–medium, not the same as cayenne powder.  
**Where (Playa):** Any mercado, Chedraui spice aisle, Walmart.  
**Tip:** Remove seeds/stems. Toast briefly dry until fragrant — black = bitter.

#### Chipotles in adobo
**What:** Smoked jalapeños in tomato-vinegar sauce (can).  
**Where:** Everywhere in Mexico.  
**Heat:** Use **half the can** this week for medium heat. Freeze leftover peppers flat in a bag.

#### Tomatillos
**What:** Green husk tomatoes — tart base of chile verde.  
**Prep:** Remove papery husk; rinse sticky coating.  
**Char:** Dry hot skillet / comal — **no oven**.

#### Masa harina
**What:** Corn flour for tortillas/tamales — thickens colorado with corn flavor.  
**Sub:** Finely chopped corn tortilla simmered 15 min in the stew (author-approved).

#### Long-grain white rice
**Not** jasmine or basmati for arroz rojo — texture comes out wrong.

### C) Store route (Playa)

2. Walmart — chipotles, broth, frozen corn backup  
3. Skip DAC unless you want fancy oregano  

### D) Nut-allergy watch

- Wash hands/boards after any other kitchen nut use before cooking her food  

### E) No-oven reminder
- Tomatillos & tomato: **skillet/comal only**

## PART 2 — COOKING TIPS

### 1. Braising (chile colorado)
Slow simmer, liquid halfway up meat, gentle bubbles, lid on, until fork-tender (~1½ hr). Don’t hard-boil. If tough, more time not more heat.

### 2. Skillet-char (tomatillos / tomato)
Hot dry pan, turn until blistered soft. Same flavor goal as oven roast without an oven.

### 3. Toast rice for arroz rojo
Rice must go light golden in oil before tomato sauce — flavor + less stickiness. Then sauce, then broth, then **don’t lift the lid**.

### 4. Bean soak + late salt
Soak frijoles overnight. Add salt in the last stretch so skins don’t toughen early. Top up only with **boiling** water.

### 5. Chipotle heat control (tinga)
Author tip: half the chipotles = milder. You can always stir in more adobo at the end; you can’t remove heat.

### 6. Batch packing
Cool slightly before sealing. Label Mon–Fri. Freeze extra Colorado if needed.
