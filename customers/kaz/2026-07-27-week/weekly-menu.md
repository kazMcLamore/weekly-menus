## 🫙 PANTRY PREP FIRST: Gluten-Free Dark Soy Sauce

**Do this Tuesday night or Wednesday morning before any dish.** Every braise this week needs dark soy for mahogany color and mild molasses depth. Real Chinese dark soy (*lǎo chōu*) almost always contains wheat. Commercial GF dark soy is basically unavailable in Playa (Lee Kum Kee makes a foodservice jug only).

**Source base ratio:** [The Woks of Life — Dark Soy Sauce substitution](https://thewoksoflife.com/dark-soy-sauce/)  
Their mix per ~1 Tbsp dark soy: **2 tsp soy sauce + ½ tsp molasses + ⅛ tsp sugar** (use GF tamari).  
**Cooked method:** simmered batch adapted from common homemade dark-soy methods (Went Here 8 This simmer 3–5 min; Pepper Thai / Vilailuck Teigen caramel-sugar + soy approach) so the sauce thickens slightly and the sweetener dissolves fully — better than a cold stir-together.

### Batch recipe (makes ~½ cup / 120 ml — enough for the whole week + a little spare)

**Ingredients:**
- 80 ml (5 Tbsp + 1 tsp) **tamari** (GF soy sauce)
- 20 ml (4 tsp / 1 Tbsp + 1 tsp) **molasses** (melaza) — or very dark piloncillo syrup
- 1 tsp sugar (or grated piloncillo)
- 1 Tbsp water (optional, helps dissolve; will cook off)

**Method:**
1. Add tamari, molasses, sugar, and water to a **small saucepan**.
2. Stir over **medium heat** until the sugar/molasses fully dissolve and the mixture just reaches a gentle simmer (small bubbles at the edges).
3. Lower heat. Simmer **3–5 minutes**, stirring often, until slightly thicker and glossy — like thin syrup, not tar. Watch carefully: sugar burns fast and can boil over.
4. Taste: should be salty-savory with mild sweetness and deep brown color. If too thick, splash of water. If too thin, 1 more minute.
5. Cool completely. Pour into a clean glass jar. Label: **“Soya oscura GF” + date**.
6. **Fridge up to 1 month.** Shake before each use (it can settle).

**How much this week uses:**
| Dish | GF dark soy |
|------|-------------|
| Hong Shao Rou | 2½ Tbsp (37 ml) |
| Chinese Beef Stew | 1½ Tbsp (22 ml) |
| Three Cup Chicken | 2 Tbsp (30 ml) |
| Garlic Eggplant | 2½ tsp (12 ml) |
| **Total** | **~100 ml** (½ cup batch covers it) |

**Quick single-portion emergency** (if you run out mid-cook): stir 2 tsp tamari + ½ tsp molasses + ⅛ tsp sugar in a cup until dissolved — Woks of Life cold mix. Prefer the simmered jar when you can.

**⚠️ Do not use regular dark soy sauce** from Asian aisles unless the label is explicitly gluten-free (almost never is — wheat is standard).

## 🐷 DISH 1: Hong Shao Rou (Red-Braised Pork)

**Source:** https://omnivorescookbook.com/hong-shao-rou/  
**Cross-check:** https://thewoksoflife.com/shanghai-style-braised-pork-belly/ (4.86★ / 267 ratings)  
**Why this recipe:** Maggie Zhu (Omnivore’s Cookbook) — Beijing-raised, ATK Editor in Residence. 5★ from 41 ratings; commenters call it a “go-to special occasion recipe” made 10+ times. Explicitly meal-prep and freezer friendly. Recipe includes GF adaptation notes (tamari + dry sherry). Uses the classic caramel + braise method for that glossy sticky sauce. For Playa batch cooking we use **pork shoulder (espaldilla)** instead of pure belly — Maggie’s own comment confirms shoulder works and is less greasy; still gets the red-braise treatment.

**Original servings:** 4–6 | **Scaled to:** 8 portions

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (customer meat only)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 380 kcal |
| Protein | 32g (34%) |
| Carbs | 8g (8%) |
| Fat | 24g (57%) |
| Fibre | 0g |

**Portion size:** ~140g cooked pork + glaze

### Macro Calculation Summary
USDA pork shoulder cooked, roasted: ~290 kcal / 25g P / 0g C / 21g F per 100g. 140g cooked ≈ 406 kcal / 35g P / 29g F. Sugar caramel + tamari glaze add ~40 kcal carbs/sugar; fat renders down during 90-min braise → net ~380 kcal / 32g P / 8g C / 24g F. Cross-check vs Omnivore’s card (485 kcal/serving for fattier belly) — shoulder is leaner, so lower is correct.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 8 portions)

**Pork:**
- 1.8 kg pork shoulder (espaldilla / bondiola), cut into 2.5 cm cubes (leaner than belly; still well-marbled). Skin-on belly OK if you find a meaty slab — use same weight.

**Blanching:**
- Water to cover
- 3 green onions (cebolletas), cut into 5 cm pieces
- 4 slices fresh ginger (~2 cm piece)

**Caramel + braise:**
- 3 Tbsp neutral oil (aceite vegetal / canola)
- 5 Tbsp sugar (azúcar blanca or piloncillo grated)
- ⅔ cup dry sherry (jerez seco) — GF Shaoxing substitute
- ~3½ cups reserved blanching liquid (or water)
- 5 Tbsp tamari (GF soy sauce)
- 2½ Tbsp **GF Dark Soy** (from pantry prep jar above)
- 4 cm ginger, sliced
- 5 green onions, cut into 5 cm pieces
- 3 whole star anise pods (anis estrellado)
- Optional: 1 small cinnamon stick

**Method:**
1. **Blanch:** Put pork in a large pot, cover with cold water by 2 cm. Add blanching ginger + green onion. Bring to a full boil over high heat. Boil 5 minutes, skimming brown foam until liquid is clear. Strain pork; **reserve the blanching liquid**. Rinse pork under cold water; pat **very dry** with paper towels (critical — wet pork splatters in caramel).
2. **Caramel (炒糖色):** In a completely dry heavy pot or Dutch oven, add sugar + oil over **low** heat. Swirl the pot — do **not** stir with a spoon. Cook until sugar melts to light amber (3–5 min). Watch constantly — burnt sugar is bitter.
3. Carefully add dried pork pieces (in 2 batches if needed). Raise heat to medium-low; turn pork to coat in caramel, ~1 min per side.
4. Add dry sherry; scrape browned bits. Add reserved blanching liquid (enough to almost cover pork), tamari, dark-soy sub, ginger, green onions, star anise (± cinnamon).
5. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cover with lid slightly ajar. Simmer **1½ hours**, stirring 2–3 times. Pork should be fork-tender.
6. If sauce is still thin: uncover, raise heat to medium, reduce 10–15 min until glossy and thick enough to coat the meat. Stir so nothing burns. Remove star anise / aromatics before packing if preferred.

**Storage:** Fridge 4–5 days. **Freezes excellently** up to 1 month (Maggie confirms). Reheat: steam 15–20 min from thawed, or microwave covered (plate on top to catch oil splatter). Sauce thickens when cold — loosen with a splash of water when reheating.

### Scaling Notes
- Original ~1.5–2 lb for 4–6 servings → scaled to 1.8 kg for 8 portions (~225g raw → ~140g cooked after render).
- Sugar scaled at ~0.85× linear (5 Tbsp vs pure 1.6× of 3 Tbsp) — caramel sweetness concentrates; too much sugar makes it cloying.
- Tamari scaled cautiously; taste in last 15 min and adjust salt.
- Star anise: 3 pods for batch (not 1.6× of 2) — anise is potent and doesn’t need linear scale.
- **Pork cut:** Shoulder preferred for macros + Playa availability. If using belly, expect higher fat macros (~450+ kcal/portion).

## 🥩 DISH 2: Chinese Beef Stew with Potatoes (土豆炖牛肉)

**Source:** https://omnivorescookbook.com/chinese-beef-stew/  
**Why this recipe:** Maggie Zhu — 4.95★ from 18 ratings. Explicitly freezer and meal-prep friendly; multiple commenters froze postpartum portions successfully. Deep star-anise / cinnamon / soy braise — exactly the saucy profile wanted. Stovetop + Instant Pot methods included. GF adaptable with tamari + dry sherry. Potatoes and carrots braised *in* the beef broth = built-in veg volume without a third side.

**Original servings:** 6–8 | **Scaled to:** 9 portions

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (customer meat + stew veg)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 420 kcal |
| Protein | 38g (36%) |
| Carbs | 22g (21%) |
| Fat | 20g (43%) |
| Fibre | 3g |

**Portion size:** ~150g cooked beef + ~100g potato/carrot + ladle of broth

### Macro Calculation Summary
USDA beef chuck braised: ~250 kcal / 30g P / 0g C / 14g F per 100g cooked. 150g ≈ 375 / 45 / 21. Potato + carrot ~100g cooked: ~70 kcal / 2g P / 16g C. Braise liquid sugar/oil: ~30 kcal. Total ≈ 475 before accounting for collagen-rich cuts losing some fat to skim → ~420 kcal / 38g P / 22g C / 20g F. Recipe card nutrition looks undercounted (188 kcal) — that appears broken vs 2 lb chuck; USDA ingredient math used instead.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 9 portions)

**Beef:**
- 2.2 kg beef chuck roast (diezmillo / chuck), cut into 4 cm chunks

**Braise liquid & aromatics:**
- ⅓ cup dry sherry (jerez seco)
- ⅓ cup tamari
- 1½ Tbsp **GF Dark Soy** (from pantry prep jar)
- 1½ Tbsp sugar
- ¾ tsp coarsely ground black pepper
- ¾ tsp salt (adjust at end)
- 8 dried red chiles (chiles de árbol or similar — whole, for aroma not extreme heat)
- 1 thumb ginger (~5 cm), sliced
- 4 green onions, halved crosswise
- 5 bay leaves (laurel)
- 2 star anise pods
- 1 large cinnamon stick (or 2 small)

**Vegetables (add near end):**
- 900g waxy potatoes (papas cambray or white potatoes), peeled, cut into 5 cm chunks
- 2 large carrots, cut into 5 cm chunks

**Method (stovetop — primary):**
1. Put beef in a large Dutch oven / heavy pot. Cover with cold water by 2 cm. Bring to a gentle boil over medium-high; skim foam 10–15 min until broth is relatively clear.
2. Add dry sherry, dried chiles, ginger, green onions, bay leaves, sugar, black pepper, star anise, cinnamon. Do **not** add tamari/salt yet (salt slows tenderizing). Reduce to medium-low, cover, simmer **40 min**.
3. Add tamari, dark-soy sub, and salt. Stir. Continue simmering uncovered until beef is tender but not falling apart — about **1½ hours** more for chuck (total ~2 hr 10 min after blanch). Add splash of water if liquid drops below halfway up the meat.
4. Fish out ginger, green onion, bay, star anise, cinnamon, chiles as best you can.
5. Add potatoes and carrots; submerge in broth. Simmer uncovered **25–30 min** until veggies are tender.
6. Taste broth — should be savory-sweet with warm spice. Adjust salt/sugar. Sauce should be brothy-saucy (not a tight glaze) — perfect over rice.

**Instant Pot option (if available):** After blanching, transfer beef + 1 cup blanch liquid + all seasonings/spices → High pressure 40 min chuck, natural release 20 min. Remove spices, add potato/carrot → High pressure 2 min, natural release 20 min.

**Storage:** Fridge 3–4 days. **Freezes excellently** up to 3 months (Maggie + commenters). Reheat stovetop with a splash of water, or microwave. Better day 2.

### Scaling Notes
- Original 2–3 lb for 6–8 → 2.2 kg for 9 portions (~245g raw beef/portion).
- Spices (star anise, cinnamon, bay, chiles) scaled sub-linear — aromatics extract strongly in long braise.
- Potatoes/carrots scaled for ~100g veg per portion.
- Keep some extra braising liquid when packing — rice soaks it up.

## 🍗 DISH 3: Three Cup Chicken (San Bei Ji / 三杯鸡)

**Source:** https://thewoksoflife.com/three-cup-chicken-san-bei-ji/  
**Why this recipe:** The Woks of Life (Sarah Leung) — family recipe with grandma’s approval. 4.98★ from 49 ratings, 198 comments. Classic Taiwanese one-pot: sesame oil + soy + rice wine reduced to a sticky dark glaze clinging to chicken. Naturally saucy, bold, and batch-friendly. We swap wings for **boneless skinless thighs** (better meal-prep protein density, easier packing, less bone waste) and use GF liquid swaps.

**Original servings:** 6 (wings) | **Scaled to:** 8 portions (thighs)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (customer)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 360 kcal |
| Protein | 42g (47%) |
| Carbs | 4g (4%) |
| Fat | 20g (50%) |
| Fibre | 0g |

**Portion size:** ~160g cooked boneless thigh + glaze

### Macro Calculation Summary
USDA chicken thigh roasted skinless: ~209 kcal / 26g P / 0g C / 11g F per 100g. 160g ≈ 334 / 42 / 17. Sesame oil + veg oil in batch ÷ 8 ≈ +45 kcal / 5g F. Sugar + tamari glaze ≈ +20 kcal. Total ≈ 360 / 42P / 4C / 20F. Leanest meat dish of the week — good protein lever.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 8 portions)

**Aromatics + oil:**
- 2 Tbsp toasted sesame oil (aceite de ajonjolí / sésamo)
- 3 Tbsp neutral oil
- 10 slices fresh ginger
- 8 garlic cloves, smashed/sliced
- 2–3 dried red chiles, halved (optional heat)

**Chicken:**
- 1.6 kg boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into large bite-size pieces (~4–5 cm)

**Sauce:**
- ⅓ cup warm water
- ⅓ cup dry sherry (jerez seco)
- 2 Tbsp **GF Dark Soy** (from pantry prep jar; add 1 tsp more for deeper color if needed)
- 2 Tbsp tamari (light soy role)
- 1 Tbsp sugar

**Finish:**
- 1 large bunch Thai basil (albahaca tailandesa) if available — OR 5 green onions cut into 5 cm lengths (scallion version is classic too and easier in Playa)
- Optional both

**Method:**
1. Medium heat in a wide heavy pan/wok: sesame oil + neutral oil + ginger + garlic + dried chile. Infuse 2 minutes until fragrant (don’t burn garlic).
2. Raise heat to high. Add chicken in a single layer (work in 2 batches if needed). Sear until golden on both sides, 6–8 min total.
3. Add water, dry sherry, dark-soy sub, tamari, sugar. Stir, cover, reduce heat to medium. Simmer **15–18 min** until chicken is cooked through and tender.
4. Uncover, raise heat high. Reduce sauce hard, stirring, until it becomes a sticky dark glaze clinging to the chicken (3–6 min). Do not walk away — sugar can scorch.
5. Toss in Thai basil and/or scallions; cook 1 min until wilted. Taste — should be savory, lightly sweet, sesame-fragrant.

**Storage:** Fridge 4 days. Reheats well microwave or skillet with splash of water. Freezes OK (texture slightly softer). **Best-eaten fresher** → cook Friday for weekend plates; freeze some Mon–Tue stock from earlier cook if needed.

### Scaling Notes
- Converted from 2¼ lb wings (~6 serv) to 1.6 kg boneless thighs (8 portions) for meal prep.
- Sauce liquids scaled ~1.3×; sesame oil kept moderate (potent).
- Thai basil is ideal but often hard in Playa — scallions are the approved alternate in the source recipe.
- Color comes from dark-soy sub; add molasses-tamari gradually.

## 🍆 DISH 4: Chinese Eggplant with Garlic Sauce (红烧茄子)

**Source:** https://omnivorescookbook.com/chinese-eggplant-with-garlic-sauce/  
**Why this recipe:** Maggie Zhu — 4.88★ from 262 ratings, 487 comments. Explicitly gluten-free adaptable. Minimal oil method with cornstarch coat for crispy-then-saucy texture. Saucy garlic glaze pairs brilliantly with rich braises. Eggplant (berenjena) is easy in Playa markets.

**Original servings:** 2–4 | **Scaled to:** 13 side portions

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (side)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 140 kcal |
| Protein | 2g (6%) |
| Carbs | 14g (40%) |
| Fat | 10g (64%) |
| Fibre | 4g |

**Portion size:** ~100g cooked eggplant with sauce

### Macro Calculation Summary
USDA eggplant cooked: ~35 kcal / 100g. Per portion veg ~80g cooked eggplant ≈ 28 kcal. Oil ~10g/portion in batch cooking ≈ 90 kcal. Sauce sugar/tamari/cornstarch ≈ 20 kcal. Total ≈ 140. Matches scaled Omnivore card (~122 kcal/119g serving) with slightly more oil for batch searing.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 13 side portions)

**Eggplant:**
- 1.6 kg Chinese/long eggplant (or regular globe if needed), cut into bite-size pieces (~3–4 cm)
- 1 Tbsp salt (for soaking)
- 5 Tbsp cornstarch (maicena), for coating

**Sauce (mix in a bowl):**
- 5 Tbsp tamari
- 5 Tbsp water
- 2½ tsp **GF Dark Soy** (from pantry prep jar)
- 3 Tbsp sugar
- 5 tsp cornstarch

**Stir-fry:**
- ~½ cup neutral oil (use as needed — eggplant drinks oil; work in batches)
- 1½ Tbsp minced ginger
- 12 garlic cloves, chopped

**Method:**
1. **Soak:** Cover eggplant with water + 1 Tbsp salt in a large bowl. Weight with a plate 15 min. Drain, pat **very dry**.
2. Toss dry eggplant with 5 Tbsp cornstarch until lightly coated.
3. Mix sauce ingredients until cornstarch dissolves.
4. **Sear in batches:** Heat 2–3 Tbsp oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium-high. Spread eggplant in a single layer (no overlapping). Cook, turning, until all sides are charred-golden and flesh is soft, 8–10 min per batch. Transfer to a tray. Repeat, adding oil as needed.
5. In the same pan, add a little oil + ginger + garlic; stir 20–30 sec until fragrant.
6. Return all eggplant. Re-stir sauce, pour over. Toss immediately — sauce thickens in seconds. Coat evenly. Done.

**Batch tip:** For 1.6 kg, oven-assist is OK: toss cornstarch-coated eggplant with oil on sheet pans at 220°C / 425°F for 20–25 min until browned, then finish with garlic + sauce in a hot pan (commenter-validated approach when skillet is maxed).

**Storage:** Fridge 3–4 days. Softens on reheat (normal). Microwave OK; skillet reheat with splash of water better. Freezes acceptably but texture goes soft — prefer fridge portions for this dish; freeze the braises first.

### Scaling Notes
- Original 10 oz eggplant / 2–4 serv → 1.6 kg for 13 sides (~120g raw/portion).
- Sauce scaled ~5× with sugar at 0.9× linear to avoid cloying.
- Garlic is the star — don’t skimp.
- Regular globe eggplant works; may need slightly longer cook / more salt soak.

## 🫘 DISH 5: Sichuan Dry-Fried Green Beans (干煸四季豆) — Playa Adaptation

**Source:** https://omnivorescookbook.com/szechuan-dry-fried-green-beans/  
**Cross-check:** https://thewoksoflife.com/sichuan-dry-fried-green-beans/ (4.85★ / 38 ratings)  
**Why this recipe:** Maggie Zhu — 4.87★ from 46 ratings. Gluten-free adaptable. Blistered beans + savory garlic/pork bits — addictive contrast to saucy braises. Authentic version uses *sui mi ya cai* (pickled mustard) which is nearly impossible in Playa; we use Maggie’s own approved substitutes: **ground pork for flavor + optional fermented black beans or a pinch of chicken bouillon**, skip hard-to-find ya cai. Sichuan peppercorn optional if DAC has it.

**Original servings:** 2–4 | **Scaled to:** 12 side portions

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (side)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 90 kcal |
| Protein | 4g (18%) |
| Carbs | 12g (53%) |
| Fat | 5g (50%) |
| Fibre | 4g |

**Portion size:** ~90g beans + pork bits

### Macro Calculation Summary
Green beans cooked 80g: ~28 kcal / 1.5P / 6C. Ground pork 25g cooked: ~50 kcal / 4P / 4F. Oil ~4g: 36 kcal. Seasonings negligible. Total ≈ 90–110 → use 90 as side average after oil drain.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 12 side portions)

**Beans:**
- 1.4 kg green beans (ejotes), ends trimmed
- Pinch salt
- 4–5 Tbsp neutral oil (for blistering, used across batches)

**Pork + aromatics:**
- 350g ground pork (optional but strongly recommended for flavor without ya cai)
- 1 tsp ground Sichuan peppercorn (pimiento de Sichuan) — skip if unavailable
- 6 dried red chiles
- 8 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 Tbsp minced ginger

**Seasoning:**
- 3 Tbsp dry sherry
- 2 Tbsp tamari
- 2 tsp sugar
- Salt to taste
- 2 tsp toasted sesame oil (finish)
- **Ya cai substitute (pick one):** 2 Tbsp fermented black beans (douchi), rinsed and chopped — OR 1 tsp GF chicken bouillon powder — OR 1 Tbsp GF Maggi Jugo if labeled wheat-free

**Method:**
1. Pat beans completely dry.
2. **Blister:** Heat 2 Tbsp oil in a large skillet over medium-high. Add a batch of beans + pinch salt. Cook, flipping every 15–30 sec, until blistered/charred in spots and just tender, 6–10 min. Don’t steam — work in batches. Transfer to a tray. (Oven option: 220°C, tossed with oil, 15–20 min until blistered.)
3. In the pan, add 1 Tbsp oil + ground pork + Sichuan pepper (if using) + fermented black beans/bouillon. Cook, breaking pork into tiny bits, until browned.
4. Add dried chiles, garlic, ginger; stir 30 sec until fragrant.
5. Return all beans. Add dry sherry, tamari, sugar, sesame oil. High heat, toss 1–2 min until seasoning coats and any liquid is absorbed. Taste; salt if needed.

**Storage:** Fridge 3–4 days. Best texture day 1–2. Reheat skillet preferred (microwave softens blister). Freezing OK but beans go soft — prioritize freezing meats instead.

### Scaling Notes
- Original 1 lb beans → 1.4 kg for 12 sides.
- Ground pork increased slightly vs linear scale so every portion gets savory bits (compensates for missing ya cai).
- Sichuan peppercorn is the “mala” tingle — nice if DAC has it; dish still excellent without.
- **Do not use doubanjiang** unless you find a verified GF jar (most contain wheat).

## 🍚 RICE: Jasmine Rice (25 portions)

**Method:** Standard absorption  
**Dry rice:** 1.25 kg jasmine rice (arroz jazmín) ≈ 25 × 50g dry  
**Water:** 1.9 L (about 1.5× by volume after rinse)  
**Optional aroma:** 2 slices ginger + 1 tsp salt in the water  

**Method:**
1. Rinse rice in cold water until water runs mostly clear (3–4 rinses).
2. Combine rice + water + optional ginger/salt in a large heavy pot.
3. Bring to boil uncovered, stir once, reduce to lowest heat, cover tight.
4. Cook 15 min. Turn off heat; rest covered 10 min. Fluff with fork/paddle.
5. Cool slightly before packing (hot rice = condensation = mush).

**Per portion cooked:** ~150g (about ¾ cup)  
**Macros per portion:** ~195 kcal / 4g P / 43g C / 0.5g F  

**Storage:** Fridge 4–5 days. Freeze well in portions. Reheat microwave with damp paper towel or sprinkle of water.

## ⚠️ NOTES & CONSIDERATIONS

1. **Shaoxing wine almost always contains wheat.** This plan uses dry sherry throughout. Do not buy Shaoxing unless the label is explicitly gluten-free (rare).
2. **Dark soy is not sold GF easily in Playa.** Make the simmered **GF Dark Soy jar** (see pantry prep) — don’t skip the 3–5 min simmer; cold-mixed molasses stays grainy and less glossy.
3. **Hong Shao Rou fat:** If pork is very fatty, chill after cooking and scrape solid fat before packing — keeps macros cleaner and reheating less greasy.
4. **Eggplant texture:** Softens by day 4–5. Eat eggplant pairings earlier in the week when possible; freeze braises preferentially.
5. **Three Cup Chicken** is the “best fresh” dish — Friday cook → Sat priority.
6. **Beef stew** is the freezer champion — prioritize it for Mon–Tue thaw.
7. **No oyster sauce / hoisin / doubanjiang** this week on purpose — those are the usual GF landmines. Flavor still deep from caramel, sesame, star anise, long braise.
8. **Spice level:** Mild-medium. Dried chiles are mostly aromatic; break them open only if you want heat.
9. **Assistant portions:** Plate 80–90% of customer meat scoops; veg/rice similar. Buffer portions cover the difference.
10. **Cross-check summary:**
    - HSR: Omnivore’s ↔ Woks of Life (liquid ratios, caramel method, cook time aligned)
    - Beef stew: Omnivore’s stovetop times cross-checked against typical Chinese red-braise beef (2–2.5 hr chuck)
    - Three Cup: Woks of Life ratios adapted thighs; sauce cling method unchanged
    - Eggplant: Omnivore’s soak + cornstarch method; sauce ratio scaled
    - Green beans: Omnivore’s + Woks dry-sear principle; ya cai sub per Maggie’s footnotes
