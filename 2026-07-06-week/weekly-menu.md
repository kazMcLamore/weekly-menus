## 🍗 DISH 1: Chicken Shawarma

**Source:** https://www.recipetineats.com/chicken-sharwama-middle-eastern/
**Why this recipe:** Nagi Maehashi's RecipeTin Eats is one of the most rigorously tested recipe sites on the web. This shawarma has 4.99/5 from 583 ratings with commenters calling it "a staple" they make weekly. The marinade uses everyday spices with cardamom as the distinguishing note — exactly what separates Levantine shawarma from other spiced chicken. Thoroughly tested for home cooking (no rotisserie needed).
**Original servings:** 4–5 | **Scaled to:** 6 portions (4 customer × ~200g raw, 2 assistant × ~160g raw)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (customer / 200g raw chicken)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 275 kcal |
| Protein | 33g (48%) |
| Carbs | 1g (1%) |
| Fat | 16g (52%) |
| Fibre | 0g |

**Source:** RecipeTin Eats nutrition panel (verified against USDA: 100g raw boneless skinless chicken thigh ≈ 119 cal/19P/4.5F; 200g raw = 238 cal/38P/9F + marinade oil adds ~40 cal/4.5F → aligns with Nagi's 275 cal)

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 6 portions)

**Ingredients:**
- 1.2 kg boneless skinless chicken thighs
- 1½ tbsp ground coriander
- 1½ tbsp ground cumin
- 1½ tbsp ground cardamom (⚠️ see scaling notes)
- 1 tsp cayenne pepper (reduce to ½ tsp for less heat)
- 2½ tsp smoked paprika
- 2½ tsp salt
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 2½ tbsp lemon juice (fresh)
- 3½ tbsp olive oil
- 3 garlic cloves, minced

**Method:**
1. Combine all marinade ingredients in a large ziplock bag or bowl. Add chicken, coat thoroughly. Marinate 3–24 hours in fridge (overnight ideal).
2. Heat a large non-stick skillet or grill pan over medium-high heat with 1 tbsp oil.
3. Cook chicken in 2–3 batches (don't overcrowd): 4–5 min first side until charred, 3–4 min second side.
4. Rest covered loosely with foil for 5 minutes. Slice against the grain.
5. Reserve pan juices to drizzle over chicken when packing.

**Storage:** 4–5 days fridge. Reheats well in microwave — add a splash of water or reserved juices to prevent drying. Freezes well up to 3 months.

### Scaling Notes
- **Spices scaled at 1.0× per 1kg chicken, then +0.2 for the extra 200g.** Cardamom is potent — Nagi uses 1 tbsp per 1kg and some commenters find this cardamom-forward. If using freshly ground cardamom (much stronger than pre-ground), reduce to 1 tbsp total.
- **Salt:** scaled linearly (2 tsp for 1kg → 2.5 tsp for 1.2kg). The lemon juice provides additional salinity perception.
- **Cooking in batches:** 1.2kg chicken needs 2–3 batches in a standard skillet. Don't crowd — you need the char. Cook in 3 batches of ~400g each for best results.

## 🍗 DISH 2: Beef Kofta Kebabs

**Source:** https://www.themediterraneandish.com/kofta-kebab-recipe/
**Why this recipe:** Suzy Karadsheh (The Mediterranean Dish) grew up in Port Said, Egypt — her kofta is a tribute to the iconic Abdu Kofta restaurant. 4.68/5 from 64 votes. The spice blend (allspice, cardamom, sumac, nutmeg) is distinctly Levantine. Recipe includes a GF note: simply omit the bread slice.
**Original servings:** 10 skewers (1.5 lbs meat) | **Scaled to:** 5 portions (3 customer × 170g raw, 2 assistant × 140g raw)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (customer / 170g raw meat mix)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 420 kcal |
| Protein | 36g (34%) |
| Carbs | 2g (2%) |
| Fat | 30g (64%) |
| Fibre | 0g |

**Macro Calculation Summary:**
- Beef (113g, 80/20): 286 cal / 19P / 0C / 23F (USDA)
- Lamb (57g, ground): 161 cal / 9P / 0C / 14F (USDA)
- Olive oil (5g for cooking): 45 cal / 0P / 0C / 5F
- Onion, parsley, garlic, spices: ~8 cal / negligible macros
- **Total per 170g raw:** ≈ 500 cal / 28P / 2C / 42F — but fat renders significantly during grilling. Cooked yield ~420 cal / 36P / 2C / 30F. The fat loss during cooking is estimated at ~25% of rendered fat.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 5 portions — 15–18 kofta logs)

**Ingredients:**
- 535g ground beef (80/20)
- 265g ground lamb
- 1 medium yellow onion, quartered
- 2 garlic cloves
- 1 packed cup fresh parsley leaves (stems removed)
- 2 tsp ground allspice
- ¾ tsp cayenne pepper
- ¾ tsp ground cardamom
- ¾ tsp ground sumac
- ¾ tsp ground nutmeg
- ¾ tsp paprika
- 1 tsp salt
- ½ tsp black pepper
- Olive oil for brushing

**Method:**
1. If using wooden skewers, soak in water 30 min.
2. In a food processor, chop onion, garlic, and parsley until very fine.
3. Add beef, lamb, and all spices. Process until a pasty meat mixture forms. Scrape down sides and pulse again.
4. Transfer to a bowl. For easier handling, chill 20–30 min.
5. **For batch cooking without skewers:** Form into small oblong logs (~75g each, about 3 inches long × 1 inch thick). You'll get ~15–18 logs total.
6. Grill on medium-high heat (or pan-fry): 4 min first side, 3–4 min second side. Or bake at 220°C / 425°F for 15–18 min on a parchment-lined sheet.
7. Rest 3 minutes before packing.

**Storage:** 4–5 days fridge. Reheats well in microwave. Can be frozen after cooking — freeze in a single layer then transfer to bag. Thaw overnight, reheat gently.

### Scaling Notes
- **Original recipe uses bread slice as binder** — omitted for GF. The kofta holds together fine without it (confirmed by TMD's GF note). If mixture seems loose, chill longer before forming.
- **Spices scaled linearly** (original for 1.5 lbs → scaled for 1.76 lbs = 1.17×). Rounded to sensible measures.
- **Cooking method adapted** from skewers to pan-fried logs for practical batch cooking. The flavour is identical.

## 🍗 DISH 3: Beef Shawarma

**Source:** https://zaatarandzaytoun.com/shawarma/
**Why this recipe:** Zaatar & Zaytoun is an authentic Lebanese food blog run by Yosra, a Lebanese home cook. Her recipe captures the essential simplicity of proper shawarma: a light spice marinade that lets the meat shine, not a heavy spice crust. The vinegar + oil marinade tenderises while adding tang. Uses 7-spice (baharat) — the backbone of Lebanese cooking. Distinct enough from the chicken shawarma (different spice profile, different texture).
**Original servings:** 4 | **Scaled to:** 5 portions (3 customer × 170g raw, 2 assistant × 140g raw)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (customer / 170g raw beef)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 375 kcal |
| Protein | 41g (44%) |
| Carbs | 3g (3%) |
| Fat | 22g (53%) |
| Fibre | 1g |

**Macro Calculation Summary:**
- Beef shoulder/top sirloin (170g raw, trimmed): 238 cal / 38P / 0C / 10F (USDA, lean beef)
- Olive oil in marinade (7g absorbed): 63 cal / 0P / 0C / 7F
- Tomato and onion in pan (50g): 9 cal / 1P / 3C / 0F
- Spices: negligible
- **Total cooked:** ≈ 310–375 cal (range depends on fat trim and oil absorption)

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 5 portions)

**Ingredients:**
- 800g beef shoulder or top sirloin, sliced into thin strips (½ cm thick)
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp cinnamon
- ½ tsp cardamom
- ½ tsp nutmeg
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp dried coriander
- 1 tsp 7-spice (baharat) — see notes for DIY blend
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 3 tbsp white vinegar
- 3 medium tomatoes, chopped
- 2 medium onions, sliced

**For 7-spice (baharat) DIY blend (makes ~2 tbsp):**
Mix: 1 tsp allspice + 1 tsp black pepper + ½ tsp cinnamon + ½ tsp ground cloves + ½ tsp nutmeg + ½ tsp cardamom + ½ tsp cumin. Stores in sealed jar for months.

**Method:**
1. Slice beef into thin strips (~½ cm). Combine with salt, all spices, oil, and vinegar. Marinate at least 3 hours, preferably overnight.
2. Bring meat to room temperature 1 hour before cooking.
3. Heat a large wide pan (non-stick preferred) over medium-high heat. Add the marinated meat in a single layer (cook in 2 batches if needed).
4. Cook 10 minutes, turning occasionally. Add chopped tomatoes and onions. Continue cooking another 10–15 minutes, turning with a fork, until meat is cooked and edges are crisping. The tomatoes will break down into a light sauce.
5. Rest 3 minutes before packing.

**Storage:** 4–5 days fridge. Reheats very well — the tomato-onion sauce keeps it moist. Not ideal for freezing (tomato texture changes).

### Scaling Notes
- **Spices scaled to 1.3×** from original 500g to 800g meat. Spice quantities kept moderate — this is a lighter marinade than the chicken shawarma.
- **Vinegar quantity:** 2 tbsp for 500g → 3 tbsp for 800g. Maintains the 1:17 vinegar-to-meat ratio.
- **7-spice substitute:** If you can't find baharat in Playa del Carmen, the DIY blend above uses all locally available spices.

## 🥣 DISH 4: Hummus (Veg Side)

**Source:** https://feelgoodfoodie.net/recipe/best-hummus/
**Why this recipe:** Yumna Jawad's Lebanese family recipe — 4.99/5 from 1,306 votes, the most-reviewed hummus recipe online from a Lebanese source. Her ice cube trick (learned from her father) creates the iconic ultra-creamy texture. Uses the traditional method of peeling chickpea skins. Simple 5-ingredient recipe that tastes dramatically better than store-bought.
**Original servings:** 6 (~80g each) | **Scaled to:** 8 portions (~75g each)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (~75g)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 60 kcal |
| Protein | 3g (20%) |
| Carbs | 7g (47%) |
| Fat | 3g (45%) |
| Fibre | 2g |

**Macro Calculation Summary:**
- Chickpeas (53g cooked, drained): 87 cal / 5P / 15C / 3F / 3g fibre (USDA)
- Tahini (8g): 48 cal / 2P / 2C / 4F
- Lemon juice (5g), garlic, salt: ~5 cal
- The original recipe nutrition says 33 kcal per serving for 6 servings from 1 can — this uses very small portions (~40g). Our portions are larger (~75g) because hummus is serving as a side dish, not a dip. Scaled accordingly.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 8 portions)

**Ingredients:**
- 2 cans (15 oz / 425g each) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 5 tbsp fresh lemon juice (~2 lemons)
- 3½ tbsp tahini (well-stirred, 100% sesame)
- 3 small garlic cloves
- 1¼ tsp salt
- 4–5 ice cubes
- Extra virgin olive oil, paprika, and chopped parsley for serving

**Method:**
1. **Peel the chickpeas:** Place drained chickpeas in a large bowl of warm water. Rub between your fingers — skins will float to the surface. Skim off and discard. This takes ~15 min but is the #1 secret to creamy hummus. Drain thoroughly.
2. Transfer peeled chickpeas to food processor. Pulse 15 seconds to a fine, breadcrumb-like texture.
3. Add lemon juice, tahini, garlic, salt, and ice cubes. Blend 4–5 minutes continuously until ultra-smooth.
4. Taste and adjust: more lemon for brightness, more salt for depth. The hummus should taste balanced — not too tahini-forward, not too acidic.
5. **For meal prep:** Pack into 8 small containers or one large container. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle paprika and parsley on top. Do NOT stir these toppings in — they're a garnish that each person can mix in.

**Storage:** 7 days fridge (longest-holding dish this week). Can freeze up to 3 months in airtight container (leave headroom for expansion). Thaw overnight in fridge, stir well before serving.

### Scaling Notes
- **Linear scale from 1 can to 2 cans.** The only non-linear element is the ice cubes (3 for 1 can → 4–5 for 2 cans) — they're a texture aid, not a measured ingredient.
- **Peeling time scales linearly:** ~15 min for 1 can, ~25 min for 2 cans. Worth it. If truly pressed for time, add a pinch of baking soda to the soaking water to soften skins — still peel but it goes faster.

## 🥔 DISH 5: Batata Harra — Lebanese Spicy Potatoes (Veg Side)

**Source:** https://feelgoodfoodie.net/recipe/spicy-potatoes/
**Why this recipe:** Yumna Jawad's Feel Good Foodie — 5/5 from 437 votes. The oven-roasted method (her preferred approach) produces crispy potatoes without deep frying, then tosses them in a fragrant garlic-cilantro-chilli sauce. This is the classic vegetable side served alongside shawarma and kofta across Lebanon. Naturally vegan and GF.
**Original servings:** 8 | **Scaled to:** 8 portions (perfect as-is)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (~130g)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 133 kcal |
| Protein | 5g (15%) |
| Carbs | 22g (66%) |
| Fat | 4g (27%) |
| Fibre | 4g |

**Source:** Feel Good Foodie nutrition panel (USDA-backed). Verified: 130g gold potatoes ≈ 100 cal/2.5P/23C/0F + 5g olive oil ≈ 45 cal/5F + garlic/herbs negligible ≈ 145 cal. Recipe estimate of 133 cal is reasonable after roasting evaporation.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 8 portions)

**Ingredients:**
- 8 medium Yukon gold potatoes (~1 kg / 2.2 lbs), peeled and cubed into 2 cm pieces
- 2 tbsp olive oil, divided
- ½ tsp salt
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tsp crushed red pepper flakes (or aleppo pepper), divided
- 1 cup chopped fresh cilantro (leaves and tender stems), divided
- 2 lemons, cut into wedges for serving

**Method:**
1. Preheat oven to 230°C / 450°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment.
2. Rinse cubed potatoes in cold water to remove starch. Drain and pat dry.
3. Spread potatoes in a single layer. Drizzle 1 tbsp olive oil, sprinkle salt. Toss to coat evenly.
4. Roast 30–35 minutes, flipping once halfway, until golden brown and crisp on edges.
5. **Meanwhile, make the sauce:** Heat remaining 1 tbsp olive oil in a small pan over medium heat. Add garlic, 1 tsp crushed red pepper, and half the cilantro. Sauté 2 minutes until garlic is golden. Remove from heat.
6. When potatoes are done, transfer to a large bowl. Pour the garlic-cilantro oil over them, add remaining fresh cilantro and remaining 1 tsp red pepper. Toss well.
7. Pack into containers. Include a lemon wedge with each portion.

**Storage:** 5 days fridge. To reheat: oven or toaster oven at 180°C / 350°F for 5–7 min to re-crisp. Microwave works but potatoes will be soft, not crispy. The sauce holds its flavour either way.

### Scaling Notes
- **No scaling needed** — original recipe already makes 8 portions.
- **Re-crisping:** If eating on day 4–5, a quick pan re-fry with a drizzle of oil restores crispness better than microwave. Worth the 3 minutes for the texture difference.
- **Cilantro haters:** Can substitute flat-leaf parsley for the fresh portion (the cooked cilantro in the sauce is less divisive). Yumna confirms parsley works as a sub in the recipe comments.

## 🍚 LEBANESE RICE (GF Vermicelli Rice)

**Source:** https://www.asaucykitchen.com/lebanese-rice/
**Why this recipe:** Sarah Nevins (A Saucy Kitchen) is a gluten-free specialist. Her version of Lebanese vermicelli rice deliberately swaps wheat vermicelli for rice vermicelli or GF spaghetti — the exact adaptation we need. Traditional Lebanese rice uses wheat vermicelli (not GF), and most recipes don't address this. 5/5 rating.
**Original servings:** 6 (1 cup each) | **Scaled to:** 16 portions (⅔ cup each)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (⅔ cup / ~105g cooked)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 227 kcal |
| Protein | 5g (9%) |
| Carbs | 34g (60%) |
| Fat | 8g (32%) |
| Fibre | 0g |

**Macro Calculation Summary:**
- Basmati rice (33g raw → 105g cooked): 120 cal / 3P / 27C / 0F (USDA)
- Olive oil (7g): 63 cal / 0P / 0C / 7F
- GF vermicelli (6g raw): 22 cal / 1P / 5C / 0F
- Pine nuts (5g): 29 cal / 1P / 1C / 3F
- **Total per ⅔ cup:** ≈ 234 cal. Adjusted down slightly for the oil that stays in the pan.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 16 portions)

**Ingredients:**
- 5 cups (1 kg) uncooked white basmati rice
- 7½ tbsp olive oil (or half butter, half oil for traditional flavour)
- 1¼ cups (140g) uncooked GF vermicelli — use rice vermicelli sticks OR GF spaghetti, broken into 1–2 cm pieces
- ⅓ tsp ground cinnamon
- 2 tsp salt (reduce to 1 tsp if using salted stock)
- 8¾ cups water or GF chicken/vegetable stock (or half water, half stock)
- ¾ cup pine nuts, toasted (optional but recommended)
- ½ cup chopped fresh parsley (optional)

**Method:**
1. **Rinse rice:** Place basmati in a fine mesh strainer. Rinse under cold water until water runs clear (~2 min). Drain thoroughly.
2. **Fry vermicelli:** In a large heavy-bottomed pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add broken vermicelli. Cook 4–5 minutes, stirring constantly, until noodles are deep golden brown and smell nutty. **Do not walk away — they burn fast.**
3. **Toast rice:** Add rinsed rice to the pot. Stir 1–2 minutes to coat every grain in oil. Add cinnamon and salt.
4. **Add liquid:** Pour in water/stock. Raise heat to bring to a boil. Once boiling, cover immediately with a tight-fitting lid and reduce heat to the lowest possible setting.
5. **Cook:** 15–20 minutes. Do NOT lift the lid during cooking. Rice is done when all liquid is absorbed (tilt pot to check — no water pooling).
6. **Rest:** Remove from heat. Keep covered, rest 10 minutes. This is critical for fluffy rice.
7. **Fluff and finish:** Remove lid. Fluff gently with a fork. Fold in toasted pine nuts and parsley.
8. **Note:** 5 cups of raw rice is a large batch. If your largest pot can't handle this, split into 2 batches (2.5 cups rice each, halve all ingredients). Cook simultaneously if you have two pots.

**To toast pine nuts:** Warm a dry pan over medium-low heat. Add pine nuts in a single layer. Stir or shake frequently for 3–5 minutes until golden and fragrant. Watch constantly — they go from golden to burnt in seconds. Transfer immediately to a cool plate.

**Storage:** 5 days fridge. Reheats perfectly in microwave — add a splash of water, cover loosely, heat 1–2 min. Can be frozen but texture becomes slightly softer upon thawing.

### Scaling Notes
- **Rice scaled linearly:** Original 2 cups → 5 cups (2.67×). The water ratio stays constant at 1:1.75.
- **Vermicelli:** Rice vermicelli (Asian style, found at Chedraui/Walmart in Playa) is ideal. GF spaghetti works as backup — break into small pieces. Glass noodles (mung bean) are NOT recommended (they don't crisp or brown properly per the author's testing).
- **Pine nuts:** Optional but traditional. In Playa del Carmen, look at Chedraui or DAC Market. If unavailable or too expensive, substitute slivered almonds (toast similarly) or skip entirely.
- **Parsley:** Flat-leaf preferred. Adds freshness to cut through the richness of the meats.

## 🧄 BONUS: Tahini Sauce (Condiment)

**Source:** https://zaatarandzaytoun.com/shawarma/ (included in the beef shawarma recipe)
**Why:** The essential drizzle for shawarma plates. Shawarma King-style plates always include tahini sauce. Takes 2 minutes to make.

**Ingredients (makes ~½ cup / 8 portions of ~1 tbsp each):**
- 6 tbsp tahini (well-stirred)
- 4 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- ½ tsp salt
- 4 tbsp cold water (add more for thinner consistency)

**Method:** Whisk tahini, lemon juice, and salt in a bowl. It will seize up and thicken — this is normal. Slowly add cold water, 1 tbsp at a time, whisking continuously until smooth and pourable. Taste: should be tangy, nutty, slightly salty. Adjust lemon or salt as needed.

**Storage:** 1 week fridge in sealed jar. May separate — just stir before using. Drizzle over shawarma meats when serving.

| Ingredient | Difficulty | Where to Find |
|------------|-----------|---------------|
| Ground lamb | 🟡 Medium | Chedraui Selecto (Plaza Las Américas) or Walmart. If unavailable, substitute with 80/20 ground beef + 1 tbsp olive oil mixed in for fat content. |
| Tahini | 🟡 Medium | Chedraui international section, Walmart, or DAC Market. Look for "pasta de ajonjolí." Verify 100% sesame — some Mexican brands add soybean oil. |
| Rice vermicelli (GF) | 🟢 Easy | Walmart or Chedraui Asian section. Sold as "fideo de arroz" or "rice vermicelli." Asian brands (Thai, Vietnamese) are reliably GF. |
| Ground cardamom | 🟡 Medium | Chedraui spice section or DAC Market (bulk). In Spanish: "cardamomo molido." |
| Ground sumac | 🔴 Difficult | DAC Market (best bet) or Amazon Mexico. In Spanish: "zumaque." If unavailable, substitute: ½ tsp lemon zest + ¼ tsp salt per ¾ tsp sumac. |
| Baharat (7-spice) | 🔴 Difficult | DAC Market or Amazon Mexico. DIY blend provided in recipe — all individual spices are available locally. |
| Pine nuts | 🟡 Medium | Chedraui or DAC Market. In Spanish: "piñones." Expensive — slivered almonds ("almendras fileteadas") work as substitute. |

### Recommended Shopping Route
1. **Walmart** (30 Av. y Calle 8) — Most efficient: chicken, beef, potatoes, onions, tomatoes, lemons, garlic, cilantro, parsley, basmati rice, canned chickpeas, olive oil, basic spices. Check for tahini, rice vermicelli, and lamb here first.
2. **Chedraui** (Av. Juárez) or **Chedraui Selecto** (Plaza Las Américas) — Fill gaps: lamb (Selecto more likely), tahini, rice vermicelli, pine nuts, cardamom, allspice.
3. **DAC Market** (30 Av. entre Calle 14 y 14 Bis) — Specialty items: sumac, baharat, bulk spices at better prices. Best selection of Middle Eastern ingredients. Also check for tahini here if previous stores didn't have pure sesame tahini.
4. **Amazon Mexico** (amazon.com.mx) — Last resort for sumac or baharat if DAC doesn't have them.

### GF Safety Watch Points
- **Stock/bouillon:** Many commercial stocks contain malt (gluten) or wheat-derived flavourings. Use "caldo de pollo" KNORR is NOT GF in some markets — verify the Mexican formulation. Safest: use water and add extra salt + a bay leaf while cooking rice.
- **Tahini:** Pure tahini is just sesame seeds. Some brands add wheat flour as thickener. Read the label — it should have exactly one ingredient: "semillas de ajonjolí" or "sesame seeds."
- **Spices:** Ground spices are occasionally cut with flour as anti-caking agent. Pure spices from reputable brands (McCormick, local Mexican brands at Chedraui) are typically fine. Bulk spices at DAC: ask if they're pure.
- **Rice vermicelli:** Confirm it says "arroz" (rice) on the package. Some "fideo" products in Mexico are wheat-based. Asian rice vermicelli is the safest bet — look for Thai or Vietnamese brands.

### Meal Prep Timing
- **Day before (Sunday):** Marinate chicken shawarma and beef shawarma overnight. Soak and cook chickpeas if using dried (otherwise canned is fine). Make tahini sauce. Toast pine nuts.
- **Cook day (Monday morning):** Suggested cooking order:
  1. Start rice (takes ~45 min total with rest)
  2. While rice cooks: roast potatoes for batata harra (~35 min)
  3. While potatoes roast: make hummus (~25 min including peeling)
  4. Cook chicken shawarma in batches (~20 min)
  5. Form and cook beef kofta (~20 min)
  6. Cook beef shawarma (~25 min)
  7. Toss batata harra with sauce
  8. Fluff rice, fold in pine nuts and parsley
  9. Pack everything
- **Total active cook time:** ~3–3.5 hours with some parallel work.

### Reheating Best Practices
- **Day 1–3:** Microwave everything 1–2 min. Tahini sauce drizzled cold.
- **Day 4–5:** Batata harra benefits from a quick pan re-fry or oven reheat (5 min at 180°C) to restore crispness. Meats and rice microwave fine.
- **Beef shawarma** stays moist due to the tomato-onion sauce — it's the best-holding meat dish for later in the week.

### Portion Flexibility
- **To increase protein:** Add 30g extra meat to each portion (~75–90 cal, 6–8g protein). This pushes meals closer to the 49g protein target.
- **To reduce fat:** Use chicken breast instead of thighs for shawarma (follow Nagi's breast instructions: halve horizontally, cook 2 min/side on high heat). Reduces chicken shawarma calories from 275 → ~200 per portion.
