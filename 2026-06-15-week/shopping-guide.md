### SPICES & SEASONINGS

#### 🟡 Garam Masala (you need 3 tbsp)
**What is it?** A blend of ground spices typical of Indian cooking. It contains cinnamon, cardamom, clove, cumin, coriander, black pepper, and nutmeg. It's THE core flavour of Indian cuisine — without it, the food won't taste Indian.

**Where to buy:**
1. **Walmart** — OG2 Especias Garam Masala (confirmed on walmart.com.mx). Look in the spice aisle, possibly in the "Importados" or "Gourmet" section.
2. **Chedraui** — Check the imported/gourmet section.
3. **Amazon Mexico** — Rani Garam Masala (~$120-180 MXN) or Badia Garam Masala (~$80 MXN). Delivery to Playa del Carmen.
4. **DAC Market** — they might carry it in bulk. Ask.

**If you can't find it, MAKE IT YOURSELF:** Mix in a jar:
- 1 tbsp ground cumin
- 1½ tsp ground coriander
- 1½ tsp ground cardamom (or open 6 green cardamom pods, remove the seeds, and grind them)
- 1½ tsp ground black pepper
- 1 tsp ground cinnamon
- ½ tsp ground clove
- ½ tsp ground nutmeg
*Makes ~4 tbsp. Store in a sealed jar.*

---

#### 🟢 Ground Cumin (you need 4 tbsp)
**What is it?** Regular cumin, the same kind used in Mexico. You'll find it at any supermarket as "Comino Molido."

**Where to buy:** Walmart (La Guacamaya 50g ~$15 MXN), Chedraui, Mega, any supermarket.

---

#### 🟢 Ground Coriander (you need 4 tbsp)
**What is it?** Ground coriander seeds. NOT the same as fresh cilantro. It tastes different — more citrusy and warm.

**Where to buy:** Walmart, Chedraui (look for "Cilantro en Polvo" or "Semillas de Cilantro Molidas"). Whole seeds are easier to find than powder — if you buy whole ones, grind them in a blender or molcajete.

---

#### 🟢 Turmeric Powder (you need 3 tbsp)
**What is it?** An intense yellow spice, sometimes called "Indian saffron" (but it ISN'T saffron). It stains EVERYTHING yellow — wash your hands and cutting board immediately. This is what gives curries their golden colour.

**Where to buy:** Walmart, Chedraui — in the spice aisle. Also at DAC Market in bulk.

---

#### 🟢 Paprika / Sweet Pimentón (you need 3 tbsp)
**What is it?** Ground dried red bell pepper. It's NOT spicy — it's sweet. In Spain they call it "pimentón dulce."

**Where to buy:** Walmart (Carmencita Pimentón Dulce 75g ~$33 MXN), Chedraui, any supermarket.

---

#### 🟢 Chili Powder / Cayenne (you need 1½ tbsp)
**What is it?** Pure ground red chili, like ground chile piquín or chile de árbol. Do NOT use American-style "chili powder" that has cumin and other things mixed in — you need PURE chili.

**Where to buy:** Any supermarket. Look for "Chile en Polvo" or "Chile Piquín Molido" or "Cayena Molida."

---

#### 🟢 Ground Cinnamon (part of homemade garam masala if needed)
**What is it?** Regular ground cinnamon. Mexican cinnamon (Ceylon cinnamon) is milder than American cassia — both work.

**Where to buy:** Walmart (Great Value 50g ~$40 MXN), any supermarket.

---

#### 🟡 Cinnamon Sticks (you need 2 sticks for Rogan Josh)
**What is it?** Whole cinnamon sticks. Widely used in Mexico for café de olla and teas.

**Where to buy:** Any supermarket in the spice section. Also at DAC Market.

---

#### 🔴 Green Cardamom Pods (you need 6)
**What is it?** Small green pods with black seeds inside. They smell like sweet, floral perfume. They're used whole (lightly crushed) and then either removed from the dish or eaten (they're soft after cooking).

**Where to buy:**
1. **DAC Market** — your best option. They carry rare spices.
2. **Walmart/Chedraui** — check the gourmet/international section. Not confirmed.
3. **Amazon Mexico** — several brands available.

**If you can't find them:** Skip the whole pods but MAKE SURE to include ground cardamom in the garam masala. It's not the same, but it helps.

---

#### 🟡 Whole Cloves (you need 6)
**What is it?** Regular cloves, the same ones used in Mexico for ponche and stews.

**Where to buy:** Any supermarket — they're common in Mexico.

---

#### 🟢 Ground Nutmeg (you need a pinch)
**What is it?** Regular nutmeg, the same kind used in Mexico.

**Where to buy:** Any supermarket.

---

#### 🟡 Whole Cumin Seeds (you need 1½ tsp)
**What is it?** Whole cumin seeds. They're toasted and then ground. They taste fresher than the powder.

**Where to buy:** DAC Market (in bulk) or Walmart/Chedraui (in a spice jar).

---

#### 🟡 Whole Coriander Seeds (you need 3 tsp)
**What is it?** Small light-brown round seeds. They taste citrusy and are completely different from fresh cilantro.

**Where to buy:** DAC Market (in bulk). Also at Walmart/Chedraui, look for "Semillas de Cilantro."

---

#### 🔴 Mustard Seeds (you need ¾ tsp)
**What is it?** Tiny black or brown seeds. When heated in oil, they pop like popcorn. They are NOT the same as yellow hot dog mustard — totally different thing.

**Where to buy:** DAC Market (in the bulk spice section). Unlikely at regular supermarkets.

**If you can't find them:** Skip them. The recipe works without — you'll just lose a bit of complexity.

---

#### 🔴 Kasoori Methi / Dried Fenugreek Leaves (you need 2 tbsp)
**What is it?** Dried leaves of a plant called fenugreek. They smell like sweet hay and curry. They give that "Indian restaurant" flavour you can't quite identify. Crumble them between your palms before adding.

**Where to buy:**
1. **Amazon Mexico** — Rani Kasoori Methi 28g (~$90-150 MXN). Worth it.
2. **DAC Market** — ask, they might have it.
3. Not confirmed at Walmart or Chedraui.

**If you can't find them:** Skip it in both recipes (Chana Masala and Dal Makhani). The flavour will be slightly less complex but still delicious. Do NOT use epazote — it tastes completely different.

---

#### 🟡 Tamarind (Concentrate/Paste for cooking, you need 3 tsp)
**What is it?** Dark brown, sticky, sour paste. Do NOT buy "concentrado de tamarindo para agua" (that's a sweet drink concentrate). You need tamarind paste for cooking. Look for "pulpa de tamarindo" or "tamarind paste" or "tamarind concentrate for cooking."

**Where to buy:**
1. **Walmart** — in the Asian/international section. The Deliciosa brand might be drink-only — check the label.
2. **Chedraui** — international section.
3. **DAC Market** — they carry Asian products, very likely.

**If you can't find it:** Substitute 1 tbsp fresh lime juice + ½ tsp brown sugar FOR EACH tsp of tamarind. Not the same but it works.

---

### LEGUMES & GRAINS

#### 🟢 Basmati Rice (you need 2 kg = 4 bags of 500g)
**What is it?** A long, thin-grain rice originally from India/Pakistan. It smells like popcorn when cooking. More expensive than regular rice but the aroma is important.

**Where to buy:**
1. **Walmart** — Arroz San Miguel Basmati 500g (~$60 MXN) or Santina Basmati 500g. Confirmed online.
2. **Chedraui** — Arroz Gourmet Basmati Santina 500g. Confirmed online.

Buy 4 bags of 500g. If the price is too high, buy 2 bags of basmati + 1 kg regular white rice and mix them.

---

#### 🟢 Canned Chickpeas (you need 2 cans)
**What is it?** Regular chickpeas, the same as Mexican ones. Canned ones are already cooked.

**Where to buy:** Any supermarket.

---

#### 🔴 Urad Dal / Black Indian Lentils (you need ¾ cup)
**What is it?** A small, oval black lentil originally from India. NOT the same as Mexican brown lentils. Also called "black gram" or "black beluga lentils" (belugas are slightly different but work).

**Where to buy:**
1. **Amazon Mexico** — search "Urad Dal" or "Black Gram." Brands like Rani or Swad.
2. **DAC Market** — ask. If they have Asian products, they might have black lentils.
3. **Walmart/Chedraui** — very unlikely.

**If you can't find them:** Use 1 cup of **REGULAR BROWN LENTILS** (the kind sold in any Mexican supermarket) + reduce the 6 tbsp of dried kidney beans to 3 tbsp. The flavour changes a bit but the dish is still delicious. Brown lentils cook faster (about 30-40 minutes without a pressure cooker).

---

#### 🟢 Dried Kidney Beans (you need 6 tbsp)
**What is it?** Regular red kidney beans. The same ones eaten in Mexico (frijol colorado or small red beans).

**Where to buy:** Any supermarket, in bulk or bagged.

---

### DAIRY & ALTERNATIVES

#### 🟢 Heavy Cream / Crema para Batir (you need 250ml)
**What is it?** Thick cow's cream, the kind used for whipping and making chantilly. NOT sour cream (crema ácida) or half-and-half (media crema). Look for "Crema para Batir" with at least 35% fat content.

**Where to buy:** Walmart, Chedraui, Mega — in the dairy section. Brands like Lyncott, Alpura, or store brand.

---

#### 🟢 Plain Greek Yogurt (you need 750g)
**What is it?** Thick yogurt, plain, no sugar. Creamier and higher in protein than regular yogurt. If you can't find "Greek," buy plain unsweetened yogurt and strain it through a clean kitchen towel for 2 hours — it'll thicken up.

**Where to buy:** Walmart, Chedraui, Mega. Brands like Oikos, Yoplait Greek, Fage, or store brands.

---

#### 🟡 Ghee / Clarified Butter (you need 250g)
**What is it?** Butter with the water and milk solids removed. It tastes like toasted butter, can handle higher heat without burning, and doesn't need refrigeration if kept sealed. It's the main cooking fat in India.

**Where to buy:**
1. **Chedraui** — Mantequilla Clarificada Ghee Tu Tipo 400g. CONFIRMED on chedraui.com.mx.
2. **Walmart** — possible in the gourmet section.

**If you can't find it, MAKE IT YOURSELF:** Melt 300g unsalted butter over low heat. Let it simmer gently for 10-15 minutes. White foam will form on top — skim it off with a spoon. When you see golden solids at the bottom and the butter looks clear and golden, strain through a clean cloth into a jar. Yields ~250g of ghee. Lasts months without refrigeration.

**Simple alternative:** Use regular butter + 1 tbsp vegetable oil to keep it from burning. Not the same but it works.

---

#### 🟢 Canned Coconut Milk (you need 1 × 400ml can)
**What is it?** Thick coconut milk in a can. Do NOT buy the carton kind for drinking (that one's watery and sweetened). The can should say "Leche de Coco" with no sugar. When you open it, it should be thick on top and more liquid at the bottom — shake it well.

**Where to buy:**
1. **Walmart** — Leche de coco Uteki en lata 400ml. CONFIRMED.
2. **Chedraui** — Leche Thai de Coco 400ml. CONFIRMED.
3. Any supermarket in the Asian/international section.

---

#### 🟡 Unsweetened Almond Milk (you only need 2 tbsp)
**What is it?** Almond milk. You only need a very small amount for the Dal Makhani.

**Where to buy:** Walmart, Chedraui. If you don't want to buy a whole carton just for 2 tbsp, substitute with regular milk or a splash of cream. The difference is minimal.

---

#### 🟢 Raw Cashews / Castañas de Cajú (you need 3 tbsp)
**What is it?** The same cashews eaten in Mexico. You need them raw and unsalted.

**Where to buy:** Any supermarket, in bulk or bagged. DAC Market has an excellent nut selection.

---

### OTHER

#### 🟢 Tomato Purée / Passata (you need ~700ml)
**What is it?** Strained, blended tomato with no skin or seeds. Thinner than regular canned tomato purée. In Italy it's called "passata." In Mexico look for "Puré de Tomate" in a glass jar or carton.

**Where to buy:** Walmart, Chedraui, Mega. If you can't find it, buy 1 large can of crushed tomatoes and blend until smooth.

#### 🟡 Asafoetida / Hing (just a pinch, OPTIONAL)
**What is it?** A yellow powder that smells strongly of concentrated garlic and onion. Used in VERY small amounts in Indian cooking. ⚠️ **Many brands cut it with wheat flour — if it doesn't say "sin gluten" or "gluten free," DO NOT BUY IT.**

**Where to buy:** Only at specialty Indian stores or Amazon. For this week, just SKIP IT. The fresh garlic and onion in the recipe provide enough flavour.

## 💡 TIPS FOR AN INEXPERIENCED COOK

### Before you go shopping:
1. **Check what spices you ALREADY HAVE at home** before buying. Many (cumin, cinnamon, clove, nutmeg, paprika) you probably already have.
2. **Bring this list printed or on your phone.**
3. **At DAC Market, ask.** The owners speak English and Spanish, and they're used to customers looking for rare ingredients. Tell them: "I'm cooking Indian food, do you have...?"
4. **Don't stress if something's missing.** Almost all the hard-to-find ingredients have a substitute or can be skipped.

### When cooking:
1. **Measure your spices BEFORE you start cooking.** Set them out in little bowls like they do on cooking shows — that way nothing burns while you're hunting for the next jar.
2. **Spices burn fast.** When the recipe says "cook 30 seconds," it's SERIOUS. If they burn, they taste bitter.
3. **Wash your hands after handling chiles.** And don't touch your eyes.
4. **Turmeric STAINS.** Wear an apron, clean up spills immediately. It permanently dyes plastic, wood, and fingernails yellow.
