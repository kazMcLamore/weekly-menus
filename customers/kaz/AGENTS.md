## Meal Plan Structure

- **Batch cooking cadence:** Weekly rolling batch — cook across Wed–Fri of week N, eat fresh Wed–Sat of cook week, eat freeze-ahead Mon–Tue of week N+1. See "Weekly Cook Schedule — Wed–Fri Rolling Batch" section below.
- **Days covered per batch:** Customer: Mon–Sat (6 days, lunch + dinner = 12 meals). Assistant: Mon–Fri lunch + dinner (10 meals) + Sat lunch only (1 meal) = 11 meals. Off Sunday. **Total strict need = 23 plate-combos.**
- **Dish count per week:** 5 unique dishes total
  - **3 meat dishes** (chicken, beef, pork, fish, etc.)
  - **2 vegetable/plant side dishes** (hummus, potato dishes, vegetable stews, legumes — these pair with the meat dishes, not standalone mains)
  - **Plus:** 1 regional rice/carb dish (e.g., Lebanese vermicelli rice, Mexican arroz rojo, Indian jeera rice, etc.) — enough portions to accompany every meal
- **Meal structure:** Each meal = 1 meat dish + 1 vegetable side + rice. This is a paired format — the veg dishes are sides that accompany the proteins, not standalone meals.
- **Rotation structure:** Fixed daily pairs pre-determined (each day specifies which meat + which veg side + rice). Lunch and dinner on the same day must use different meat dishes. With 3 meat dishes spread across 6 days × 2 meals, each meat dish repeats ~4× through the week. **Saturday asymmetry:** the assistant eats lunch only on Saturday (no dinner) while the customer still has Saturday dinner — pick the Saturday dinner meat so it does not repeat Saturday lunch's meat.
- **Total portions per batch:** ~25 plate-combos (customer 12 meals + assistant 11 meals = strict 23, plus ~2 buffer). The buffer covers the assistant's slightly smaller plates (80–90% of a full portion frees ~1–2 portions), run-out / waste headroom, and 1–2 portions to freeze. **Per-meal calorie target stays ~600–700 kcal — the buffer is NOT for bigger plates.**
- **Portion distribution:** Meat dishes get ~8–9 portions each (3 dishes ≈ 25). Veg sides get ~12–13 portions each (2 dishes ≈ 25). Rice makes ~25–26 portions. Each plate = 1 meat + 1 veg + 1 rice.

## Weekly Cook Schedule — Wed–Fri Rolling Batch

The assistant has other duties and cannot cook every day. Fixed weekly cadence:

- **Tuesday:** Shop. Single trip covering all ingredients for the week. (See weekly supplement for store-by-store route.)
- **Wednesday (~6 hr — heavy day):** Rice (~25 portions, large pot) + slowest / most freezer-friendly meat dish (long braise — beef, pork shoulder) + 1st veg side (stew, dal, beans — anything that benefits from a long simmer).
- **Thursday (~4–5 hr):** 2nd meat dish (medium-cook — chicken in sauce, fish curry) + 2nd veg side (potatoes, calabacitas, sautéed veg).
- **Friday (~4–5 hr):** 3rd meat dish (best-eaten fresh — pan-fish, quick-sauté chicken). **FREEZER ROUTING at end of session.**
- **Sunday:** Off.

Total cook budget ~14–16 hr/week vs ~8–10 hr actual cook + ~2 hr housework (clean-up, container-pack, shopping-unpack). Buffer covers learning curve, mishaps, extra dishes from scaling.

### Eat-week routing (rolling model)

- Food cooked Wed–Sat of week N is eaten **Wed–Sat of week N** (0–3 days in fridge, fresh).
- Monday and Tuesday of week N+1 are eaten from **week N's Friday freezer stock**, thawed overnight (~1 day before).
- Sunday is off — no meal prep needed.
- System is self-sustaining once established. First transition week (no prior freeze-stock) the customer wings Mon–Tue.

### Cook-day ordering rules

When assigning specific dishes to Wed/Thu/Fri each week:

- Cook slowest + most freezer-friendly dishes (long-braised stews) on **Wed** → they become Mon–Tue freeze-ahead for the next week.
- Cook most delicate / best-fresh dishes on **Fri** → go to Saturday eats (freshest, ~1 day in fridge).
- Mid-week dishes (medium cook) on **Thu**.

### Friday freezer routing (critical)

At end of every Friday cook session the assistant divides each dish into:

- **Fridge portions** (~4 per dish, eaten Wed–Sat current week)
- **Freezer portions** (~4–5 per dish, labeled with date + dish, frozen same day → become next week's Mon–Tue eats)

Sunday night: move Monday's meals from freezer to fridge. Monday night: move Tuesday's meals.

### Prep consolidation (saves 1–2 hr/week)

- Chop all onions/garlic/ginger for ALL dishes in one session (Tue evening post-shop or Wed morning).
- Bloom/toast whole spices in one batch if multiple dishes share overlapping spice blends.
- Stock / salsa / sauce bases shared across dishes — make one large batch, divide.
- Rice is cooked once (Wed) and reheated portion-by-portion through the week.

### Per-week output requirement

Each weekly plan must include a per-week cook-day mapping showing:

- Which specific dish goes Wed vs. Thu vs. Fri (with rationale)
- What prep crosses dishes (shared bases, joint chopping)
- The Mon–Tue thaw schedule for that week's freezer stock (which containers to move Sunday night vs Monday night)
- Estimated per-dish cook time to validate it fits the day's hour budget
