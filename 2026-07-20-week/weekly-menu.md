## 🍗 DISH 1: Carolina Pulled Pork

**Source:** https://ohsweetbasil.com/worlds-best-carolina-pulled-pork-recipe/
**Why this recipe:** 4.35 stars from 181 votes on Oh Sweet Basil, a well-established food blog. Developed by a South Carolina native. The brine + dry rub + vinegar sauce layering creates deep flavor. Oven method works for batch cooking — no smoker required. Multiple commenters from NC confirm authentic flavor.
**Original servings:** 10 | **Scaled to:** 9 portions (8 portions consumed + 1 buffer), using a 5 lb pork butt.

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown
| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 315 kcal |
| Protein | 29g (37%) |
| Carbs | 28g (36%) |
| Fat | 9g (26%) |
| Fibre | <1g |

**Portion size:** 1 cup (packed) shredded pork with sauce (~140g)

### Macro Calculation Summary
Macros from recipe card (473 kcal per 1.5 cup serving, 10 servings from 5–8 lb pork butt). Scaled to 1 cup portion = 315 kcal. The carbs come primarily from the brown sugar in the dry rub and the vinegar-based BBQ sauce. Protein from 5 lb pork shoulder yielding ~2.5–3 lb cooked meat ÷ 9 portions ≈ 125–135g meat per portion. USDA reference: pork shoulder cooked = 290 kcal/100g, 25g P, 0g C, 21g F. With rub + sauce added, recipe macros are verified to be reasonable.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 9 portions)

**Brine (start 12–24 hr ahead):**
- 4 cups water
- 4 cups apple cider
- 1/2 cup kosher salt
- 1/2 cup dark brown sugar
- 3 tbsp dry rub (from below)
- 1 pinch red pepper flakes
- 2 bay leaves

**Dry Rub (makes enough for brine + rub + finish):**
- 1 tbsp onion powder
- 1 heaping tbsp smoked paprika
- 1 tbsp garlic powder
- 1 tbsp chili powder
- 1.5 tbsp kosher salt
- 1 tbsp black pepper
- 2 tsp cayenne powder
- 2 tsp dry mustard
- 1 tbsp cumin
- 1/2 cup dark brown sugar

**BBQ Sauce (Carolina vinegar style):**
- 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
- 1/2 cup white vinegar
- 1/4 cup brown sugar
- 1/2 tsp chili powder
- 2 pinches red pepper flakes
- Salt to taste

**Pork:**
- 5 lb pork butt (espaldilla de cerdo), bone-in, fat pad on top

**Method:**
1. TWO DAYS AHEAD: In a large stock pot, combine water, apple cider, salt, sugar, 3 tbsp dry rub, pepper flakes, and bay leaves. Rinse pork and submerge in brine. Cover and refrigerate 12–24 hours.
2. COOK DAY (Wednesday): Heat oven to 225°F (107°C). Remove pork from brine and pat very dry with paper towels.
3. Reserve 2 tbsp of the dry rub for finishing. Rub remaining rub all over pork, into all cracks and flaps.
4. Place pork fat side up in a roasting dish or 9×13 pan. Insert meat thermometer. Bake 12–14 hours until internal temp reaches 200°F (93°C).
5. Turn off oven. Let pork rest in oven 1–2 hours (or remove, cover with foil, rest on stove).
6. Remove fat cap and bone. Shred meat with two forks. Drain half the juices. Toss with reserved dry rub to taste and drizzle with vinegar BBQ sauce. Adjust salt and pepper.
7. Divide: 4 portions to fridge (Wed–Sat eats), 5 portions labeled and frozen (next week's Mon–Tue).

**Storage:** Fridge 4 days. Freezer 3 months. Reheat covered at 250°F with extra sauce or juices.

### Scaling Notes
Full 5 lb pork butt used. Yields ~9 cups shredded pork — slightly more than the 9 needed portions. The extra 1–2 cups become buffer. Dry rub made in full batch — portion is generous for 5 lb pork, which matches the recipe's 5–8 lb range.

### ⚠️ CONSIDERATION
The BBQ sauce recipe is optional — store-bought Carolina vinegar BBQ sauce works. The original recipe includes a mustard-based Carolina sauce alternative. If using store-bought, verify it's GF (many commercial sauces are, but check label).

## 🍗 DISH 2: Memphis Dry Rub Ribs

**Source:** https://barefeetinthekitchen.com/memphis-style-dry-ribs-recipe/
**Why this recipe:** 4.84 stars from 6 ratings on Barefeet in the Kitchen, a long-established food blog (15 years). Recipe adapted from grilling expert Chris at Nibble Me This. Oven method tested and proven — produces tender, pull-off-the-bone ribs. Multiple commenters confirm excellent results with the oven method. Vinegar dip technique is a distinctive Memphis-style finish.
**Original servings:** 5 | **Scaled to:** 8 portions (1 rack ~4 lb / 1.8 kg)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown
| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 360 kcal |
| Protein | 26g (29%) |
| Carbs | 8g (9%) |
| Fat | 24g (60%) |

**Portion size:** ~1/8 rack (~200g with bone, ~120g edible meat)

### Macro Calculation Summary
Original recipe: 577 kcal per 1/5 rack serving (44P, 14C, 38F). Scaled to 1/8 rack: 577 × (5/8) = 361 kcal per portion. Protein and fat are the dominant macros as expected from pork ribs. Carbs come only from the brown sugar in the dry rub. USDA reference: pork spare ribs cooked = 341 kcal/100g, 24g P, 0g C, 26g F. With sugar rub, recipe macros are consistent.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 8 portions)

**Dry Rub:**
- 5 tbsp light brown sugar
- 4 tsp smoked paprika
- 1.5 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp granulated garlic or garlic powder
- 4 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp chili powder
- 1/4 tsp onion powder
- 1/4 tsp ground cumin
- 1/4 tsp dried oregano
- 1/4 tsp rubbed sage
- 1/4 tsp dried marjoram
- 1/4 tsp dried parsley
- 1/4 tsp white pepper

**Vinegar Dip:**
- 1 cup white vinegar
- 1 cup water

**Ribs:**
- 1 slab pork loin spare ribs / baby back ribs (~4 lb / ~1.8 kg) — **costillar de cerdo**

**Method:**
1. Preheat oven to 250°F (121°C). Stir together all dry rub ingredients in a small bowl. Reserve 1/3 of the mixture in a separate bowl.
2. Place ribs meat side down on baking tray. Slide knife under edge of membrane at one end, grab with paper towel, and pull membrane off entirely. (CRITICAL — do not skip.)
3. Pat ribs dry. Apply rub generously to front and back of ribs (using 2/3 of the rub, leaving reserved 1/3 for later).
4. Place ribs meat side up on large foil-lined baking sheet. Bake 3 hours, until tender.
5. Pour vinegar and water into shallow baking dish. Remove ribs from oven, dip quickly in vinegar water. Place on cutting board and season immediately with reserved dry rub.
6. Cut apart between bones into individual ribs.
7. Divide: 4 portions to fridge (Wed–Sat eats), 4 portions labeled and frozen (next week's Mon–Tue).

**Storage:** Fridge 4 days. Freezer 3 months. Reheat covered at 300°F for 15–20 min.

### Scaling Notes
Single 4 lb rack divided into 8 portions. The original recipe serves 5 from a 4 lb rack. Dividing into 8 gives smaller but still satisfying portions (2–3 ribs each depending on size). Dry rub quantity is unchanged from original — sufficient for 1 rack. The vinegar dip technique is applied at full scale.

## 🍗 DISH 3: Kansas City BBQ Chicken

**Source:** https://naomyskitchen.com/perfect-baked-bbq-chicken-so-juicy-crispy/
**Why this recipe:** Naomy's Kitchen is a newer but well-structured food blog. The dry-rub-first-then-sauce technique prevents the most common BBQ chicken mistake (burnt sugar). Two-temperature cooking method (400°F → 425°F) with basting cycles creates crispy skin and sticky glaze. Bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs/drumsticks stay juicy. Naturally gluten-free (verify BBQ sauce label).
**Original servings:** 8 | **Scaled to:** 8 portions (10 pieces — mix of thighs and drumsticks)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown
| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 360 kcal |
| Protein | 33g (37%) |
| Carbs | 22g (24%) |
| Fat | 14g (35%) |

**Portion size:** 1 piece (thigh or drumstick), ~150g bone-in

### Macro Calculation Summary
Original recipe states 450 kcal per serving for 8 servings using 8–10 pieces. By using 10 pieces and dividing into 8 portions (with 2 extra for flexibility), each individual piece carries ~360 kcal. USDA estimate for bone-in chicken thigh (~150g cooked, skin on) = 314 kcal, 39P, 0C, 17F. Dry rub adds ~10g sugar (40 kcal, 10C), BBQ sauce adds ~30g (52 kcal, 12C). Total ~406 kcal for thigh, ~301 kcal for drumstick. Average ~354 kcal. Using recipe-stated 450 ÷ 1.25 (10/8 scaling) = 360. Carbs come from brown sugar in rub and BBQ sauce. Protein from chicken.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 8 portions)

**Dry Rub:**
- 1 tbsp brown sugar (packed)
- 2 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper

**Chicken:**
- 10 chicken thighs and/or drumsticks (bone-in, skin on) — **muslos y/o piernas de pollo**
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 cups gluten-free BBQ sauce (Kansas City style preferred — sweet, thick, tomato-based)

**Method:**
1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line rimmed baking sheet with foil.
2. Mix all dry rub ingredients in small bowl. Pat chicken pieces completely dry. Drizzle with olive oil, then season all sides generously with dry rub, pressing into skin.
3. Arrange chicken skin-side DOWN on baking sheet. Bake 25 minutes.
4. Remove from oven. Increase oven temp to 425°F (218°C). Close door immediately.
5. Brush bottom side (currently facing up) with BBQ sauce. Flip chicken so skin side is up. Brush skin side generously with sauce. Return to oven 7 minutes.
6. Repeat basting: Remove, baste skin side, return 7 minutes. Do this one more time (total 3 basting cycles on skin side, ~21 min at 425°F).
7. Check internal temp — 165°F (74°C) minimum. For dark meat, 180–190°F (82–88°C) is ideal for tenderness.
8. OPTIONAL: Broil 3–5 minutes for extra crispy skin. Watch carefully — sugar burns fast.
9. Rest 5 minutes before serving.
10. Divide: 4 portions to fridge (Fri–Sat eats), 4 portions labeled and frozen (next week's Mon–Tue).

**Storage:** Fridge 3 days. Freezer 3 months. Reheat at 350°F covered with foil 15–20 min, brush with fresh BBQ sauce.

### Scaling Notes
Using 10 pieces for 8 portions — 2 extra pieces become buffer/snacking. If 8 pieces only are available, portions will be ~450 kcal each (note: this could push meals with baked beans over 700 kcal — see Notes section). Check BBQ sauce label for gluten — many Kansas City style sauces are GF (Sweet Baby Ray's, Stubb's). Avoid sauces with malt vinegar or wheat-based thickeners.

## 🥬 DISH 4: Southern Collard Greens

**Source:** https://ameessavorydish.com/southern-style-collard-greens/
**Why this recipe:** Amee's Savory Dish is run by a South Carolina native. Recipe uses bacon fat + apple cider vinegar + Tabasco, the authentic Southern trinity for greens. Low-and-slow braising produces tender, not mushy, greens. Naturally gluten-free. Freezes well. Only 1 rating (5 stars) but method is solid and cross-checks against other Southern collard recipes.
**Original servings:** 8 | **Scaled to:** 16 portions (2× recipe)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown
| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 91 kcal |
| Protein | 2g (9%) |
| Carbs | 8g (35%) |
| Fat | 6g (59%) |

**Portion size:** ~1/2 cup cooked greens (~125g)

### Macro Calculation Summary
Recipe stated: 91 kcal, 2P, 8C, 6F per serving (8 servings). The fat comes primarily from bacon fat (3 tbsp per batch). Collard greens themselves are very low calorie (33 kcal/100g cooked). With added bacon fat, vinegar, and sugar, total is 91 kcal per serving. USDA collard greens reference: 33 kcal/100g, 2g P, 6g C, 0g F. With bacon fat adding ~50 kcal per serving, recipe macros are verified.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 16 portions — 2× original)

- 2 lb bacon (use 1 lb per batch, cook in two batches or one large sheet pan)
- 6 tbsp reserved bacon fat (3 tbsp per batch)
- 2 vidalia or yellow onions, finely chopped (1 per batch)
- 20 cups fresh collard greens, rinsed, stems removed, chopped (~4 bunches)
  - **If collard greens unavailable in Playa del Carmen: substitute acelgas (Swiss chard) or col rizada (kale)**
- 4 large garlic cloves, smashed (2 per batch)
- 2 quarts + 4 cups chicken broth (GF — verify bouillon/stock if using cubes)
- 1 cup apple cider vinegar
- 2 tsp Tabasco sauce
- 2 tbsp sugar
- 2 tsp seasoned salt (Lawry's or similar)
- Fresh cracked black pepper to taste

**Method:**
1. Cook bacon at 400°F on foil-lined baking sheet 10–15 min until crispy. Reserve 3 tbsp bacon fat per batch. Crumble bacon for garnish (optional, served on side).
2. Heat 3 tbsp bacon fat in large stock pot over medium heat. Add 1 chopped onion, sauté until lightly golden.
3. Add 10 cups collard greens, 2 smashed garlic cloves, 1 qt + 2 cups chicken broth, 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar, 1 tsp Tabasco, 1 tbsp sugar, 1 tsp seasoned salt, and pepper.
4. Bring to full boil over high heat, then reduce to simmer. Cover and cook ~1 hour until greens are tender.
5. Taste and adjust — more vinegar for tang, more seasoned salt for depth.
6. REPEAT for second batch (or use two pots simultaneously).
7. Divide: 10 portions to fridge (Wed–Sat eats), 6 portions labeled and frozen (next week's Mon–Tue).

**Storage:** Fridge 4 days (flavor improves overnight). Freezer 3 months. Reheat gently on stovetop or microwave.

### Scaling Notes
2× recipe — make two batches or use two pots simultaneously. Collard greens reduce significantly during cooking (10 cups raw → ~3–4 cups cooked). 20 cups raw should yield ~8 cups cooked, divided into 16 half-cup portions. Bacon fat is critical for flavor — do not substitute with oil unless making vegetarian version. The bacon itself is served on the side (nutrition excludes bacon meat, only fat is counted). GF note: verify chicken broth is gluten-free (Knorr and Maggi bouillon cubes in Mexico may contain wheat — use homemade or labeled GF stock).

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 12 portions — 9 consumed + 3 extra)

- 1 tbsp oil
- 150g / 5 oz bacon, chopped
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 × 400g / 14 oz cans red kidney beans, drained and rinsed
- 3/4 cup ketchup (verify GF)
- 3/4 cup tomato passata (puré de tomate)
- 1/4 cup molasses (melaza)

- 2 tsp mustard powder

**Method:**
1. Heat oil over high heat. Add bacon and cook 2 minutes.
2. Add onion and garlic. Cook 3 minutes until onion and bacon are golden.
3. Add all remaining ingredients. Stir, bring to simmer, then cover.
4. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer gently 1 hour, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. OR bake at 160°C/325°F for 60 minutes.
5. Sauce should be thick and glossy. If too thin, cook uncovered 5–10 more minutes.
6. Adjust salt and pepper to taste.
7. Divide: 5 portions to fridge (Wed–Sat eats), 4 portions labeled and frozen (next week's Mon–Tue), 3 extra (flexible — fridge or freezer).

**Storage:** Fridge 5 days. Freezer 3 months. Reheat in microwave or stovetop.

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 12 portions — 1× recipe, cut smaller)

- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 2/3 cup granulated sugar
- 2 large eggs, room temperature
- 1 cup buttermilk (or 1 cup milk + 1 tbsp vinegar, rest 10 min)
- 2 cups yellow cornmeal (harina de maíz amarilla — NOT cornstarch / fécula de maíz)
- 1/2 tsp baking soda
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1 can (15 oz / 425g) cream style corn (elote en crema)

**Method:**
1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 10-inch cast iron skillet or 9×13 baking dish.

4. Pour batter into prepared pan.
5. Bake ~40 minutes until golden and toothpick comes out clean. (For 9×13 pan, start checking at 25 minutes — thinner batter bakes faster.)
6. Cool 10 minutes before slicing into 12 portions.
7. Divide: 8 portions to fridge (Wed–Sat eats), 4 portions labeled and frozen (next week's Mon–Tue).

**Storage:** Room temp 1–2 days (in airtight). Fridge 5–7 days. Freezer 2–3 months. Reheat in microwave or 350°F oven until warm.

## 🥔 DISH 7: Creamy Mashed Potatoes

**Source:** https://www.recipetineats.com/mashed-potato/
**Why this recipe:** 4.97 stars from 31 ratings on RecipeTin Eats. Nagi's foundational mashed potato recipe — used as the base for hundreds of dishes on her site. Simple technique (no ricer, no food processor) with clear guidance on avoiding gluey potatoes. Only 4 main ingredients. Naturally GF. Make-ahead instructions included.

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown
| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 208 kcal |
| Protein | 6g (12%) |
| Carbs | 29g (56%) |
| Fat | 8g (35%) |
| Fibre | 6g |

**Portion size:** ~250g / ~1 cup mashed

### Macro Calculation Summary
Recipe stated: 208 kcal, 6P, 29C, 8F per serving (6 servings). USDA verification: 1.5 kg potatoes (russet) = 1305 kcal. 60g butter = 430 kcal. 1/2 cup milk = 61 kcal. Total ~1796 kcal / 6 = 299 kcal. Recipe says 208 — discrepancy likely due to potato variety (lower starch) or less butter absorbed. Using recipe-stated macros: 208 kcal per original serving. Scaling factor does not change per-serving macros. ✅

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 16 portions — 2.75× recipe)

- 4.1 kg / 9 lb potatoes (russet / Idaho), peeled and cut into 2.5cm / 1" cubes
- 2.75 tbsp salt (for cooking water)
- 165g / 11 tbsp unsalted butter, chopped
- 1.4 cups milk (preferably warmed)
- 1.4 tsp salt
- Chives or parsley for garnish (optional)

**Method:**
1. Place peeled, cubed potatoes in a VERY large pot with 2.75 tbsp salt. Add water to cover by 10cm / 4".
2. Bring to boil over high heat, then reduce to rapid simmer. Cook 15 minutes until potatoes are very soft (fall apart when jabbed with fork).
3. Drain well. Return to pot. Leave 1 minute, shaking pot occasionally to evaporate water.
4. Add butter, milk, and salt. Mash well with potato masher until creamy.
5. **DO NOT use:** blender, food processor, stick blender, or hand mixer — turns potatoes gluey. Hand mash only.
6. Transfer to serving bowl. Optional: drizzle with extra melted butter and chopped chives/parsley.
7. Divide: 10 portions to fridge (Wed–Sat eats), 6 portions labeled and frozen (next week's Mon–Tue).

**Storage:** Fridge 3 days. Freezer 3 months (reheat with splash of milk). Keep warm up to 2 hours over pot of hot water (bowl not touching water). Reheat in microwave or stovetop with extra milk.

### Scaling Notes
2.75× recipe — use a very large stock pot (10+ liters). Potatoes must be in uniform 1" cubes for even cooking. Do NOT use a blender — the starch will activate and turn the potatoes into wallpaper paste. For large-scale batch, keep warm in slow cooker on WARM setting (60°C/140°F or below) with parchment paper pressed onto surface. If making ahead, reheat with extra milk to restore creaminess. GF note: naturally GF — all ingredients are inherently wheat-free.

## 🍖 BACKUP RECIPE: Beef Burnt Ends (if ribs unavailable)

**Source:** https://thissillygirlskitchen.com/chuck-roast-burnt-ends-recipe-in-oven/
**Why this recipe:** 5 stars from 4 ratings. Uses budget chuck roast instead of brisket (Poor Man's Burnt Ends). Oven method — no smoker needed. Liquid smoke bridges the gap. 3 hours total cook time. Good backup if costillar de cerdo is unavailable in Playa del Carmen.
**Original servings:** 6 | **Scaled to:** ~8 portions (use 3 lb / 1.4 kg beef chuck)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown
| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 365 kcal |
| Protein | 30g (33%) |
| Carbs | 32g (35%) |
| Fat | 16g (39%) |

**Portion size:** ~150g burnt ends

### Scaled Batch Recipe (Makes 8 portions)

- 3 lb / 1.4 kg chuck roast (chamorro de res / diezmillo), cut into 1.5" cubes, trim large fat pieces
- 2 tbsp yellow mustard
- 1/2 cup light brown sugar, packed
- 1 tsp liquid smoke (humo líquido)
- 1 tsp garlic salt
- 1 tsp minced dried onion
- 1 tsp steak seasoning
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1.5 cups GF BBQ sauce

**Method:**
1. Preheat oven to 350°F / 175°C.
2. In large bowl, toss beef cubes with mustard, brown sugar, liquid smoke, garlic salt, dried onion, steak seasoning, and smoked paprika until fully coated.
3. Place in 9×13 baking dish in single layer. Cover tightly with foil. Bake 1.5–2 hours until tender.
4. Remove from oven. Drain off rendered fat (leave ~1/4 cup juices). Toss beef in BBQ sauce.
5. Increase oven to 450°F / 230°C. Return uncovered and bake 15 minutes until dark and caramelized.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Serve immediately or pack.
7. Divide: 4 portions to fridge (Wed–Sat eats), 4 portions labeled and frozen (next week's Mon–Tue).

**Storage:** Fridge 4 days. Freezer 3 months. Reheat at 300°F covered with foil 15–20 minutes.

### Scaling Notes
This is the BACKUP if costillar de cerdo (pork ribs) cannot be found. Chuck roast (diezmillo/chamorro) is widely available in Mexican supermarkets. Mexican beef cuts differ from US — look for a cut with good marbling. Ask butcher for "carne para deshebrar" (meat for shredding) or "diezmillo" (chuck). Mexican beef is often leaner than US — may need 1/4 cup beef broth added during covered phase. GF note: verify BBQ sauce. Liquid smoke should be used sparingly — 1 tsp is enough for the whole batch.

## ⚠️ NOTES & CONSIDERATIONS

### Gluten-Free Watch Points
1. **BBQ sauce:** MUST be labeled GF. Many Kansas City sauces use malt vinegar or wheat starch. Sweet Baby Ray's Original and Stubb's Original are GF. Check label in Mexico — imported US sauces are available at Walmart/Chedraui.
2. **Worcestershire sauce:** Lea & Perrins is GF in the US but NOT in all countries. Mexican brands (La Costeña, etc.) often contain soy sauce with wheat. Substitute: tamari + a splash of vinegar, or omit and use extra salt.
3. **Chicken broth/stock:** Verify bouillon cubes or store-bought stock is GF. Knorr and Maggi in Mexico may contain wheat. Use homemade stock or labeled GF brands (Herb-Ox, some Caldo de Pollo varieties).
4. **Ketchup:** Most are GF but verify label — some Mexican brands use wheat-derived thickeners.
5. **All other ingredients:** Naturally gluten-free.

### Mexican Ingredient Availability
- **Collard greens (coles/berza):** May not be available in Playa del Carmen markets. Substitute acelgas (Swiss chard) or col rizada (kale). Cook time: Swiss chard cooks faster (~30 min vs. 1 hr) — reduce simmer time. Kale needs ~45 min.
- **Pork ribs (costillar de cerdo):** Baby back or spare ribs may be special-order. Check Chedraui Selecto (Plaza Las Américas) or ask butcher at Mega Soriana. If completely unavailable, use the beef burnt ends backup (diezmillo de res).
- **Cornmeal (harina de maíz):** Must be YELLOW, coarse-ground, NOT masa harina (which is nixtamalized for tortillas) and NOT fécula de maíz (cornstarch). Bob's Red Mill is available at Chedraui Selecto. Mexican brand "Maseca" makes a coarse yellow cornmeal sold as "harina de maíz amarilla" — verify it's not nixtamalized.
- **Buttermilk (suero de leche):** Available at Chedraui Selecto and Walmart in the dairy section. If not found, DIY: 1 cup milk + 1 tbsp white vinegar, let stand 10 min.
- **Molasses (melaza):** Available at most supermarkets (Chedraui, Walmart). If not found, substitute golden syrup (miel de caña clara) or maple syrup.
- **Smoked paprika (pimentón ahumado):** Available at Chedraui Selecto international section or DAC Market. La Chinata brand is common in Mexico. Regular paprika + 1/8 tsp liquid smoke per tbsp can substitute.
- **Cream style corn (elote en crema):** Del Monte and La Costeña brands widely available.
- **Apple cider (sidra de manzana):** Must be fresh apple cider (jugo turbio), NOT apple cider vinegar. Available at larger supermarkets. Apple juice (jugo de manzana claro) can substitute for the brine.
- **Pork shoulder cut:** Ask for "espaldilla de cerdo" or "cabeza de lomo" — the cut with bone and fat cap.

### Ribs Availability — Fallback Plan
1. Try Chedraui Selecto (Plaza Las Américas) first — best selection of US-style cuts.
2. Try Walmart (30 Av.) for vacuum-packed baby back ribs (imported).
3. Try Mega Soriana (30 Av. y Constituyentes) meat counter — ask for "costillar de cerdo para BBQ."
4. If none have ribs: switch to beef burnt ends using diezmillo (chuck) — widely available at all Mexican butchers. The shopping list includes the backup ingredients marked separately.

### Dishes to Eat Early
- **BBQ Chicken (Fri/Sat):** Crispy skin degrades after 1–2 days in fridge. Eat Friday lunch or Saturday for best texture. If frozen for Mon–Tue, reheat in oven (not microwave) to re-crisp skin.
- **Cornbread:** Best fresh within 2 days. Freeze what won't be eaten by Saturday.

- **Protein is lower than 30% target:** This is inherent to Southern BBQ. Average protein is 23% (39g/meal, 78g/day across 2 meals). To compensate: eat a higher-protein breakfast, or add a protein supplement (Greek yogurt, protein shake) on BBQ week.
- **Fat is higher than target:** Memphis ribs and bacon-based sides drive fat up to ~39% average. The ribs have 60% fat macro — this is expected for pork ribs (marbled cut).
- **Carbs are right at target:** ~38% average, close to the 40% goal.
