## 🍗 DISH 1: Butter Chicken (Murgh Makhani)

**Source:** https://www.recipetineats.com/butter-chicken/
**Why this recipe:** Nagi (RecipeTin Eats) is one of the most trusted recipe developers online. This specific recipe has 4.96 stars from 1,093 reviews — an extraordinary level of validation. It's a chef recipe (adapted from Australian chef Luke Mangan), uses supermarket-available ingredients, and is naturally gluten-free. The yogurt marinade + cream sauce is exactly the saucy, bold profile the customer wants.

**Original servings:** 3–4 | **Scaled to:** 3 portions

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (customer)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 635 kcal |
| Protein | 42g (26%) |
| Carbs | 55g (35%) |
| Fat | 27g (38%) |
| Fibre | 2g |

*Includes 1 cup (150g cooked) basmati rice. Assistant portions are approximately 80–90% of customer portion — plated at cook's discretion.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
Chicken thighs (~250g cooked per portion): 520 cal / 60g P / 0g C / 28g F. Sauce (cream, passata, butter, yogurt): ~115 cal / 2g P / 7g C / 10g F. Rice (150g cooked): 200 cal / 4g P / 45g C / 0.5g F. Total ≈ 635 cal with rice. (Sauce calories slightly lower per portion vs. original since chicken is the dominant calorie driver.)

### Batch Recipe (Makes 3 portions, cooked at ~1× original)

**Ingredients:**
- 750g boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-size pieces
- 1/2 cup plain full-fat yogurt
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- 1 tsp turmeric powder
- 2 tsp garam masala
- 1/2 tsp chilli powder (pure, or cayenne)
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
- 2 cloves garlic, crushed
- 2 tbsp ghee or butter
- 1 cup tomato passata (pureed tomatoes)
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 1 tbsp sugar
- 1 1/4 tsp salt
- Fresh coriander/cilantro for garnish

**Method:**
1. Combine yogurt, lemon juice, turmeric, garam masala, chilli powder, cumin, ginger, and garlic in a bowl. Add chicken, coat well. Cover and marinate overnight (minimum 3 hours).
2. Heat ghee in a large skillet over high heat. Remove chicken from marinade (don't shake off excess) and cook ~3 minutes until white all over. Work in batches if needed — don't crowd the pan.
3. Add passata, cream, sugar, salt, and any remaining marinade from the bowl. Stir well.
4. Reduce heat to low and simmer 20 minutes until sauce thickens and deepens in colour.
5. Garnish with coriander. Serve over basmati rice.

**Storage:** Keeps 4–5 days refrigerated. Freezes well up to 3 months (sauce may separate slightly on thaw — stir while reheating). Reheat gently on stovetop or microwave.

### Scaling Notes
- Cooked at original scale (3-4 serving recipe makes 3 generous portions). No scaling adjustments needed — this is the recipe as written.

## 🍗 DISH 2: Lamb Rogan Josh

**Source:** https://www.recipetineats.com/rogan-josh/
**Why this recipe:** Another RecipeTin Eats powerhouse — 4.97 stars from 253 ratings. Based on Rick Stein's India recipe with Nagi's refinements for more curry-house depth. All spices are supermarket-available (no Indian grocer required). Naturally GF. The 2-hour slow simmer produces fall-apart lamb in a rich, creamy tomato sauce — ideal batch cooking. Gets BETTER on day 2-3, making it the perfect choice for the 4-portion dish that stretches into Saturday.

**Original servings:** 4 | **Scaled to:** 4 portions (as-is)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (customer)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 670 kcal |
| Protein | 35g (21%) |
| Carbs | 63g (38%) |
| Fat | 28g (38%) |
| Fibre | 5g |

*Includes 1 cup basmati rice. Protein is moderate — this dish leans into the rich sauce and lamb fat for satiety.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
Lamb shoulder (~190g cooked per portion, from ~375g raw for 4 servings at 750g total): 360 cal / 31g P / 0g C / 26g F. Sauce (ghee, passata, yogurt, spices): ~110 cal / 2g P / 18g C / 5g F. Rice (150g cooked): 200 cal / 4g P / 45g C / 0.5g F.

### Batch Recipe (Makes 4 portions, original scale)

**Ingredients:**
- 3 1/2 tbsp ghee (or butter)
- 2 cinnamon sticks
- 6 green cardamom pods, lightly bruised
- 4 cloves
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
- 5 tbsp tomato passata
- 1 tsp salt
- 750g boneless lamb shoulder, cut into 3cm cubes
- 1 1/2 cups chicken stock (reduced salt)

**Spices:**
- 2 tbsp paprika
- 1/2 tsp chilli powder (or to taste)
- 4 tsp ground coriander
- 4 tsp ground cumin
- 2 tsp turmeric
- 1/4 tsp nutmeg
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 1/2 tsp fennel powder

**Finishes:**
- 1/2 cup plain yogurt
- 1/2 tsp extra garam masala
- 1/2 tsp extra fennel powder

**Method:**
1. Melt ghee in a heavy-based pot over medium heat. Add cinnamon, cardamom, cloves — cook 1 minute.
2. Add onion, cook 7 minutes until golden. Add garlic and ginger, cook 1 minute.
3. Stir in all ground spices, toast 30 seconds.
4. Add tomato passata and salt, then stock. Stir, add lamb. Bring to a simmer.
5. Cover, reduce heat to low-medium, simmer gently 1 hour 45 minutes. Stir occasionally.
6. Remove lid, simmer 15 minutes more to reduce sauce. Lamb should be fork-tender.
7. Stir in yogurt, extra garam masala, and extra fennel. Cook 2 more minutes.
8. Serve over basmati rice, garnish with coriander.

**Storage:** Keeps 5 days refrigerated, even better on day 2–3. Freezes 3 months. Reheat stovetop or microwave. ⚠️ The Saturday portion for the assistant — keep refrigerated, it holds fine to day 6.

### Scaling Notes
- Used at original (4-serving) scale — no adjustments. This is the 4-portion dish by design since lamb holds excellently across the full week including Saturday.

## 🍗 DISH 3: Goan Fish Curry

**Source:** https://www.carolinescooking.com/goan-fish-curry/
**Why this recipe:** 4.85 stars from 32 ratings with consistent rave reviews. Caroline's Cooking is a well-established food blog. This recipe makes its own masala from whole toasted spices — no pre-made curry powder — giving authentic Goan depth. Coconut-tamarind base is naturally GF, tangy, and creamy. Quick: 20 minutes total, good for the fresher dish in the rotation.

**Original servings:** 2 | **Scaled to:** 3 portions (1.5×)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (customer)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 655 kcal |
| Protein | 43g (26%) |
| Carbs | 56g (34%) |
| Fat | 33g (45%) |
| Fibre | 4g |

*Includes 3/4 cup (115g cooked) basmati rice. Fish provides lean protein; coconut milk drives the fat content.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
White fish (~225g per portion): 190 cal / 42g P / 0g C / 2g F. Coconut milk (~1/2 cup per portion): 220 cal / 2g P / 4g C / 24g F. Oil, onion, tomato, tamarind, spices: ~95 cal / 1g P / 10g C / 4g F. Rice (115g cooked): 150 cal / 3g P / 34g C / 0.4g F. Total ≈ 655 cal.

### Batch Recipe (Makes 3 portions, 1.5× scale)

**Ingredients:**
**Spice paste (masala):**
- 3 tsp coriander seeds
- 1 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
- 3/4 tsp mustard seeds
- 3–4 cloves garlic
- 1 1/2 tbsp fresh ginger, chopped
- 1/3 tsp salt
- 3/4 tsp cayenne pepper
- 3/4 tsp paprika
- 3/4 tsp turmeric

**Curry:**
- 1 1/2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 3 green chilis (serrano or similar), finely diced
- 1 1/2 onions, finely diced
- 1 1/2 tomatoes (or 2 small), diced
- 675g firm white fish (huachinango/red snapper, mero/grouper, or cod), cut into large chunks
- 3 tsp tamarind concentrate
- 1 1/2 cups coconut milk (1 × 400ml can)

**Method:**
1. Toast coriander, cumin, and mustard seeds in a dry skillet over medium heat ~2 minutes until fragrant.
2. Crush garlic and ginger with salt to a paste. Add toasted seeds, crush. Mix in cayenne, paprika, turmeric.
3. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Sauté onion 3–4 minutes until softened.
4. Add spice paste, cook 1–2 minutes. Add tomato and chilis, cook until liquid evaporates (~1 minute).
5. Add tamarind and coconut milk, stir to combine.
6. Add fish chunks, cook 3–4 minutes until just cooked through.
7. Serve over basmati rice.

**Storage:** ⚠️ Fish doesn't hold as long. Eat within 2–3 days. The Wednesday assistant portion and Friday customer portions should be frozen right after cooking. Alternatively, freeze the sauce base (steps 1-5) separately and cook the fish fresh mid-week.

### Scaling Notes
- Scaled at 1.5× from original (2 → 3 portions). Spices scaled at 1.4× (slightly under linear) since masala pastes can intensify. Coconut milk rounded up to 1 full can — practical and slightly saucier.
- ⚠️ **Fish freshness:** Portions are spread across Tue (customer dinner), Wed (assistant lunch), Fri (customer lunch). The Tue and Wed portions are fine. The Fri portion must be frozen and thawed Thursday night.

## 🥬 DISH 4: Chana Masala (Chickpea Curry)

**Source:** https://ministryofcurry.com/chana-masala/
**Why this recipe:** Ministry of Curry is run by Archana Mundhe, an Indian home cook teaching authentic techniques. This recipe has 4.58 stars from 26 ratings with strong user feedback. Naturally GF and vegan-friendly. Chickpeas hold up excellently for batch cooking and actually improve as flavours meld.

**Original servings:** 4 | **Scaled to:** 3 portions (0.75×)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (customer, with yogurt)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 650 kcal |
| Protein | 28g (17%) |
| Carbs | 100g (62%) |
| Fat | 16g (22%) |
| Fibre | 12g |

*Includes 1.5 cups basmati rice and 170g Greek yogurt. Without the side: 530 cal / 15g P / 92g C / 12g F. The yogurt or 2 boiled eggs are essential for protein balance.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
Chickpeas (~1 cup cooked per portion): 269 cal / 15g P / 45g C / 4g F. Onion, tomato, ghee, spices: ~60 cal / 1.5g P / 8g C / 4g F. Rice (225g cooked, 1.5 cups): 300 cal / 6g P / 68g C / 0.7g F. Greek yogurt (170g): 100 cal / 17g P / 6g C / 0.7g F.

### Batch Recipe (Makes 3 portions)

**Ingredients:**
- 1 1/2 (15 oz / 425g) cans chickpeas, drained and rinsed *(or 3/4 cup dry, soaked overnight and pressure cooked)*
- 1 1/2 tbsp ghee (or oil for vegan)
- 1 bay leaf
- 3/4 large yellow onion, finely diced
- 1 1/2 tsp ginger paste
- 1 1/2 tsp garlic paste
- 1 green chili, slit (optional)
- 1 1/2 plum tomatoes, finely diced
- 2 tsp chana masala spice blend *(or: 3/4 tsp garam masala + 3/4 tsp coriander powder + 1/2 tsp red chili powder + pinch turmeric + squeeze of lemon at the end)*
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/4 tsp turmeric
- 1/2 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder (or paprika)
- 3/4 cup water
- 3/4 tbsp kasoori methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
- 3 tbsp chopped cilantro

### Side (per portion, do not skip):
- 170g Greek yogurt OR 2 boiled eggs

**Method:**
1. Heat ghee in a skillet over medium heat. Add bay leaf and onion, sauté 8–10 minutes until golden.
2. Add ginger paste, garlic paste, green chili. Cook 1 minute.
3. Add tomato, cook 5 minutes until soft and mushy.
4. Stir in spice blend, salt, turmeric, chili powder. Cook 30 seconds.
5. Add drained chickpeas and water. Simmer 5–10 minutes.
6. Crush kasoori methi between palms, stir in. Garnish with cilantro.
7. Serve with rice and Greek yogurt or boiled eggs on the side.

**Storage:** Keeps 5 days refrigerated excellently. Freezes 2–3 months. Reheat stovetop or microwave.

### Scaling Notes
- Scaled at 0.75× from original (4 → 3 portions). Chickpeas: 2 cans would be too many, 1 can too few — 1.5 cans is the sweet spot (or 3/4 cup dry).
- Kasoori methi kept generous at 3/4 tbsp — it's a finishing herb, hard to overdo.
- ⚠️ **PROTEIN NOTE:** This dish is low in protein (~15g without sides). The yogurt or egg side is NOT optional.

## 🥬 DISH 5: Dal Makhani (Creamy Black Lentils & Kidney Beans)

**Source:** https://www.veganricha.com/dal-makhani-beans-in-creamy-buttery/
**Why this recipe:** Vegan Richa is a widely respected Indian food blogger with multiple published cookbooks. This recipe has 5 stars from 21 ratings and comes from her mother's home kitchen. Naturally GF, uses almond + cashew milk for creaminess. Slow-simmered lentils are ideal batch cooking — gets creamier each day.

**Original servings:** 4 | **Scaled to:** 3 portions (0.75×)

### Per-Portion Macro Breakdown (customer, with egg)

| Nutrient | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Calories | 665 kcal |
| Protein | 35g (21%) |
| Carbs | 82g (49%) |
| Fat | 27g (37%) |
| Fibre | 16g |

*Includes 1 cup basmati rice, 1 tbsp butter/ghee, and 1 boiled egg. Without egg: ~595 cal / 29g P — still decent for a vegetarian dish.*

### Macro Calculation Summary
Black gram + kidney beans (~1 cup cooked per portion): 210 cal / 14g P / 37g C / 1g F. Almond/cashew milk, oil, onion, tomato: ~100 cal / 3g P / 6g C / 8g F. Butter (1 tbsp): 100 cal / 0g P / 0g C / 12g F. Rice (150g): 200 cal / 4g P / 45g C / 0.5g F. Boiled egg: 70 cal / 6g P / 0.5g C / 5g F. Total ≈ 680 cal with egg.

### Batch Recipe (Makes 3 portions)

**Ingredients:**
- 3/4 cup whole urad dal (black gram) *— or substitute black beluga lentils*
- 6 tbsp dried kidney beans (rajma)
- 3 cups water (for pressure cooking)
- 3/4 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp red chili powder or cayenne

**Tempering:**
- 1 1/2 tsp oil
- 3/4 tsp cumin seeds
- 1–2 green chilis (serrano), finely chopped
- 3/4 inch ginger, finely chopped
- 4–5 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 3/4 medium onion, chopped
- Pinch asafoetida (hing) — *⚠️ omit if not GF-certified*
- 1/3 tsp turmeric
- 1 1/2 medium tomatoes, chopped
- 3/4 tsp garam masala
- 3/4 tbsp dried fenugreek leaves (kasoori methi)
- 1 1/2 tbsp almond milk (or dairy cream)
- 3–4 tbsp cashew cream (blend 3 tbsp cashews + 4 tbsp water)
- Butter or ghee for serving

**Method:**
1. Soak urad dal and kidney beans overnight (8+ hours). Drain.
2. Pressure cook with 3 cups water, salt, and chili powder: 25 minutes at high pressure with natural release. *Or stovetop: simmer covered 60–70 minutes until very soft.*
3. In a pan, heat oil. Add cumin seeds, cook until fragrant.
4. Add ginger, garlic, green chili — sauté until golden.
5. Add onion, asafoetida — cook until translucent.
6. Add tomato, turmeric — cook until tomatoes are mushy (~5 min).
7. Add cooked dal/beans with their cooking water. Add garam masala, fenugreek, almond milk, cashew cream.
8. Simmer 30 minutes on low, mashing some beans against the pot. Add water if too thick.
9. Serve with a dollop of butter/ghee, rice, and a boiled egg on the side.

**Storage:** Keeps 5 days refrigerated — gets creamier over time. Freezes excellently up to 3 months.

### Scaling Notes
- Scaled at 0.75× from original (4 → 3). Legumes and aromatics scale linearly.
- ⚠️ **URAD DAL AVAILABILITY:** Whole urad dal (black gram) may be hard to find in Playa del Carmen. Use black beluga lentils (lentejas negras) or substitute 1 cup brown lentils + 3 tbsp kidney beans. Slightly different flavour but still excellent.

## ⚠️ NOTES & CONSIDERATIONS

### GF Watch Points
- **Asafoetida (hing):** Many commercial hing powders are cut with wheat flour. Skip it entirely, or confirm a GF brand. The dal makhani works fine without it.
- **Garam masala:** Check your brand — some cheap blends add flour as filler. Most quality brands are GF.
- **Kasoori methi:** Pure dried fenugreek leaf, naturally GF.
- **Yogurt:** Plain yogurt is GF, but flavoured ones may have additives. Stick to plain full-fat Greek.

### Ingredient Availability in Playa del Carmen
- **Urad dal:** May require a trip to an Indian grocer or the international aisle at Walmart/Chedraui. Substitute black beluga lentils (lentejas negras) if unavailable — they're smaller and cook faster but give a similar earthy flavour.
- **Kasoori methi:** Hard to find locally. Can be omitted — the dal and chana masala will still be excellent. Epazote is NOT a substitute.
- **Tamarind concentrate:** Should be available at Chedraui or Walmart in the Asian/International section. If not, substitute 1 tbsp lime juice + 1/2 tsp brown sugar per tsp of tamarind.
- **Fish:** Huachinango (red snapper) or mero (grouper) are excellent local substitutes — both are firm white fish common in the Yucatán.
- **Passata:** Look for "puré de tomate" or puree canned tomatoes yourself in a blender.

### Freshness Schedule
- **Eat early (Mon–Wed):** Goan Fish Curry. Fish doesn't hold as long. The Wednesday (assistant) and Friday (customer) portions should be FROZEN right after cooking and thawed the night before.
- **Peak on day 2–3:** Lamb Rogan Josh and Dal Makhani — both improve as flavours meld.
- **Steady all week:** Butter Chicken, Chana Masala — both hold well for 5 days.
- **Saturday (assistant only):** Lamb Rogan Josh holds fine to day 6 refrigerated. If concerned, freeze that portion and thaw Friday night.

### Protein Boosts
- The vegetarian dishes (Chana Masala, Dal Makhani) are lower in protein. The yogurt/egg sides are built into the plan — don't skip them.
- Customer: if still hungry, increase rice by 50% (+100 cal) or add a second egg.

### Assistant Portions
- The assistant plates his own lunch from the batch. Exact portion size is at his discretion — ~80–90% of the customer's portion is a reasonable guide.
